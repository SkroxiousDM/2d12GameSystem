## Cultures Within the Empire

Rome was above all a Mediterranean empire that pulled together a vast diversity of cultures under one administrative system. At its height, roughly one in four people on Earth lived under Roman control. [kids.nationalgeographic](https://kids.nationalgeographic.com/history/article/ancient-rome)

**Italian Peninsula & Core**
- Latin Romans (the founding culture)
- Etruscans (absorbed early in Rome's expansion)
- Samnites and Italic tribes (Osci, Umbrians, Volsci, Sabines)
- Magna Graecia Greeks (southern Italy and Sicily)

**Western Europe**
- Gauls / Celts (modern France — Gallia Narbonensis, Belgica, Lugdunensis, Aquitania)
- Britons / Celts (modern England and Wales — Britannia)
- Iberians and Celtiberians (modern Spain and Portugal — Hispania Baetica, Tarraconensis, Lusitania)
- Basques (peripheral, never fully conquered)
- Ligurians (northwest Italy/southern France)

**Central & Eastern Europe**
- Dacians (modern Romania — eventually conquered by Trajan)
- Illyrians (modern Albania, Croatia, Bosnia — Illyricum)
- Pannonians (modern Hungary/Slovenia)
- Thracians (modern Bulgaria/northern Greece)
- Macedonians (absorbed after the Greek Wars)

**North Africa**
- Carthaginians / Phoenician descendants (modern Tunisia — Africa Proconsularis)
- Berbers / Numidians (modern Algeria — Numidia, Mauretania Caesariensis)
- Mauretanians (northern Morocco — Mauretania Tingitana)
- Egyptians (ancient Egyptian culture persisted strongly; Egyptian cults like Isis spread across the empire)

**The Levant & Near East**
- Phoenicians (modern Lebanon — part of Roman Syria)
- Syrians (kept as the province of Syria)
- Jews / Judeans (Judaea — a major source of cultural and religious tension)
- Nabataeans / Arabs (modern Jordan — Arabia Petraea)
- Anatolian peoples: Phrygians, Lydians, Bithynians, Galatians, Cappadocians, Pontians (modern Turkey)
- Armenians (client kingdom, buffer state between Rome and Persia, partially integrated)
- Palmyrenes (Syria — briefly an independent breakaway empire)

**Greece & the Aegean**
- Greeks (Athens, Corinth, Sparta — deeply influential on Roman culture)
- Cretans
- Cypriots

## Peripheral / Contact Cultures

These peoples were never fully conquered but had significant contact, trade, conflict, or cultural exchange with Rome.

**Germanic Peoples** (never conquered beyond the Rhine)
- Franks, Alamanni, Goths (Visigoths & Ostrogoths), Vandals, Saxons, Lombards, Burgundians, Marcomanni, Quadi

**Eastern Rivals & Neighbors**
- Parthians / Sassanid Persians (Rome's great eastern rival for centuries)
- Scythians and Sarmatians (steppe peoples north of the Black Sea)
- Kushan Empire (Central Asia — trade connections via the Silk Road)

**Northern Frontier**
- Picts / Caledonians (unconquered Scotland, beyond Hadrian's Wall)
- Irish (Hibernia — never invaded)
- Scandinavian peoples (distant trade contact)

**African Frontier**
- Garamantes (Saharan people, modern Libya — raided and traded)
- Axumites / Kingdom of Aksum (modern Ethiopia — major trade partners via the Red Sea)
- Sub-Saharan African peoples (indirect contact through trade networks)

**Arabian Peninsula**
- Various Arab tribes (some allied, some independent)
- Himyarites and Sabaeans (modern Yemen — trade networks)

## Cultural Influences Flowing *Into* Rome

Rome was notably syncretic — it absorbed rather than erased. Key cultural contributions included: 

- **Greek**: philosophy, art, architecture, language (the eastern empire remained Greek-speaking)
- **Egyptian**: mystery cults (Isis), embalming practices, artistic motifs
- **Persian**: the cult of Mithras, which spread widely through Roman legions
- **Jewish/Christian**: Christianity, which became the state religion by the 4th century
- **Celtic**: local gods (Coventina, Cocidius, Belatucadrus) syncretized with Roman deities
- **Germanic**: military practices, eventually large numbers of Germanic soldiers in Roman armies
