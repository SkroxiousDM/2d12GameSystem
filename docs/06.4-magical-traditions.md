# Chapter 6.3 — Magical Traditions

Magic in 500 AD is not a secret. It is a vocabulary, ancient and disputatious, spoken by a hundred schools with a hundred names for the same door. The Western Empire has guttered out; the old gods scramble for congregants; and in the wreckage, men and women who have learned the names of things find that those names are more powerful than they have ever been. The veil between the visible and invisible worlds is thin in these years — thinner than it was under Augustus, thinner than it will be under Charlemagne. Now is the hour of magi.

Every magical tradition presented in this chapter operates on the same fundamental principle: **magic is the imposition of a name on reality**. To speak a thing's true name in its true tongue is to compel it. The traditions differ not in kind but in method — in what names they have inherited, what powers they have bargained with, and what price they are willing to pay.

---

## How Traditions Work

A character learns spells from one or more traditions. The **tradition** defines the cultural and cosmological framework; the **spell** is the practical working. A character cannot simply pick up a foreign tradition's spell — the grammar of one tradition's names is not the grammar of another's. Learning a second tradition requires access to a teacher, a grimoire, or a supernatural patron willing to share.

### Caster Types

Three kinds of practitioners access magical traditions:

- **Traditional** casters have been initiated into a living lineage — they trained under a master, passed through rites, carry the marks of the tradition. Power derives from **Insight (INS)**. The tradition's grammar is part of their bones.
- **Research** casters have achieved their understanding through study of texts, papyri, and inscriptions. Power derives from **Reason (REA)**. They may lack the living spark of initiation but compensate with systematic rigor.
- **Dabblers** work by force of personal will rather than system. Power derives from **Willpower (WIL)**. They understand just enough to be dangerous — usually to themselves.

### Power Rating

Power represents a character's reserves for casting spells and performing rituals. It determines how many d3 are rolled when a spell's damage or healing is resolved. Power is calculated as follows:

- **Traditional:** Power = 2 + INS + Traits
- **Research:** Power = 2 + REA + Traits
- **Dabbling:** Power = 1 + WIL + Traits (can increase Base to 3 whenever they roll a magic test, in exchange for generating 1 Doom)

Some Talents and other abilities may increase a spellcaster's Power rating under specific circumstances. Spell effects expressed as "power + N" use this value.

### Focuses

Each tradition requires a **Focus** — a physical object that concentrates the tradition's symbolic grammar. A spell cast without a Focus suffers a +1 Difficulty penalty. A Focus that is lost, destroyed, or desecrated renders all bound spells of that tradition immediately inactive until a new Focus is prepared (typically one week of ritual effort).

## Theurgic Tradition

*That which is named in the language of the spheres is heard by the spheres.* The Theurgic tradition is the primary working magic of the Concord of the Seven Seals — the most ancient and most cautious of the hidden world's governing bodies. Its roots descend from Iamblichus of Chalcis and Porphyry of Tyre, through Proclus in Constantinople, through a thousand initiates who learned from masters who learned from masters who learned, it is said, from Plato himself sitting at the knees of Egyptian priests. The theurgist does not compel the gods; the theurgist *ascends* toward the divine principle, climbing the seven planetary spheres — Moon, Mercury, Venus, Sun, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn — until the soul stands at the threshold of the One and the universe *agrees* to cooperate.

It is the slowest of the traditions. It is also, under most circumstances, the safest. The Concord's Seals exist precisely because theurgists learned, two generations before the current crisis, that certain names should never be spoken aloud by anyone moving faster than ceremony.

**Practitioners:** Called *theurgists* or *magisters*. Found primarily in Constantinople, Alexandria, Rome, and Antioch. The Concord of the Seven Seals counts most senior theurgists as members or affiliates.

**Caster Type:** Traditional only. The theurgic lineage cannot be absorbed through texts — it requires a living initiatory chain.

**Focus:** A polished bronze or silver disk engraved with the seven planetary sphere-names in their divine order (a *lamella*). Most magisters have worn theirs since initiation.

---

### **Anagogē** *(Ascent of the Soul)*

*The ancient masters taught that the soul, in its pure state, carries the image of all seven spheres like a mirror carries light. The theurgist holds that image before the divine, and the divine reaches down to steady the hand.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 3 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** rounds equal to power
* **Category:** Blessing spell

**Spell Effect:** The theurgist stills the soul and opens a channel to the higher spheres. For the duration, they may spend 1 Fortune to automatically succeed (not critically succeed) on any one skill test — the universe, briefly, inclines toward right order. This may be used once per round.

**Flawed:** The connection slips; the channel becomes noise rather than signal. The theurgist suffers Drain and cannot benefit from Fortune on skill tests until the end of the scene.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the Fortune cost to trigger the automatic success is reduced to 0 once per scene.
* For 3 Success Levels, allies within Close range may also benefit, though each use still costs Fortune.

---

### **Exorkismos Daimōnōn** *(Binding of Daimones)*

*Every daimon has a sphere-name and a number and a seal. To name these three in sequence is to remind the creature of what it is — not a terror, but a function — and functions, when named correctly, obey.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 4 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Banishment spell

**Spell Effect:** The theurgist recites the sphere-name, numeral, and seal-name of a supernatural target within Close range. The target suffers power + 2 mental Stress with the Stun effect. If the target is reduced to 0 Resolve by this spell, it is immediately cast back to its point of origin — banished, not destroyed. Against creatures native to a sphere above the Moon, difficulty increases by one step.

**Flawed:** The wrong name is spoken. The daimon hears its name mangled and takes offense. Generate 2 Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* For 1 Success Level, the range extends to Medium.
* For 2 Success Levels, the Stun effect is upgraded to Drain.
* For 3 Success Levels, the banishment also applies to a second supernatural target within range.

---

### **Phōs Hyperkosmikon** *(Light Beyond the World)*

*There is a fire that is not fire — that burns without consuming, that the old masters called the light of the Demiurge. Even a fraction of it, imperfectly channeled through mortal flesh, is sufficient to blind those things that have never known warmth.*

* **Skill:** Resilience
* **Difficulty:** Hard
* **Cost:** 4 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** A column of pale, sourceless radiance descends through the theurgist and expands outward. All enemies in Close range suffer power + 1 physical Stress with the Intense effect. Supernatural creatures (daimones, Nephilim, undead, creatures from beyond the spheres) additionally suffer Vicious.

**Flawed:** The light fails to find the caster's soul as a conduit and inverts. The theurgist suffers power physical Stress.

**Success Levels:**
* For 1 Success Level, the range extends to Medium.
* For 2 Success Levels, blinded creatures (those reduced to 0 Wounds) are also treated as Banished if supernatural.

---

### **Telestikē** *(Animation of the Divine Image)*

*The old ritual technique of animating cult statues is not metaphor. A properly consecrated statue is a lamella large enough to stand upright, and the planetary names inscribed in its base call down a fraction of the sphere-intelligence to inhabit it.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Hard
* **Cost:** 5 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** 24 hours
* **Category:** Ward spell

**Spell Effect:** The theurgist consecrates a stone or bronze statue, idol, or significant carved object within Touch range. For the duration, the object radiates the influence of the highest sphere the theurgist can currently invoke: all allies within Close range gain +1 to all social skill tests and +1 Morale resistance. Additionally, any supernatural creature attempting to enter the warded area must succeed on a Willpower + Resilience test (Difficulty: Challenging) or be repelled.

**Flawed:** The wrong sphere is invoked and the statue becomes a locus of low daimonic attention. Generate 2 Doom and the statue must be re-consecrated before this spell can be cast again.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the Morale resistance bonus increases to power.
* For 2 Success Levels, the ward area extends to Medium range.

---

### **Manteia Sphairōn** *(Oracle of the Spheres)*

*The spheres do not speak in words. They speak in ratios, in angles, in the particular silence between one note and the next in the music of the heavens. The trained soul, ascending even briefly above the Lunar sphere, hears this.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Hard
* **Cost:** 3 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Divination spell

**Spell Effect:** The theurgist withdraws into contemplative trance for ten minutes and directs their awareness toward the spheres. They may ask the GM two yes/no questions about any person, place, or event within the physical world — the spheres answer through impressions, symbols, and the movement of light on their lamella. The GM answers truthfully but in sphere-metaphor: "the Moon darkens" means concealment or deception; "Jupiter rises unchallenged" means dominance or success; etc.

**Flawed:** The trance goes too deep. The theurgist suffers 1d3 mental Stress and surfaces with a false impression substituted for one true answer (GM chooses which).

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, one question may be open (non-yes/no), answered in a direct sentence rather than sphere-metaphor.
* For 3 Success Levels, the casting time is reduced to one minute.

---

### **Kleis Heptasphragis** *(Key of the Seven Seals)*

*It is said that the Concord's Seven Seals are not merely organizational tradition — that each seal, when spoken with proper understanding, closes one of the seven doors through which the outer things press. The Eighth Door, the one beyond Saturn, remains unsealed. The masters do not discuss this.*

* **Skill:** Resilience
* **Difficulty:** Very Hard
* **Cost:** 6 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** until end of scene
* **Category:** Ward spell

**Spell Effect:** The theurgist speaks all seven sphere-names in ascending order, concluding with the seal-cipher of the Concord. A ward is established that encompasses everything within Close range. For the duration: all supernatural creatures entering the ward must overcome Hard opposed Willpower + Resilience or be immediately Banished. The caster and all allies within the ward gain Cover resistance equal to power against all supernatural attacks. Any Doom spent by the GM while the ward is active costs the GM an additional 1 Doom.

**Flawed:** The seventh seal is spoken incorrectly. The half-opened ward acts as a beacon. Generate 3 Doom and the ward fails entirely.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the ward expands to Medium range.
* For 3 Success Levels, the duration extends to 24 hours and the ward persists even while the caster moves.

## Goetic Tradition

Before the first philosopher put stylus to wax to theorize the spheres, someone scratched a name on a lead tablet and pushed it through the bars of a tomb. The Goetic tradition is older than philosophy and indifferent to it. Its fundamental claim is simpler and more dangerous than Theurgy's: every power has a *true name*, and a person who knows that name has authority over its bearer regardless of the bearer's preference. The Greek Magical Papyri — those promiscuous, practical documents that combine Jewish angels with Egyptian gods with Greek daimones with Mesopotamian gate-wardens — are the Goetic tradition's most visible scripture, but the tradition itself predates any particular text. It lives in the hands of curse-workers, in the memory of women who have spent twenty years learning which chthonic warden responds to which sequence of vowels, in the mouths of itinerant exorcists who charge by the hour.

The Concord of the Seven Seals regards most Goeteia as a violation of its charter. Goetes are frequently hunted, occasionally recruited, and never trusted.

**Practitioners:** Called *goetes* (singular: *goēs*). Found across the Mediterranean — itinerant curse-workers, hired assassins, black-market magicians, and occasionally a scholar who should have known better. Concentrated in Egypt, Syria, Rome, and wherever there are unquiet dead to be compelled.

**Caster Type:** Traditional and Research. The Goetic tradition can be learned from living masters or reconstructed from papyri, though Research casters rarely know which entity they are invoking until it is too late.

**Focus:** A lead tablet (*defixio*) inscribed with the name of a bound entity, nailed shut with an iron nail, or a clay figurine (*kolossoi*) bearing a target's likeness. The Focus must be prepared fresh for any new binding.

---

### **Katadesmos** *(Binding of the Named)*

*The tongue goes first: one nail through the wax impression of the mouth. Then the hands. Then the feet. The entity named on the tablet has no choice in this matter — it is a function, and functions obey their grammar. The man paying for the service does not need to understand this.*

* **Skill:** Manipulation
* **Difficulty:** Difficult, opposed by target's WIL + Resilience
* **Cost:** 4 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** until end of scene
* **Category:** Curse spell

**Spell Effect:** The goēs names a target within Close range on a prepared defixio. The target suffers −2 to all skill tests for the duration. Additionally, the target cannot willingly move more than one zone away from the goēs without succeeding on a Hard Willpower + Resilience test.

**Curse Breaking:** The curse is broken if the defixio is found and destroyed, or if a Banishment spell (any tradition) successfully dispels it with at least 2 Success Levels.

**Flawed:** The entity named on the tablet responds to the botched invocation by making its presence known. Generate 2 Doom; the entity is now mildly hostile to the caster.

**Success Levels:**
* For 1 Success Level, the penalty increases to −3.
* For 2 Success Levels, the duration extends to 24 hours.

---

### **Epagōgē Nekydaimōn** *(Raising of a Shade)*

*The dead are many and available. The chthonic gate-wardens who manage them are amenable to negotiation, provided one uses the correct sequence of barbarous names and does not ask for anything the shade cannot provide. The shade of a murdered man knows who murdered him. This is frequently worth the price.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Hard
* **Cost:** 4 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** until end of scene
* **Category:** Summoning spell

**Spell Effect:** The goēs speaks the name of a specific dead person and the names of the two chthonic gate-wardens responsible for their shade. If the casting succeeds, the shade manifests within Close range. It is bound to answer truthfully up to power + 1 questions about events it witnessed in life or about the realm of the dead. The shade cannot affect the physical world.

**Flawed:** The wrong shade answers — or the right shade, but resentful and lying. The GM makes a secret roll; the shade's answers may be partially or entirely false.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the shade can be directed to carry a message to another specific dead person and return with a reply (takes one hour).
* For 2 Success Levels, the duration extends to 24 hours.

---

### **Agōgē Daimōnos** *(Compulsion of a Named Spirit)*

*There is a daemon called Aeshma-deva in the Persian tongue, Asmodeus in the Hebrew, and seventeen different compound names in the papyri. Each name is a different key to the same door. The goēs who knows three names has three locks on the same cage. The goēs who knows only one name knows which one to use when the cage door opens.*

* **Skill:** Manipulation
* **Difficulty:** Hard, opposed by target's WIL + Resolve
* **Cost:** 5 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** end of scene
* **Category:** Control spell

**Spell Effect:** The goēs speaks the true name of a supernatural creature within Close range. If the creature fails to resist, it is compelled to follow one explicit instruction of 25 words or fewer per Success Level. The instruction cannot include self-destruction or actions contrary to the creature's fundamental nature. While under compulsion the creature suffers −2 to all skill tests.

**Flawed:** The name is mispronounced. The creature is not compelled — it is *warned*. It immediately attacks the caster with full awareness that its name has been exposed. Generate 2 Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* For 1 Success Level, the instruction limit increases by 25 words.
* For 2 Success Levels, the duration extends to 24 hours.
* For 3 Success Levels, the creature may be given two separate instructions rather than one.

---

### **Maleficium** *(Working of Harm)*

*The simplest of the old techniques. A wax image, a lock of hair, a name spoken over a fire built from wood taken from a graveyard. The target's body is a text; the maleficium is an edit. Whether the target believes in such things is entirely irrelevant.*

* **Skill:** Observation
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 3 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** 24 hours
* **Category:** Curse spell

**Spell Effect:** The goēs prepares a wax or clay image (*kolossoi*) incorporating a material connection to the target (hair, nail, cloth, blood — any physical sample gathered within the past month). The target, who may be anywhere within Long range, suffers power physical Stress with the Persistent 3 effect as the maleficium works on their body over the following day.

**Curse Breaking:** Destroying the kolossoi ends the Persistent effect immediately.

**Flawed:** The material connection was insufficiently fresh or incorrectly identified. The Persistent effect does not manifest; the caster wastes the cost.

**Success Levels:**
* For 1 Success Level, add the Vicious effect to the initial Stress.
* For 2 Success Levels, the Persistent rating increases to 6.

---

### **Styx Horkos** *(Oath of the River of Hate)*

*By the waters of Styx the gods swear unbreakable oaths. The goēs who knows the correct chthonic word for the river can lay that same oath on a mortal throat — and the Styx does not distinguish between Olympian and slave.*

* **Skill:** Manipulation
* **Difficulty:** Difficult, opposed by target's WIL + Resilience
* **Cost:** 4 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** permanent (until the oath is fulfilled or broken)
* **Category:** Curse spell

**Spell Effect:** The goēs binds a target within Close range with an oath of one specific action or prohibition (e.g., "you will not betray this name to your master" or "you will deliver this letter before the new moon"). While the oath is active the target suffers −1 to all skill tests — the Styx weighs on the oath-breaker's potential. If the target deliberately breaks the oath, they immediately suffer power + 3 mental Stress with Intense and generate 1 Doom.

**Curse Breaking:** A successful Banishment spell from any tradition (Hard difficulty) dissolves the oath-binding. Alternatively the goēs who cast the spell can release it voluntarily.

**Flawed:** The chthonic word is mispronounced. The oath binds the *caster* instead of the target for one scene. Generate 1 Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the oath can bind two targets simultaneously.
* For 3 Success Levels, the penalty while the oath is active increases to −2.

---

### **Nekromanteion** *(Consultation of the Underworld)*

*There are places — Avernus, the cave at Herakleia Pontika, certain spots along the Orontes — where the boundary between the living and the dead is not a boundary at all but a membrane, and the membrane answers questions. The goēs who knows the threshold-names can open the consultation anywhere, given time and silence.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Very Hard
* **Cost:** 5 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Divination spell

**Spell Effect:** The goēs performs a nekromantic consultation, opening a channel to the chthonic archive — the accumulated memory of the dead in a given region. They may ask up to 3 questions about any event that occurred within Long range in the past year; the answers arrive as whispers, shadows on walls, or involuntary speech through the caster's mouth. The dead know what they witnessed; they do not know what they did not witness.

**Flawed:** The archive opens too wide. The GM may ask the caster's player one question about a character secret, which the GM now knows the dead have whispered to something that serves no friendly power. Generate 3 Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the time window extends to ten years.
* For 2 Success Levels, the range restriction is lifted entirely — the dead remember events anywhere if the goēs can name the place correctly.

---

### **Defixio Animae** *(Binding of the Soul)*

*The most feared technique in the goetic canon. The defixio does not curse the body; it nails the soul to the tablet. While nailed, the soul cannot leave — cannot die — and serves as a battery for the goēs who holds the nail. The Concord has made this spell a capital offence in six of its seven sealing jurisdictions.*

* **Skill:** Manipulation
* **Difficulty:** Very Hard, opposed by target's WIL + Resolve
* **Cost:** 6 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** permanent (until the defixio is destroyed)
* **Category:** Control spell

**Spell Effect:** The goēs creates a special defixio — a lead tablet inscribed with the target's full name, birthplace, mother's name, and at least one drop of their blood. If the target fails to resist, their soul is partially bound. While bound, the target is compelled to obey the goēs's direct spoken commands (as per *Agōgē Daimōnos*, but without the word limit); generates 1 Doom when this compulsion is exercised; and cannot be killed by conventional means — they stabilize at 1 Wound instead of dying. The defixio itself must be physically secured by the caster.

**Flawed:** The soul-binding backlashes through the goēs's own defixio-focus. The caster suffers power + 3 mental Stress with Drain and immediately generates 3 Doom. The spell fails entirely.

## Hermetic Tradition

*As above, so below; as within, so without; as the soul, so the world.* The Hermetic tradition is an inheritance from the libraries of Alexandria, from the centuries when Greek philosophy and Egyptian temple-science and Babylonian star-lore mixed in the same schoolrooms and produced something that was none of them and all of them. Its scripture is the *Corpus Hermeticum* — a collection of dialogues between the thrice-great Hermes and his students on the subject of the divine mind, the structure of the cosmos, and the relationship between names and things. The hermetist does not compel reality; they perceive the web of correspondence connecting all things, and they adjust one strand in the knowledge that the adjustment propagates through the web. Planetary metals resonate with planetary spheres. Seven herbs cure seven ailments. Every event in the visible world mirrors an event in the invisible. The hermetist pulls the mirror.

The danger of the Hermetic path is not the power it grants but the dissolution it requires. To truly perceive the correspondence-web is to stop experiencing oneself as a separate entity. Practitioners who go too far dissolve into the Nous — the divine mind — and are found, days later, sitting in their libraries unable to answer to their own names.

**Practitioners:** Called *hermetists* or *philosophers*. Found in Alexandria, Antioch, Rome, and wherever there are well-stocked libraries. The tradition has no formal institution — it is a body of knowledge rather than a school.

**Caster Type:** Research primarily; Dabblers also common (the Hermetic texts are widely copied). Any literate person can attempt the simpler workings; few survive the more advanced applications.

**Focus:** A set of seven planetary seals — small disks of the appropriate metal for each sphere (silver for Moon, quicksilver-coated for Mercury, copper for Venus, gold for Sun, iron for Mars, tin for Jupiter, lead for Saturn) — or a Hermetic *table* inscribed with the Tree of Emanation.

---

### **Sympatheia** *(Working of Correspondence)*

*The lead correspondent to Saturn; the sapphire to the Moon; the raven to Saturn again, and the wolf to the Moon, and the silver lamp in the west tower to the silver seal in the caster's hand. To alter one is to alter all. The practitioner need only know which chain to pull.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Challenging
* **Cost:** 2 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** until end of scene
* **Category:** Blessing spell

**Spell Effect:** The hermetist identifies a correspondence-chain connecting an object or substance they possess to a target within Close range. They impose a sympathetic link: for the duration, any Stress suffered by the linked object is also suffered (at −2, minimum 1) by the target, and any fortune affecting the target also affects the linked object. Common chains: a wax candle linked to a specific person (snuffing it inflicts Stun), a stone linked to a wall (breaking it weakens the wall's structural integrity).

**Flawed:** The correspondence is misidentified and the link runs backward. The caster suffers the next Stress from the chain rather than the target.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the Stress reduction for the target decreases from −2 to −1 (the link transmits more faithfully).
* For 2 Success Levels, the hermetist may maintain two simultaneous correspondence-chains.

---

### **Planētēs Phronēsis** *(Wisdom of the Wanderers)*

*The wandering stars move through the fixed stars as a stylus moves through wax. Each leaves a mark; each mark is legible to the trained eye. The philosopher who can read seven marks at once sees the same truth from seven different angles, and the truth is not ambiguous when seen from seven angles.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Hard
* **Cost:** 3 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** until end of scene
* **Category:** Divination spell

**Spell Effect:** The hermetist consults their planetary seals and performs a brief astrological correspondence-reading over a target person, object, or location they can see or touch. They receive one true sentence of information about the target for each Success Level achieved (minimum 1) — planetary correspondences, hidden associations, past influences, likely futures. This information arrives as symbolic impressions that must be interpreted; it is never ambiguous enough to be cryptic, but it is never literally stated.

**Flawed:** The philosopher's reading eye turns inward. They receive accurate information about themselves rather than the target — usually deeply unwelcome information. The GM provides one true, uncomfortable sentence.

**Success Levels:**
* For 1 Success Level, +1 additional true sentence of information.
* For 2 Success Levels, the reading can penetrate active magical concealment.

---

### **Nous Katalēpsis** *(Seizure of the Divine Mind)*

*The texts say the Nous is everywhere at once and available to all. They do not say it is safe. The philosopher who genuinely opens to the divine mind achieves, for a moment, perfect perception — they see all correspondence-chains operating in their vicinity simultaneously. The experience is, by all accounts, something between sublime and shattering.*

* **Skill:** Resilience
* **Difficulty:** Hard
* **Cost:** 4 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Divination spell

**Spell Effect:** The hermetist opens their mind to the Nous for one breath's duration. They instantly perceive every active magical working, bound entity, or supernatural effect within Close range — its tradition of origin, approximate power, and the name of its practitioner or patron if such is knowable. They also gain +3 to all Lore tests made in the next scene regarding anything they perceived.

**Flawed:** The opening is too wide. The caster suffers power mental Stress with Intense and gains the scar **"The Nous Has Seen Through Me"** — something in the correspondence-web now knows the hermetist's true correspondence-address. Generate 2 Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the range extends to Medium.
* For 3 Success Levels, the hermetist may ask one question of the Nous itself: "Is the correspondence-chain I am working correct?" — answered as a yes or no by the GM.

---

### **Metallourgeia Psuchēs** *(Transmutation of the Soul)*

*The alchemists say the work of transmuting lead to gold is not about lead and gold. It is about the leaden weight of material attachment and the golden clarity of the intellect aligned with the Nous. The philosopher who understands this correctly finds the formula works in both directions.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 3 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** rounds equal to power
* **Category:** Transmutation spell

**Spell Effect:** The hermetist works a transmutation of spiritual quality on themselves or one willing target within Touch range. Choose one of the following effects for the duration:
- **Saturnine Solidity**: the target's Wounds increase by power (removed at the end of the duration, injuries caused by these extra Wounds are permanent).
- **Solar Radiance**: the target gains +2 to all Presence and Manipulation tests.
- **Lunar Fluidity**: the target can perceive supernatural creatures and active magical workings as a shimmer on the air.

**Flawed:** The transmutation is impure. The chosen effect manifests but the target also suffers −1 to one attribute (GM's choice) until they sleep.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the caster may apply two effects simultaneously to the same target.

---

### **Sphragis Tou Hermou** *(Seal of Hermes)*

*The god Hermes moves between all realms because he is the correspondence between them. His seal does not exclude the supernatural — it acknowledges it and reminds it that acknowledgement implies limits.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Challenging
* **Cost:** 2 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** 24 hours
* **Category:** Ward spell

**Spell Effect:** The hermetist draws the Hermetic seal on a doorway, threshold, vessel, or person using a stylus and the appropriate planetary metal's ink. The seal creates a correspondence-barrier: any supernatural creature attempting to cross the threshold or affect the sealed target must first succeed on a Difficult Willpower + Resolve test. Ordinary humans are unimpeded.

**Flawed:** The seal is drawn in the wrong correspondence-order. It functions but also acts as a beacon, marking the sealed location as significant in the correspondence-web. Generate 1 Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the difficulty for supernaturals to cross increases to Hard.

## Zoroastrian Tradition

There is a fire that Zarathustra lit. Not metaphorically — the Atar, the sacred fire kept perpetually burning in the fire-temples of the Mobedān, is a continuous flame passed by hand from priest to priest across centuries. The Zoroastrian magical tradition is built on the premise that Ahura Mazda, the Wise Lord, is the one Watcher among all the great powers who remains genuinely aligned with truth and light — that the other gods are *daēvas*, lies-made-flesh, and that the work of the Magu is to impose *Asha* (Truth, Right Order) on the *Druj* (the Lie, the Corruption, everything the daēvas represent). This is not mere theology. When a Magu speaks Asha against a daēva, the daēva flinches because the word is structurally incompatible with its existence.

The Sasanian Mobedān regard the Concord of the Seven Seals with polite professional suspicion. The Concord theurgists invoke the daimones as servants; the Mobedān consider this collaboration with the Druj and decline to comment further.

**Practitioners:** Called *Magu* (singular) or *Mobedān* (plural). Found in the Sasanian Empire, Mesopotamia, and parts of Syria. Fire-temple-initiated only; the lineage descends without interruption from the Achaemenid magi.

**Caster Type:** Traditional only. The sacred fire cannot be carried in a text.

**Focus:** A fire-vessel — a small portable brazier or oil lamp kept perpetually alight, lit from the sacred fire of a legitimate fire-temple. If the flame is extinguished, all bound spells of the Mobedān immediately become inactive. Relighting from an ordinary flame does not restore the tradition's power; the caster must relight from a fire-temple flame within seven days or lose their access until a full re-initiation.

### **Atar Vohu** *(The Good Fire)*

*Ahura Mazda's first creation was light. His second was fire. The Atar that burns in the temple is both simultaneously — a little piece of the beginning of the world, kept alive by the priests who remember what it is.*

* **Skill:** Resilience
* **Difficulty:** Difficult

Additionally, any darkness — natural or magically created — within Close range is dispelled.

**Flawed:** The flame gutters (though it does not go out). The blessing manifests but the Cover resistance does not.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the resistance bonus increases to power.
* For 2 Success Levels, the range extends to Medium.

### **Yāsna Druj** *(Curse Upon the Lie)*

Against Zoroastrian demons specifically (Aeshma, Nasu, Azi Dahaka and their kin), the Stress gains Vicious.

**Flawed:** The lie-aspect name is spoken incorrectly and the daēva's true name inverts the effect. The caster suffers power mental Stress with Stun. Generate 1 Doom.

**Success Levels:**

## Christian Hieratica

The priests of Christ work real miracles. The Church knows this; the Concord knows this; the Nephilim and the daimones know this most directly, having been on the receiving end. Whether the power derives from the God of Abraham as the bishops understand him, from the accumulated worship-investment of ten million believers, or from some more ambiguous arrangement between a dead Galilean wonder-worker and a Gnostic principle that predates him — the hierarchy of the Church does not encourage such questions, and those who ask them aloud in the wrong setting are briefly available for heresy proceedings.

A Christian priest does not generate magical power. They spend it. The worship-harvest of their patron flows through the relic in their reliquary, and the relic is the physical conduit of that flow. A bishop with a full cathedral congregation in Constantinople handles more power than a hermit monk with three congregants in the desert — the numbers are real, the accounting is exact, and the patron manages the ledger.

**Practitioners:** Bishops, priests, deacons, and consecrated monks and nuns. Found across the former Roman Empire, North Africa, Anatolia, and Syria.

**Caster Type:** Traditional only. Ordination is the tradition's initiatory structure.

**Focus:** A relic — bone fragment, cloth from a saint's garment, vial of holy oil — housed in a reliquary. The relic is the physical medium through which the patron's harvested power flows. A priest stripped of their reliquary cannot work Hieratica until they acquire or consecrate another.

### **Impositio Manuum** *(Laying on of Hands)*

Against Stress inflicted by supernatural means (curses, daimonic attack, undead affliction), add +2 additional Stress removed.

**Flawed:** The reliquary's power reserves are lower than expected. The target removes Stress equal to power only.

**Success Levels:**
* For 1 Success Level, +2 additional Stress removed.
* For 2 Success Levels, one injury suffered by the target begins mending as if treated by a skilled physician.

## Coptic/Egyptian Hieratica

The temples of Isis and Osiris and Thoth and Horus are still standing along the Nile. Some have been converted to churches; others stand empty; a handful, in the most isolated curves of Upper Egypt, in the deep desert near the Faiyum, still maintain their fires and their chants and their careful preservation of knowledge that was already ancient when Alexander visited the oracle at Siwah. The priests who serve these dying temples are called *hem-netjer* — servants of god — and the gods they serve are genuinely, visibly diminishing. The worship-harvest is measured in hundreds where once it was measured in hundreds of thousands. Every working this tradition can perform, it performed better a century ago. Every spell costs a little more than it should.

There is something else, though. The old Egyptian tradition carries an accumulated depth of knowledge about death, transformation, and the weighing of the soul that no other tradition in the hidden world can match. The Coptic/Egyptian hieratist who reaches into the oldest strata of the tradition touches techniques that the theurgists and the hermetists are still trying to rediscover from fragments.

**Practitioners:** Called *hem-netjer* (servant of god) or *kher-heb* (lector priest). Found in Egypt — primarily the Faiyum, Memphis, and the isolated temple complexes of Upper Egypt. A very few serve in Alexandria, coexisting uncomfortably with the hermetists.

**Caster Type:** Traditional only. The tradition requires initiation into a living temple lineage.

**Focus:** A *was*-sceptre (a carved staff surmounted by a stylized animal head, emblem of divine power) or a sacred sistrum (a rattle whose sound is the voice of Hathor). The focus must be consecrated in a legitimate temple; a *was*-sceptre fashioned without consecration is merely a staff.

### **Wedjat Amulet** *(Eye of Horus)*

* **Difficulty:** Challenging
* **Cost:** 2 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** 24 hours

For the duration, the target gains Cover resistance equal to power against the next instance of Stress they would suffer. Once triggered, the Ward is exhausted.

**Flawed:** The consecration draws on the patron's diminished reserves; the amulet provides Cover resistance equal to power − 1 (minimum 0).

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the amulet can absorb two separate instances of Stress before expiring.
* For 2 Success Levels, the amulet also grants Morale resistance equal to power against mental Stress until triggered.

### **Heka Rn** *(Magic of the Name)*

**Success Levels:**

* For 2 Success Levels, range extends to Medium.

*The Duat — the realm of the dead — is not elsewhere. It is the same space as the living world, slightly to the left. ### **Weighing of the Heart**

**Flawed:** The scales find the priest themselves too light — a moment of self-doubt undermines the invocation. The curse does not land.

**Success Levels:**

### **Sekhem Kheperu** *(Power of Transformation)*

*The gods change forms constantly. Ra is falcon at dawn, sun-disk at noon, scarab-beetle at sunset. The initiated priest carries a portion of this transformative power — not the full divine shape-change, which would destroy a mortal body, but enough to pass through a guarded door or speak to a predator as an equal.*

* **Skill:** Survival
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 4 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** rounds equal to power
* **Category:** Transmutation spell

**Spell Effect:** The hem-netjer invokes the transformative power of the gods and alters their surface presentation for the duration. The transformation is not a full shape-change — they remain essentially human — but they may adopt any of the following: the aspect of Horus (appear as a figure of authority and divine mandate; all Presence tests gain +2), the aspect of Set (appear as a figure of power and threat; all Intimidation tests gain +2 and enemies take −1 to attacks against the priest), or the aspect of Thoth (appear as a figure of ancient wisdom; all Lore and Insight tests gain +2 and supernatural creatures recognize the priest as a practitioner of an ancient and legitimate tradition).

**Flawed:** The invocation draws too much from the diminished patron's reserve. The transformation manifests but lasts only one round.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the duration extends to hours equal to power.

---

### **Isis Knot** *(Tyet Warding)*

*The tyet — the knot of Isis — is bound into a blood-red cloth or garnet amulet and speaks to the power Isis holds over life, death, and the blood that connects them. It has been consecrated against violence since before the first dynasty. Its record is unbroken.*

* **Skill:** Medicae
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 4 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** 24 hours
* **Category:** Ward spell

**Spell Effect:** The hem-netjer consecrates a tyet-amulet and ties it around a target within Touch range. The target gains the following protections for the duration: Cover resistance equal to power against all physical Stress; Morale resistance equal to power − 1 against all mental Stress (the old gods' diminishment — this used to be equal to power); and they cannot be detected by supernatural divination (scrying, spirit-sight, necromantic contact) unless they choose to be.

**Flawed:** The diminished patron provides what it can. The Cover resistance applies only to the first instance of Stress; thereafter the ward is exhausted.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the Morale resistance equals power (not power − 1), briefly matching the tradition's older potency.
* For 2 Success Levels, the ward also deflects one hostile magical effect (a curse, a control attempt) during the duration — it absorbs the effect and the tyet turns black and crumbles; the ward is otherwise still active.

---

### **Am-Duat Rite** *(Traversal of the Underworld Hours)*

*The sun passes through twelve hours of the Duat each night and emerges renewed. A priest who knows the names of the wardens of each hour can, in extremis, impose that traversal on a dying soul — dragging it through the twelve gates and out the other side, still alive. The gods charge a price. They always charge a price.*

* **Skill:** Medicae
* **Difficulty:** Very Hard
* **Cost:** 6 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Ward spell

**Spell Effect:** The hem-netjer performs the emergency rite of the twelve Duat-hours over a dying target (one who would otherwise die at the end of this scene or has just been reduced to 0 Wounds). The target is stabilized and survives — but the gods exact their payment. The target returns to 1 Wound, all injuries persist, and they gain the permanent scar **"I Have Passed Through the Twelve Hours"**: until this scar is removed through a separate rite (requiring the hem-netjer to spend one week in the target's service, performing the nightly recitation), the target finds sleep disturbed and may not benefit from a full rest's recovery of Stress on consecutive nights.

**Flawed:** The twelve-hour rite is performed incompletely. The target is stabilized but the price is doubled: the scar persists until *two* separate week-long rites have been performed, and the target suffers −1 to all skill tests until the first is complete. Generate 2 Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the target recovers to 3 Wounds rather than 1.
* For 3 Success Levels, the scar's specific penalty (no consecutive rest recovery) is waived, though the scar itself remains until the rite is completed.

## Jewish Babylonian Tradition

The *ba'alei shem* — the masters of the Name — inherit the deepest known library of entity-names in the hidden world. The Babylonian Jewish tradition sits at the intersection of three different name-cataloguing systems: the old Babylonian demonological tradition, absorbed during the Exile; the Merkabah and Hekhalot mysticism, which catalogues the celestial hierarchy from serafim down to the least gate-warden; and the Solomonic tradition, which provides the practical apparatus for binding and dismissing the entities those catalogues describe. The *Sefer ha-Razim* — Book of the Mysteries — the *Shiur Komah* — the Book of Dimensions — these are not the tradition's only texts, but they are the most practical. A *ba'al shem* who knows both is better armed than almost any other practitioner in the visible world.

The tradition's edge and its danger are the same thing: the entities it names are *aware* that they are being named. An angel invoked by the ba'alei shem knows the name being used, knows where it is being spoken, and chooses to respond or not. The entities do not invariably comply. The malakhim (angels) are particularly proud; the shedim (demons) are particularly patient; and the Sar ha-Panim (princes of the divine face) simply do not respond to invocations from anyone without a spotless soul-record, regardless of technical competence.

**Practitioners:** Called *ba'alei shem* (masters of the Name) or *hakhamim* (sages). Found in Babylonia, Mesopotamia, Palestine, and diaspora communities in Alexandria and Rome.

**Caster Type:** Traditional and Research. The tradition can be learned from living masters (Traditional) or reconstructed from the Babylonian Talmud and Hekhalot texts (Research).

**Focus:** A *mezuzah* scroll (a parchment inscribed with divine names, rolled and sealed, carried in a small case) or a Solomonic seal ring (*tabaʿat Shlomo*) inscribed with the Tetragrammaton and the binding-seal of King Solomon. The seal ring is more powerful but more dangerous — entities bound by Solomon's seal remember that binding.

---

### **Qeri'at Shem** *(Calling of the Name)*

*The Name is not a word. It is a fact about the nature of reality that happens to be expressible as a word. When the ba'al shem pronounces it correctly — with the correct cantillation, the correct breath, the correct intention — the universe hears it as a fact, not as a request.*

* **Skill:** Resilience
* **Difficulty:** Challenging
* **Cost:** 2 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** until end of scene
* **Category:** Ward spell

**Spell Effect:** The ba'al shem traces the divine Name on their own forehead or the forehead of a willing target and speaks the invocation. The target is marked with divine authority for the duration: supernatural creatures must make a Difficult Willpower test before targeting the marked individual with any hostile magical working; if they fail, the working cannot be directed at this target this scene. Mundane attackers are unaffected.

**Flawed:** The cantillation is incorrect. The Name is spoken but not heard. The ward does not manifest; the cost is lost.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the difficulty for supernaturals increases to Hard.
* For 2 Success Levels, the mark becomes visible to supernatural sight — a glow that warns entities off before they even attempt to target the marked person.

---

### **Invocatio Malakhim** *(Summoning of Angels)*

*The Hekhalot texts provide the names, seals, and preferred hours of over three hundred angelic beings. They also provide, in careful detail, the conditions under which each angel will respond and the conditions under which an invocation will simply be ignored. The ba'al shem who skips the preliminary purification rites may find they are calling into an empty room.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Hard
* **Cost:** 4 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** until end of scene
* **Category:** Summoning spell

**Spell Effect:** The ba'al shem invokes a named malakh (angel) using its correct appellation, seal, and preferred invocation from the Hekhalot literature. If the caster has observed the preliminary purifications (three days of clean diet, prayer, and no shedding of blood), the angel manifests within Close range as a barely visible luminous presence and renders one of the following services: provides power + 1 true sentences of information about matters within its sphere of authority; assists with one Banishment spell (granting +2 to the roll); or stands guard over one location until sunrise.

**Flawed:** The invocation is correct but the caster's preliminary purifications were incomplete. The angel does not respond, and the silence is noted. For the next 24 hours, all Banishment spells the ba'al shem casts suffer +1 Difficulty. Generate 1 Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, two services may be requested rather than one.
* For 3 Success Levels, the angel manifests visibly — a figure of light — and can physically intervene, acting as an ally with power equal to the caster's for one scene.

---

### **Khotimat Shlomo** *(Seal of Solomon)*

*The seal ring is not a metaphor about kingship. Solomon bound seventy-two demon-princes with it, and the *Sefer ha-Razim* preserves the method. The binding works because the seal carries the Name three times in three scripts, and any entity whose name appears in the tradition's catalogues recognizes the authority those three inscriptions represent.*

* **Skill:** Manipulation
* **Difficulty:** Hard, opposed by target's WIL + Resolve
* **Cost:** 5 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** permanent (until the seal is deliberately broken)
* **Category:** Control spell

**Spell Effect:** The ba'al shem presses the Solomonic seal ring against a surface (the ground, a vessel, a wall) and speaks the entity's name in full. A supernatural target within Close range that fails to resist is bound: it cannot leave the area within Close range of the seal's impression, cannot use hostile abilities against the caster or their allies, and must answer truthfully any direct question put to it by the caster. The entity knows precisely what has been done and who did it.

**Flawed:** The seal is correctly applied but the entity's name was imperfectly recalled — one syllable wrong, one consonant shifted. The entity is not bound. It is *offended*. It acts immediately against the caster if it can, generating 3 Doom. The seal is expended regardless.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the binding includes a compulsion to perform one service (as per Goetic *Agōgē Daimōnos*) in addition to the restrictions.
* For 3 Success Levels, the bound entity cannot perceive or act on the caster even when the caster is not present — the binding is total.

---

### **Refuʾat ha-Shem** *(Healing of the Name)*

*The body is created in the divine image; illness is a corruption of that image; the correct Name, applied directly to the site of corruption, corrects the description and the body follows. This is not very different from what the hermetists call the *Eikōn* — the practitioners of both traditions prefer not to discuss the resemblance.*

* **Skill:** Medicae
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 3 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Blessing spell

**Spell Effect:** The ba'al shem writes or speaks the appropriate divine Name over a target within Touch range. The target immediately removes Stress equal to power + 2. If the Stress being removed is the result of a supernatural curse, also remove one ongoing Curse effect from the target (it is dispelled).

**Flawed:** The name chosen is correct but not the most efficacious for this particular affliction. The target removes Stress equal to power only; any curse is not dispelled.

**Success Levels:**
* For 1 Success Level, +2 additional Stress removed.
* For 2 Success Levels, one injury also begins mending, as if treated by a skilled healer.
* For 3 Success Levels, the healing extends to all allies within Close range at power only.

---

### **Harʾat ha-Shedim** *(Destruction of Demons)*

*The shedim are not the same as the malakhim inverted. They are something else — older than the current celestial order, aligned with no living tradition's patron, operating on their own agenda. They are patient, knowledgeable, and entirely without sympathy. The ba'al shem who faces one in the field calls on Solomon's precedent.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 4 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Banishment spell

**Spell Effect:** The ba'al shem speaks the full liturgy of names — the seventy-two shedim-names, abbreviated to the three most relevant for the target's classification — and commands the creature by Solomon's authority. The target suffers power + 2 mental Stress with Intense. If reduced to 0 Resolve, the creature is banished. Against shedim specifically, this spell gains Vicious. Against any named creature in the tradition's catalogues (about half of all supernatural creatures the characters will encounter), this spell's difficulty reduces by one step.

**Flawed:** One of the three relevant shedim-names is recalled incorrectly. The entity recognizes the error and is not harmed; instead it gains the knowledge of who is invoking it and where. Generate 2 Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, range extends from Close to Medium.
* For 3 Success Levels, a banished shedim cannot re-manifest in the same geographic region for one year.

---

### **Histaklut ha-Merkavah** *(Vision of the Chariot)*

*Ezekiel saw the chariot — the divine throne-vehicle — and described it in terms that the Hekhalot mystics have been unpacking for five centuries. Entering the vision is not metaphor. The ba'al shem who successfully ascends the seven heavenly halls stands, briefly, in a position from which the entire spiritual topology of the local region is visible.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Very Hard
* **Cost:** 5 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Divination spell

**Spell Effect:** The ba'al shem enters the Merkavah vision — a state of deep trance that takes ten minutes and leaves them physically vulnerable but spiritually elevated. From this vantage, they perceive the complete spiritual map of everything within Long range: all active magical workings, all supernatural presences, all curse-bindings, all hidden connections between entities, and the true nature of any being they observe directly (its tradition, its patron, its approximate power, and one secret it is actively concealing). The caster may ask the GM up to three direct yes/no questions about anything they observe.

**Flawed:** The ascent reaches the third hall rather than the seventh. The caster gains information about Close range only, not Long, and one of the GM's three answers is false (GM chooses which; player does not know which). Generate 1 Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, one of the three questions may be open (a sentence of information rather than yes/no).
* For 3 Success Levels, the caster may act on one piece of information immediately — a banishment, a warding, or a truth-speaking — as a free action upon returning from the vision.

---

### **Kefidat ha-Shem** *(Binding of the Name)*

*The most dangerous of the Solomonic techniques. The practitioner does not merely compel an entity — they rewrite the entity's functional identity, replacing its name in the celestial catalogue with a new name that belongs to the ba'al shem. The entity becomes, for the duration, a part of the caster's extended spiritual household. The entity knows this. It waits.*

* **Skill:** Manipulation
* **Difficulty:** Very Hard, opposed by target's WIL + Resolve
* **Cost:** 6 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** 24 hours
* **Category:** Control spell

**Spell Effect:** The ba'al shem speaks a new name over a supernatural target within Close range — a name derived from the target's original name by systematic permutation, which constitutes a temporary identity-rewrite. If the target fails to resist, it is completely controlled for the duration: it follows all instructions, uses all abilities at the caster's direction, and cannot act against the caster or their allies. When the duration expires, the entity's original name reasserts. At the moment of expiration, the entity generates 2 Doom and immediately attacks the caster if it is able to do so and still present.

**Flawed:** The name-permutation is incorrect. The entity is not bound; it is named as prey. It immediately moves to destroy the caster, targeting them specifically and exclusively until either the caster escapes beyond its range or it is banished. Generate 3 Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the duration extends to one week.
* For 3 Success Levels, when the duration expires, the entity departs peacefully rather than attacking — the name-rewrite is clean enough that it holds no grudge.

## Runic Tradition

Literacy is almost unheard of among the Germanic peoples — the writing of runes is reserved nearly exclusively for magic, and the magic of runes is not theoretical but immediate and physical. Runes are carved into objects that must then enter the world and act. A rune carved on a doorpost keeps out the hungry dead. A rune carved on a blade makes it eager. The Germanic and Norse practitioners of this tradition work in an oral-initiatory lineage that stretches back through the shamanic practices of the Migration Age steppe — their cosmology names the World-Tree, the nine worlds, the Norns who weave fate, and the gods who read and enact what the Norns have woven. In 500 AD this tradition is at the height of its practical power, carried in the belts and belt-knives of Gothic federates serving in Roman armies, in the halls of Frankish war-leaders, in the longhouses of settlements that do not yet have churches.

**Practitioners:** Called *rune-workers* or, in the Norse tradition, *seiðr*-practitioners and *vitki* (rune-masters). Found throughout the Germanic kingdoms — Frankish Gaul, Gothic Italy and Hispania, Saxon Britain, and the Norse settlements along the Baltic and North Sea coasts.

**Caster Type:** Traditional only.

**Focus:** A carved rune-stave — a length of yew, ash, or bone inscribed with the practitioner's working-runes, each carved with their own blood at initiation.

---

### **Blessing of Fylgjur**

*The fylgja accompanies every soul born into the world — a companion-spirit, a guardian, a piece of the Norns' original handiwork that walks beside the person it belongs to. The rune-worker who knows the fylgja's name can, briefly, give it a physical form powerful enough to share its strength with others.*

* **Skill:** Medicae
* **Difficulty:** Hard
* **Cost:** 4 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Ward spell

**Spell Effect:** All allied creatures within Close range of the rune-worker immediately remove Stress equal to the rune-worker's power.

**Flawed:** The invocation blurs the distinction between ally and enemy. All creatures within Close range — friend and foe — are affected.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, each affected ally also removes one injury.
* For 2 Success Levels, range extends to Medium.

---

### **Bounty of Baldr**

*Baldr is the one the weapons turn away from. That a god of this quality was killed by an arrow of mistletoe is a fact that practitioners of the tradition prefer not to discuss with outsiders. The protection remains real; the god is elsewhere.*

* **Skill:** Resilience
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 3 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** rounds equal to power
* **Category:** Ward spell

**Spell Effect:** Attacks made against the rune-worker or allies within Close range suffer +2 Difficulty for the duration.

**Success Levels:**
* Per Success Level, affected allies gain additional Cover resistance equal to the number of Success Levels.

---

### **Curse of Loki**

*Loki does not hate. He simply cannot stop noticing the gap between what things are and what they could be, and this noticing, directed outward with intent, is sufficient.*

* **Skill:** Manipulation
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 5 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** All enemies within Close range suffer power + 2 mental Stress with the Stun effect.

**Flawed:** The invocation cannot distinguish between ally and enemy. All creatures in Close range are affected.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, add the Persistent 6 effect.
* For 2 Success Levels, add the Snare effect.

---

### **Hammer of Thunraz**

*Thunraz — Donar to the Franks, Thor to the Norse — is the guarantor of the boundary between ordered and unordered world. His hammer is not a weapon primarily; it is a consecration tool. It sets boundaries. What ignores boundaries is what gets struck.*

* **Skill:** Fighting
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 4 (Piercing 1 on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** A single enemy within Medium range suffers power + 2 physical Stress with the Area effect.

**Success Levels:**
* For 1 Success Level, the Area effect is replaced with Piercing 2.
* For 2 Success Levels, add Vicious.
* For 2 Success Levels, add Stun.

---

### **Ravens of Wodan**

*The two ravens sit on the Allfather's shoulders and whisper the day's events in his ears. The rune-worker who knows their names can borrow the ravens' function — not intelligence, exactly, but *awareness*, a sense of the entire field of battle as a single coherent fact.*

* **Skill:** Academia
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 4 (Piercing 1 on Complication)
* **Duration:** rounds equal to power
* **Category:** Ward spell

**Spell Effect:** The rune-worker and all allies within Close range gain Morale resistance equal to the rune-worker's power for the duration.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, affected allies also gain +2 Stress on all melee attacks.
* For 3 Success Levels (each), each affected ally gains 1 Fortune point (Fortune gained this way is lost at the end of the duration).

---

### **Swiftness of Sleipnir**

*Eight legs, and Sleipnir runs between the worlds. The rune-worker does not borrow the horse — they borrow the principle of eight-legged motion, which is a principle about not being anywhere you are not supposed to be.*

* **Skill:** Survival
* **Difficulty:** Difficult

* **Category:** Blessing spell

**Spell Effect:** The rune-worker and all allies within Close range may each immediately move up to 2 zones. For the duration of the current combat, Keep the Initiative actions cost the affected characters 0 Success Levels.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, each affected character may take one free minor action on their next turn.

## Oracular Tradition

Oracular magic is ancient and diverse in its methods and origins — the Pythia at Delphi, the haruspex reading entrails in a Roman augural tent, the Norse *vǫlva* sitting on her high seat and speaking in someone else's voice. What these practitioners share is not a cosmology but a *relationship* with the threads of fate that run through all events: they can see them, touch them, and on their best days, redirect them. By 500 AD the Pythia has been silent for a century, Delphi is officially closed, and the surviving oracular practitioners are either working under the cover of legitimate temple roles or operating as itinerant consultants. The Norse *seiðr* tradition, which arrives at the same techniques through a different mythology, is flourishing.

**Practitioners:** Called *oraculum* (Latin), *mantis* (Greek), or *vǫlva/seiðr-worker* (Norse). Found across the former Roman world and the Norse/Germanic territories.

**Caster Type:** Traditional and Research. The living initiatory tradition is primary; the Greek divinatory texts support Research access.

**Focus:** A polished obsidian mirror, a bronze bowl of water, or a carved seiðr-staff (*seiðstafr*) — any object used as the focal medium for fate-vision.

---

### **Augury**

*An hour watching the ravens over the forum tells you nothing. Two hours watching them, knowing their names, having made an offering this morning — that tells you rather more. The birds are not the message. They are the medium the universe uses because it knows you are watching.*

* **Skill:** Observation
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 3 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** up to 24 hours
* **Category:** Manifestation spell

**Spell Effect:** The oracle spends 10 minutes consulting birds, entrails, the pattern of cracks in a heated shoulder-blade, or the movement of smoke over their focus. The omen is either Auspicious or Inauspicious. The oracle chooses which to invoke, and the effect activates once during the duration and then ends:
- **Auspicious:** The oracle or one designated ally may re-roll any single set of dice once, keeping the better result.
- **Inauspicious:** The oracle may force the GM to ignore one Doom spend (the Doom is returned to the pool unspent), then the effect ends.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the duration extends by 24 hours.
* For 2 Success Levels, the oracle may choose whether the omen is Auspicious or Inauspicious after seeing the augury (rather than declaring in advance).

---

### **Guiding Hand**

*The Parcae know which thread leads where. The oracle who has glimpsed even a yard of a particular person's thread can, for a day, feel when that person is spending their fortune in a way that flows with their thread rather than against it — and, feeling it, can reinforce it.*

* **Skill:** Tactics
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 2 (Piercing 1 on Complication)
* **Duration:** 24 hours
* **Category:** Blessing spell

**Spell Effect:** The oracle designates one ally within Close range. For the duration, whenever that ally spends a Fortune point, the oracle rolls 1 die: on an effect result (6 on a d6, or the equivalent in the 2d12 system), the Fortune point is returned to the ally.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the duration extends by 24 hours.
* For 2 Success Levels, the oracle rolls 2 dice instead of 1 (the Fortune is returned if either produces an effect result).

---

### **Invoke the Parcae**

*The three Fates are older than the Olympians. They were older than the gods before the gods; they will be older than the gods after. The oracle who invokes them is not requesting assistance. They are acknowledging a relationship that the Fates have always been willing to honour, for their own reasons.*

* **Skill:** Manipulation
* **Difficulty:** Very Hard
* **Cost:** 4 (Stun on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Blessing or Curse spell

**Spell Effect:** As a reaction to any skill test being made by a character within Close range, the oracle invokes the Parcae and may choose one of the following interventions:
- **Critical Success**: Alter one d12 in the roll to show 1, generating 2 successes on that die.
- **Complication**: Alter one d12 in the roll to show its maximum value, generating a Complication.
- **Re-Roll**: Force the active character to reroll all dice in their pool.

The target of the intervention gains the scar **"The Fates Must Be Balanced"**. While this scar persists, the target's Complication range increases by 1. The scar is removed when the oracle rolls a Complication on any test.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the oracle may choose two of the listed effects simultaneously.
* For 2 Success Levels, the oracle may react to a test within Medium range rather than Close.
* For 3 Success Levels, the oracle may react to a test within Long range.

---

### **Prescience**

*The future is not fixed. But it is *consistent*. A careful observer of the present can identify the consistencies — the places where only one thing can happen next, the intersections of several threads where the result is overdetermined. The oracle calls this working "merely paying attention," which is either humility or the most accurate possible description.*

* **Skill:** Observation
* **Difficulty:** Hard
* **Cost:** 3 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** until end of scene
* **Category:** Divination spell

**Spell Effect:** The oracle glimpses the near future and attunes to its flow. Once per scene, before any character acts, the oracle may declare the result of one d12 in any roll they can observe — the declared result stands regardless of what the die actually shows. This declaration must be made before any dice are picked up.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the oracle may declare two dice rather than one.
* For 2 Success Levels, the ability also applies to rolls out of sight but within Medium range.

---

### **Seiðr Binding**

*The vǫlva does not kill with weapons. She sits on her high seat and touches the thread of the person she has been asked to address, and she gives it a small tug in a direction it did not intend to go, and the thread's owner spends the rest of the day walking slightly sideways from where they thought they were going.*

* **Skill:** Manipulation
* **Difficulty:** Difficult, opposed by target's WIL + Resilience
* **Cost:** 4 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** until end of scene
* **Category:** Control spell

**Spell Effect:** The oracle reaches into the fate-threads of a target within Close range and introduces a small systematic misdirection. The target suffers −1 to all skill tests for the duration as fate ceases to cooperate with their intentions.

**Success Levels:**
* For 1 Success Level, the penalty increases by 1.
* For 2 Success Levels, the effect extends to Medium range.
* For 3 Success Levels, the duration extends to 24 hours.

---

### **Web of Wyrd**

*Wyrd is not destiny in the deterministic sense. It is more like a debt-ledger — a running account of what has been done, what has been promised, what has been taken without payment. The oracle who perceives the Web of Wyrd sees exactly who owes what to whom, and this information is almost always immediately actionable.*

* **Skill:** Tactics
* **Difficulty:** Hard
* **Cost:** 5 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Manifestation spell

**Spell Effect:** The oracle perceives the threads of fate connecting all creatures within Close range, seeing one secret fact about each character's near future (one sentence, per the GM's discretion). This information is true and specific — not symbolic. Common examples: "Within three days, this person will make a decision that ends a friendship" or "The coin this person carries was stolen from a man who is looking for it."

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the range extends to Medium.
* For 2 Success Levels, the oracle may ask one additional follow-up question per affected creature (yes/no only).

## Druidic Tradition

Druidic magic is rooted in a faith and legend cycle that emphasises a deep connection to the land, a deep reverence for the knowledge of the natural world, and a certainty that the boundary between the visible world and the world of spirits is not a wall but a membrane that the properly initiated can move through at will. The Druids of Roman Britain were massacred on Anglesey in 61 AD; the Druids of Gaul were suppressed; the Druids of Hibernia — Ireland — were not suppressed, and they survived and developed, and in 500 AD the *filid* of the Irish kingdoms and the *derwydd* of the Welsh successor-kingdoms carry a tradition that is both old and vigorously alive. Its mythology names the Tuatha Dé Danann as the gods of the tradition — beings who were defeated by mortal invaders, withdrew to the *Sídhe* (the hollow hills), and now offer their power to initiated priests in exchange for worship.

**Practitioners:** Called *druid*, *filid* (Irish poet-priests), or *derwydd* (Welsh). Found in Hibernia, the Welsh kingdoms, and pockets of Hibernian-influenced practice in Dalriada (western Scotland) and Armorica.

**Caster Type:** Traditional only.

**Focus:** A staff of sacred wood (oak or yew, cut at midsummer with a bronze blade) or a bundle of sacred herbs and mistletoe, freshly gathered.

---

### **Bounties of the Dagda**

*The Dagda's cauldron never runs empty. Whatever is taken from it, it refills. The druid who invokes this principle is not performing a trick with matter — they are reminding the pattern of the world that abundance is the default state, and injury is a deviation from the pattern.*

* **Skill:** Medicae
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 4 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Ward spell

**Spell Effect:** All allied creatures within Close range immediately remove Stress equal to the druid's power.

**Success Levels:**
* For 1 Success Level, all defeated allies within range are restored from defeat.
* For 2 Success Levels, each affected ally also removes one injury.
* For 2 Success Levels, range extends to Medium.

---

### **Cyclone of Cernunnos**

*The Lord of Wild Things does not move through the forest. The forest moves through him. When a druid invokes this principle as a weapon, the result is not metaphorical — the wind arrives, and it is annoyed.*

* **Skill:** Survival
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 4 (Piercing 1 on Complication)
* **Duration:** rounds equal to power
* **Category:** Summoning spell

**Spell Effect:** A cyclone manifests in a zone within Medium range. On its own turn each round, it inflicts power + 1 physical Stress with Piercing 1 and Stun on all creatures within Close range of it, then moves to an adjacent zone. Supernatural creatures also suffer Vicious from the cyclone's touch. If the druid is within Medium range, they may direct the cyclone's movement; otherwise it moves as directed by the GM (who spends 1 Doom to move it).

**Flawed:** The cyclone manifests without any directional compulsion — the GM may move it freely without spending Doom.

**Success Levels:**
* Per Success Level, the cyclone inflicts +1 Stress per hit.

---

### **Gaze of Balor**

*The Fomorian king Balor had an eye that killed everything it looked at. The eye is the tradition's most direct attack — the evil eye inherited through the Tuatha Dé's victory over Balor, kept in their gift-hoard, lent to initiates who have sufficient standing to handle it without going blind themselves.*

* **Skill:** Manipulation
* **Difficulty:** Difficult, opposed by target's WIL + Resilience
* **Cost:** 5 (Stun on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** A single enemy within Medium range suffers power + 2 mental Stress with the Intense effect.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, add the Drain effect.
* For 2 Success Levels, add Persistent 6.

---

### **Gift of Arduinna**

*Arduinna is the goddess of the Ardennes forest, the wild boar at her side, the path through the wilderness her particular gift. The druid who invokes her does not receive the wilderness — they receive the knowledge of how to move through it without the wilderness noticing.*

* **Skill:** Survival
* **Difficulty:** Hard
* **Cost:** 4 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** rounds equal to power ÷ number of targets (round down)
* **Category:** Blessing spell

**Spell Effect:** The druid targets up to power characters (the druid themselves plus allies within Close range). For the duration, each affected character may move 1 extra zone and take 1 free minor action per turn.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels (per affected ally), that ally may take one extra major action on one of their turns during the duration (maximum once per character).

---

### **Mistletoe Ward**

*The mistletoe grows without touching the earth. It is betwixt — neither tree nor ground-plant, neither the realm of the living nor the realm of the dead. The druids have used this betweenness as a ward since before their tradition had a name for what it was doing.*

* **Skill:** Medicae
* **Difficulty:** Challenging
* **Cost:** 2 (Drain on Complication)
* **Duration:** 24 hours
* **Category:** Ward spell

**Spell Effect:** The druid anoints a target with sacred mistletoe preparations. Until the next sunrise, the target has Cover resistance equal to the druid's power against the next instance of Stress they would suffer. Once triggered, the ward is exhausted.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the ward may be applied to two targets simultaneously.
* For 2 Success Levels, the target also gains Morale resistance equal to power against mental Stress (applied to the same first instance — the ward covers both simultaneously).

---

### **Voice of the Land**

*Everything that happens leaves a mark on the place where it happens. Stones remember weight; roots remember shadow; water remembers shape. The druid who knows how to ask finds that the land is a more reliable witness than most people, and considerably more patient.*

* **Skill:** Observation
* **Difficulty:** Difficult
* **Cost:** 3 (Doom on Complication)
* **Duration:** Instant
* **Category:** Divination spell

**Spell Effect:** The druid communes with the spirit of the land, placing their hands on stone, root, or water. They may ask up to 2 yes/no questions about events that occurred within Long range in the last 24 hours. The land answers truthfully within the limits of what it can perceive — it cannot report conversations held indoors unless a window was open, but it can report the number of footsteps, the direction of blood, or the passage of something that walked without touching the ground.

**Success Levels:**
* For 2 Success Levels, the druid may ask one open (non-yes/no) question instead.
* For 2 Success Levels, the time window extends to 7 days.

> ## ☽ GM Sidebar: The Black Speech
>
> *"To learn even a syllable is to leave a door open behind your eyes."*
> — Attributed to Iamblichus, possibly apocryphal; cited in the Concord's founding charter
>
> The Black Speech is not a magical tradition. It has no initiatory lineage, no spellbook, no patron, and no practitioners who survive long enough to be called practitioners. It is the language in which the Outer Ones — those entities that exist outside the seven spheres, outside the cosmology of named powers entirely — think and occasionally, catastrophically, communicate. It is not structured the way human languages are structured. It does not have grammar in any recognizable sense. What it has are *implications*, and the implications arrive directly in the nervous system of anyone who successfully processes even a syllable.
>
> A character may encounter fragments of the Black Speech in several ways: inscribed on the walls of a pre-human ruin, transcribed (badly, madly) in the notes of a scholar who did not survive the transcription, or whispered by an entity of sufficient antiquity to remember when the Outer Ones were the only speakers of anything. Any character who attempts to *translate* or *speak* a fragment — a Research or Dabbler approaching it as a text, a Traditional caster trying to force the sounds through their tradition's framework — immediately suffers 1d3 Sanity loss and generates 2 Doom, regardless of whether they succeed or fail. The knowledge cannot be unlearned. The door is open.
>
> **Mechanically:** No spell or tradition effect produced by the Black Speech is written in this book. The Concord's Seven Seals explicitly prohibit teaching, speaking, or writing the Black Speech — knowledge of even a single phrase is grounds for summary execution under Seal law. A character who is discovered to possess Black Speech knowledge will find the Concord's response rapid, thorough, and not concerned with their continued survival.
>
> **For the GM:** The Black Speech is a plot device, not a character resource. If a player character acquires it, they should find it functionally useless — no spellbook, no tradition, no way to activate it deliberately — but cosmically dangerous in the way that carrying a live coal in one's hand is dangerous. Something has noticed the open door. Something is deciding whether to use it.
