# Resting and Recovery  
## Short Rest  
A Short Rest requires 1 hour of uninterrupted rest. At the end of a Short Rest:  
- All Spent Stamina is recovered.  
- 1 Vitality point is recovered.  
- 1 Resolve point is recovered.  
## Long Rest  
A Long Rest requires 8 hours of uninterrupted rest.  At the end of a Long Rest:  
- All Spent Stamina is recovered.  
- Resolve is recovered by 1d4 \+ WIL.  
- Vitality is recovered by 1d4 \+ CON.  
- Attribute Drain heals at a rate of 1 point per drained attribute (see Attribute Drain below).  
>**First Aid**. A character may tend to an injured ally with a Hard Reason–Medicine (Field Treatment) check requiring 10 minutes. On a success, the patient regains 1 additional Vitality at the end of the Long Rest. A creature can benefit from this care only once per day.  
## Full Rest  
A Full Rest requires 24 hours in a safe location, with at least 6 hours spent sleeping. The remaining time may be used for downtime activities.  
At the end of a Full Rest:  
- All Spent Stamina is recovered.  
- All Spent Resolve is recovered.  
- All Spent Power is recovered.  
- 1 Critical Wound is healed.  
- Attribute Drain heals at a rate of 3 points per drained attribute.  
>**First Aid**. A character may provide additional care with a Hard Reason–Medicine (Field Treatment) check requiring 10 minutes. On a success, the patient heals 1 additional Critical Wound at the end of the Full Rest. A creature can benefit from this care only once per day.

### Attribute Drain

Some diseases, poisons, curses, and magical effects do not deal Stress but instead reduce one of a character's eight attributes by a number of points. This reduction is **Attribute Drain**.

- A drained attribute is reduced for all purposes — derived stats, skill bonuses, and resistance — until the drain is healed.
- An attribute cannot be reduced below 0 by Attribute Drain. A character whose Constitution reaches 0 falls unconscious; whose Willpower reaches 0 falls insensible; whose Reason reaches 0 cannot reason. The GM decides further effects in keeping with the fiction.
- Attribute Drain heals at the rates listed in Long Rest and Full Rest above, unless the source specifies otherwise.
- Attribute Drain caused by an active disease does not heal through rest. The lost points return only after the character has fully recovered from the disease itself.

### Fatigue  
When your character suffers fatigue, you temporarily reduce their Stamina, Vitality and Resolve by 1 for each point of Fatigue suffered. If their maximum of any of them are reduced to 0, they are defeated— collapsing from exhaustion, deprivation, or despair. 

Your character can remove fatigue with a Challenging Might \+ Resilience or Willpower \+ Resilience test, once they have removed themself from any sources of fatigue. For example, if they took fatigue from sleep deprivation and starvation, getting a hot meal and a good night’s sleep allows your character to try to remove that fatigue. Passing the test removes 1 point of fatigue, plus 1 more for each Success Level.
