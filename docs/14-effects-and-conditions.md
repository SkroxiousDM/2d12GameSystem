
**Drain:** The target suffers 1 Fatigue for each unsoaked stress.

**Intense:** If the attack inflicts a wound and a success level is spent, the attack inflicts one additional wound.

**Knockdown:** The attack or hazard causes the target to stumble or fall. If the number of success levels spent equals or exceeds the target's Athletics skill rank, the target is knocked prone.

**Persistent X:** The attack or hazard has a lingering effect. If the attack generates one or more success levels spent, the target rolls X dice at the start of each of their turns for a number of rounds equal to the success levels spent, suffering any stress generated. These rolls do not trigger additional qualities or effects. Against objects rather than characters, the Persistent X effect begins immediately.

**Snare:** The attack or hazard entangles and binds the target, who may take no actions other than attempting to break free. Breaking free requires a skill test — typically Might + Athletics for physical snares — with a Difficulty equal to the number of success levels spent. Breaking free consumes the character's major action, though minor actions remain available afterward.

**Stun:** The attack or hazard leaves the target momentarily unable to act. If the number of success levels spent equals or exceeds the target's Resilience skill rank, the target may take no actions on their next turn.

**Vicious:** The attack or hazard is especially devastating. For each success level spent, it adds +1 to the total stress inflicted.


***

By spending 2 Success Levels as part of an attack, you may impose one condition on your target. Conditions are not tied to any specific weapon or spell, but rather to how you employ them in combat.

**Beat Back:** Your attack drives your foe off balance. You may either move the target anywhere within the same zone or force them to lose their Guard.

**Bleeding:** You open a vicious wound in your target. Until the target takes the Catch Breath major action, it suffers +1 point of physical stress at the end of each of its turns.

**Disarmed:** You knock a weapon or item from your target's hands. The item falls somewhere within the same zone. The target may spend 3 Fortune or 3 Doom to prevent this.

**Frightened:** Your attack is particularly brutal. The target immediately loses 1 point of Morale resistance, if it has any. If the target has no Morale resistance, it suffers +1 point of mental stress instead.

**Prone:** You knock your target to the ground. The Difficulty of melee attacks targeting a prone character is reduced by 1, as is the Difficulty of ranged attacks made by a character within Reach of the prone character. Standing from prone requires a minor action.

**Sundered:** Your attack damages your target's armour. The target immediately loses 1 point of Armour resistance, if it has any. If the target has no Armour resistance, it suffers +1 point of physical stress instead.

# Conditions

### Ablaze
*You are on fire.*

- **Ablaze (Minor):** You suffer 1d5 stress at the start of each of your turns, ignoring armour.
- **Ablaze (Major):** You suffer 1d10 stress at the start of each of your turns, ignoring armour.

While Ablaze, you automatically fail all Stealth tests. Unless otherwise specified, you may remove the Ablaze condition by dropping Prone and using a major action to make a successful Athletics test (Difficulty 1).

***

### Bleeding
*You are losing blood.*

- **Bleeding (Minor):** You suffer 1 stress at the end of each of your turns, ignoring armour.
- **Bleeding (Major):** You suffer 3 stress at the end of each of your turns, ignoring armour.

If you exceed your wound threshold due to Bleeding, you suffer a Critical Wound as normal. Once this occurs you no longer suffer stress from Bleeding, but cannot recover wounds until the Bleeding is treated. Bleeding not caused by a Critical Wound may be treated with a Medicine test (Difficulty 1) or through use of a Physician's Kit.

***

### Blinded
*You cannot see.*

- You may only succeed on tests that rely on sight — such as Observation (Sight) and ranged attack tests — on a natural roll of 01–05.
- You have Disadvantage on all Melee and Agility (Dodge) tests.

Unless otherwise specified, the Blinded condition ends after 1d10 rounds.

***

### Deafened
*You cannot hear.*

- You may only succeed on tests that rely on hearing — such as Observation (Hearing) — on a natural roll of 01–05.

Unless otherwise specified, the Deafened condition ends after 1d10 minutes. There is rarely a means to end either Blinded or Deafened sooner.

***

### Fatigued
each point of Fatigue reduces Vitalty, Resolve, and Stamina, by 1 point each

***

### Frightened
*You are gripped by fear.*

- **Frightened (Minor):** Heightened senses grant you Advantage on Observation and Insight tests. However, you have Disadvantage on all tests that involve directly confronting the source of your fear.
- **Frightened (Major):** You are terrified. You must flee from the source of your fear by the most direct route available, stopping only to open doors or otherwise escape your current situation.

Unless otherwise specified, at the end of each round you may make a Challenging Resilience test  to remove this condition.

***

### Incapacitated
*You cannot move or act.*

- You cannot take any actions or move.
- You cannot defend yourself. Melee attacks made against you automatically score a Critical Hit.

***

### Poisoned
*You are sick or unwell.*

- **Poisoned (Minor):** You have Disadvantage on Might and Fortitude tests. The maximum success levels you may achieve on any test equals your Fortitude rank.
- **Poisoned (Major):** You immediately become Prone and Incapacitated.

Unless a duration is specified, the Poisoned condition lasts 1d5 hours. Most poisons can be treated with a Challenging Medicine test  and a Physician's Kit. Particularly dangerous substances increase to Difficult or higher. Truly lethal concoctions may require a Very Hard test — and once their duration expires, the victim may simply die.

***

### Prone
*You are on the ground.*

- You may only move by crawling unless you spend your Move action to stand up.
- You have Disadvantage on all Melee tests.
- Characters attacking you from within Reach have Advantage on their attack tests.
- Characters attacking you from beyond Reach have Disadvantage on their attack tests.

***

### Restrained
*You are unable to move normally.*

- **Restrained (Minor):** You cannot take Move actions. You have Disadvantage on all tests involving movement, including Athletics, Agility, Melee, and ranged attack tests.
- **Restrained (Major):** You become Incapacitated.

Removing this condition typically requires a test appropriate to the situation — such as an Athletics test to break free of physical bonds, or a Thievery (Lock Picking) test to escape manacles.

***

### Stunned
*You are dazed and disoriented.*

- **Stunned (Minor):** Your Alacrity is reduced to 1.
- **Stunned (Major):** As Minor, and you have Disadvantage on all tests.

Unless a duration is specified, the Stunned condition lasts 1d5 rounds. An ally may spend their major action attempting to rouse you, allowing you to make a Challenging Resilience (Fortitude) test to end the condition immediately.

***

### Unconscious
*You have lost consciousness.*

- You immediately drop anything you are holding, fall Prone, and become Incapacitated.
- Any character within Reach armed with a weapon may kill you without needing to make a test.

Here are the Item Qualities and Flaws rewritten in the 2d12 house style:

***

# Item Qualities

Each Item Quality doubles the item's listed price and reduces its Availability by one step. If a piece of equipment has more Qualities than Flaws, it is regarded as fine craftsmanship and may suitably impress — or inspire covetous envy in — those who see it.

***

**Lightweight**
This item employs lighter materials or slimline construction to reduce its weight substantially. Reduce the item's Encumbrance by 1. If the item already has an Encumbrance of 0, the GM may instead grant it the Subtle quality.

***

**Master Crafted**
These are legendary works of art, handed down across generations as prized relics. Master Crafted weapons, tools, and gear grant +1 success level on all relevant tests. Master Crafted armour gains +2 Armour. Master Crafted items always cost a minimum of 500 denarii.

***

**Ornamental**
This item is adorned with fine carvings, rare materials, or other aesthetic embellishments. This quality may be taken multiple times, each time increasing the item's impressiveness. At the GM's discretion, a suitably resplendent item may grant +1 success level on Manipulation tests with characters who would appreciate its artistry and style.

***

**Durable**
Built with robust materials and redundant construction, this item keeps working even under extreme stress. If the item is a weapon, it gains the Reliable quality. Otherwise, the first time this item would lose a point of Armour or otherwise cease to function, it continues to operate normally instead.

***

## Item Flaws

Each Item Flaw halves the item's listed price (rounded up) and improves its Availability by one step. Exotic equipment is never made more available by Item Flaws — it remains prohibitively difficult to obtain regardless of quality. If a piece of equipment has more Flaws than Qualities, it is regarded as poor workmanship and may invite derision from those who notice.

Many unscrupulous traders attempt to conceal flaws in the items they sell. Short of discovering the hard way, a successful Academia (Evaluation) test is required to identify any Item Flaws before purchase.

***

**Bulky**
Crafted from heavy materials or lacking refinement, this item is more cumbersome than it ought to be. Increase the item's Encumbrance by 1. Bulky items lose the Subtle quality.

***

**Shoddy**
Worn out or made from inferior materials, this item is difficult to use effectively. Shoddy weapons and tools suffer −1 success level on all relevant tests. Shoddy armour has −2 Armour, to a minimum of 1.

***

**Ugly**
This item looks as though it was dragged from a midden heap — rusted, cracked, or held together with strips of rotting leather. At the GM's discretion, a suitably wretched item may impose −1 success level on Manipulation tests with characters to whom appearance and quality matter.

***

**Unreliable**
Whether the result of poor craftsmanship or simple bad luck, this item always seems to fail at the worst possible moment. Unreliable weapons gain the Unstable quality. If you suffer a Critical Hit to a location protected by Unreliable armour, that armour is immediately destroyed. Other Unreliable equipment breaks whenever you Fumble while using it.

***

Here are the Weapon and Armour Traits rewritten in the 2d12 house style:

***

# Weapon & Armour Traits

Many weapons and pieces of armour possess unusual qualities known as Traits. Traits with a numerical rating have variable effects as described in each entry.

***

**Blast**
When attacking with a Blast weapon, target a zone within range and make a single Fighting (Thrown) or Ranged test, as appropriate. All characters in the target zone may use their reaction to make an opposed Agility (Dodge) test against your result. Those who fail suffer stress equal to the weapon's damage plus the difference in success levels. Characters in the same zone may therefore suffer very different outcomes.

> *Simplified Explosions: When multiple characters are caught in a Blast, the GM may make a single opposed test against all targets sharing the same Agility (Dodge) rank, applying the result to all of them simultaneously.*

***

**Close**
This ranged weapon may be used at Immediate range without incurring Disadvantage on the attack test.

***

**Defensive**
You have Advantage on opposed tests when defending against melee attacks with this weapon.

***

**Heavy (Rating)**
Heavy equipment requires a Might rank equal to the rating to wield effectively. If you do not meet this requirement:

- Heavy armour imposes Disadvantage on Agility tests.
- Heavy melee weapons impose Disadvantage on Fighting tests.
- Heavy ranged weapons impose Disadvantage on Ranged tests.

Instead of moving, a character may brace a Heavy ranged weapon against a suitable terrain feature. While braced, the Heavy trait is ignored until the character next takes a Move action.

***

**Ineffective**
This weapon struggles to penetrate armour. All Armour values are doubled against attacks from Ineffective weapons.

***

**Inflict (Condition)**
If an attack with this weapon causes unsoaked stress, the target suffers the listed condition. If no damage rating is listed, the attack need only hit to apply the condition. Unless otherwise specified, the condition lasts for one round. Conditions have default durations and means of removal, but specific equipment may stipulate otherwise.

***

**Loud**
This equipment is noticeably noisy. A weapon with the Loud trait alerts all characters within Long range when used to make an attack. Armour with the Loud trait imposes Disadvantage on all Stealth tests.

***

**Penetrating (Rating)**
This weapon's attacks ignore Armour equal to its rating.

***

**Reach**
You may attack any character within your zone with a Reach weapon without becoming Engaged with the target. When you are already Engaged, you have Disadvantage on Fighting tests to defend yourself with a Reach weapon.

***

**Reliable**
When you Fumble while attacking with this weapon, roll 1d12. On a result of 5 or higher, ignore the Fumble result entirely.

***

**Rend (Rating)**
When an attack with this weapon inflicts one or more wounds, the outermost armour on the hit location is reduced by the Rend rating until repaired. Armour may be reduced multiple times in this way; if reduced to 0 it is completely destroyed.

***

**Shield (Rating)**
When you brace with this shield against incoming attacks, you gain additional Armour equal to the rating.

***

**Subtle**
This weapon or piece of armour is quiet and easily concealed.

- Subtle weapons are silent in use and easy to hide, imposing Disadvantage on Observation tests to detect their use or locate them on your person.
- Subtle armour is not obviously defensive at first glance, imposing Disadvantage on Observation tests to determine whether you are wearing it. Subtle armour may also be worn beneath other armour that lacks the Subtle trait; when worn this way, add both Armour values together.

***

**Thrown (Range)**
This melee weapon may be thrown as a ranged attack. The range at which it can be thrown is listed in parentheses.

***

**Two-Handed**
This weapon requires two hands to wield.

***

**Unstable**
When you Fumble while attacking with this weapon, on a result of 5 or higher on the Fumble table the weapon self-destructs, dealing its damage to the arm wielding it — or to a random arm if the weapon is Two-handed.
