# Equipment

This chapter is the master reference for how gear works in the 2d12 system. It covers item categories, encumbrance, the Restriction and Availability ratings used elsewhere in the rules, the Quality/Flaw economy, and the full list of Weapon & Armour Traits. Specific items live in:

- [15.1 — Armour](15.1-armour.md): helmets, body armor, shields.
- [15.2 — Weapons](15.2-weapons.md): melee and ranged weapons, ammunition.
- [15.3 — Adventuring Gear](15.3-adventuring-gear.md): kits, tools, light, consumables.
- [15.4 — Wealth, Trade, and Maintenance](15.4-wealth-and-trade.md): coinage, purchasing, repair, the economy of play.

## Encumbrance Categories

Every item belongs to one of four weight categories. Encumbrance is paid in **invested Stamina** (see Chapter 4).

| Category | Stamina Invested | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Heavy** | 2 | Lorica segmentata, two-handed axe, ballista bolt quiver, sealed cask. |
| **Standard** | 1 | Gladius and scabbard, leather satchel, lantern, week of rations. |
| **Light** | 1 per 2 items | Pugio, dagger, scroll case, waterskin, flask of oil. |
| **Tiny** | 0 | Coin, ring, sealing wax, lockpick, pinch of salt. |

**Coins** scale across categories: 100 coins count as a Light item, 200 as Standard, 400 as Heavy. A character carrying their wealth on their person is encumbered by it.

**Modifying encumbrance.** The Lightweight quality reduces a category by one step; Bulky raises it. An item cannot drop below Tiny.

## Restriction Rating

Every weapon, piece of armor, and many gear items carry a **Restriction rating** from 0 to 5. This rating represents how readily a character may acquire the item by law, custom, and circulation. It is referenced in starting equipment, magical components (Chapter 3), and the purchasing rules in 15.4.

| Restriction | Description | Example Items |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Mundane, common, sold openly. | Walking stick, sling, rope, lamp oil, common knife. |
| 1 | Common but regulated by guild or custom. | Short bow, padded cloth, javelin, hunting axe. |
| 2 | Restricted to soldiers, citizens, or tradesfolk of the right station. | Gladius, hasta, leather armor, scutum. |
| 3 | Restricted to standing military, vouched citizens, or licensed retainers. | Mail (lorica hamata), helm with face guard, recurve bow, spatha. |
| 4 | Restricted to elites: officers, magistrates, equestrians, knights. | Plate cuirass (lorica musculata), lamellar, falx, complex traps. |
| 5 | Forbidden or rare even among elites; possession is itself a crime or a marvel. | Cheirosiphōn, magical foci, war engines, foreign and exotic armaments. |

The GM is the final arbiter of whether a setting region treats a given item at its listed Restriction. The northern frontier, the eastern marches, and the deep wilds may relax or invert these tables.

### How Restriction Interacts With Other Systems

- **Starting equipment.** Cultures, Backgrounds, and Roles (Chapter 2) name a maximum Restriction the character may freely choose from. Many list "Restriction 3 or lower."
- **Magic.** Spell and ritual components reference Restriction as a difficulty (Chapter 3). A high-Restriction component is a meaningful gating cost.
- **Purchasing.** See *Availability* below and the full purchasing rules in 15.4.

## Availability

Restriction is what the item *is*; **Availability** is whether the *seller in front of you* has it. Availability is set scene by scene by the GM, modified by location and the merchant.

| Availability Step | Find by | Typical Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Common | Any market | Same day | Restriction 0–1; everyday wares. |
| Uncommon | Town market or specialist | 1d3 days | Restriction 2; ask around. |
| Rare | Cities, guilds, or commissions | 1d6+1 days | Restriction 3; reputation may matter. |
| Very Rare | Capital, specific person, or commission | weeks | Restriction 4; must be earned. |
| Unique | One source in the campaign, if any | Adventure-scale | Restriction 5; treat as a quest. |

Item Qualities (below) reduce Availability by one step per Quality. Item Flaws improve Availability by one step per Flaw — **except for Restriction 5 (Exotic) items**, which remain difficult to obtain regardless of how poorly made they are.

## Improvised Equipment

Characters lacking the right tool may often still attempt the action with an improvised substitute.

- **Improvised tool.** A test that normally requires a kit may be made without it at +1 step of Difficulty.
- **Improvised weapon.** Any heavy or pointed object may be wielded as a weapon. Treat as a Light weapon with Damage 2 (resolved 2d3-2 per Damage Resolution) and no traits. The improvised weapon breaks on any Fumble.
- **Improvised armor.** Layers of cloth, a wineskin under the tunic, or a board strapped to the arm may grant Armour 1 to a single hit location, lasting one scene. After one strike, treat as Sundered.

Improvised gear is not a substitute for the right tool. It is a way to avoid telling the players they cannot try.

## Item Qualities

Each Item Quality **doubles** the item's listed price and **reduces** its Availability by one step (it becomes harder to find). If a piece of equipment has more Qualities than Flaws, it is regarded as fine craftsmanship and may suitably impress — or inspire covetous envy in — those who see it.

***

**Lightweight.** This item employs lighter materials or slimline construction to reduce its weight substantially. Reduce the item's Encumbrance category by one step. If the item is already Tiny, the GM may instead grant it the Subtle quality.

***

**Master Crafted.** These are legendary works of art, handed down across generations as prized relics. Master Crafted weapons, tools, and gear grant +1 success level on all relevant tests. Master Crafted armour gains +2 Armour. Master Crafted items always cost a minimum of 500 denarii.

***

**Ornamental.** This item is adorned with fine carvings, rare materials, or other aesthetic embellishments. This quality may be taken multiple times, each time increasing the item's impressiveness. At the GM's discretion, a suitably resplendent item may grant +1 success level on Manipulation tests with characters who would appreciate its artistry and style.

***

**Durable.** Built with robust materials and redundant construction, this item keeps working even under extreme stress. If the item is a weapon, it gains the Reliable trait. Otherwise, the first time this item would lose a point of Armour or otherwise cease to function, it continues to operate normally instead.

***

**Balanced.** *(weapons only)* The weapon's distribution of mass is exact. The wielder gains Advantage on the first Fighting test made with it each combat round.

***

**Versatile.** *(weapons only)* The weapon may be wielded effectively one-handed or two-handed. While wielded two-handed, it gains the Penetrating 1 trait or +1 Damage rating (one extra d3, with the subtraction increased by 1 to match), wielder's choice declared on attack.

## Item Flaws

Each Item Flaw **halves** the item's listed price (rounded up) and **improves** its Availability by one step (it becomes easier to find). Exotic equipment (Restriction 5) is never made more available by Item Flaws — it remains prohibitively difficult to obtain regardless of quality. If a piece of equipment has more Flaws than Qualities, it is regarded as poor workmanship and may invite derision from those who notice.

Many unscrupulous traders attempt to conceal flaws in the items they sell. Short of discovering the hard way, a successful **Academia (Evaluation)** test against a Difficulty set by the trader's Subterfuge is required to identify any Item Flaws before purchase.

***

**Bulky.** Crafted from heavy materials or lacking refinement, this item is more cumbersome than it ought to be. Increase the item's Encumbrance category by one step. Bulky items lose the Subtle trait.

***

**Shoddy.** Worn out or made from inferior materials, this item is difficult to use effectively. Shoddy weapons and tools suffer −1 success level on all relevant tests. Shoddy armour has −2 Armour, to a minimum of 1.

***

**Ugly.** This item looks as though it was dragged from a midden heap — rusted, cracked, or held together with strips of rotting leather. At the GM's discretion, a suitably wretched item may impose −1 success level on Manipulation tests with characters to whom appearance and quality matter.

***

**Unreliable.** Whether the result of poor craftsmanship or simple bad luck, this item always seems to fail at the worst possible moment. Unreliable weapons gain the Unstable trait. If you suffer a Critical Hit to a location protected by Unreliable armour, that armour is immediately destroyed. Other Unreliable equipment breaks whenever you Fumble while using it.

***

**Mismatched.** *(armour only)* This piece was repaired or assembled from parts that do not perfectly fit. It cannot be worn beneath or above other armor on overlapping locations.

***

**Worn.** The item has seen long use without proper maintenance. The first time in a scene that you would gain a Critical Success with it, treat the roll as an ordinary Success instead.

## Weapon & Armour Traits

Many weapons and pieces of armour possess unusual qualities known as Traits. Traits with a numerical rating have variable effects as described in each entry. Where this section repeats Trait language used in 15.1 and 15.2, this chapter is the authority.

***

**Blast.** When attacking with a Blast weapon, target a zone within range and make a single Fighting (Thrown) or Ranged test, as appropriate. All characters in the target zone may use their reaction to make an opposed Agility (Dodge) test against your result. Those who fail suffer damage as normal (roll the weapon's damage per Ch 15 Damage Resolution, adding each defender's individual SL deficit). Characters in the same zone may therefore suffer very different outcomes.

> *Simplified Explosions: When multiple characters are caught in a Blast, the GM may make a single opposed test against all targets sharing the same Reflexes (Dodge) rank, applying the result to all of them simultaneously.*

***

**Close.** This ranged weapon may be used at Immediate range without incurring Disadvantage on the attack test.

***

**Defensive.** You have Advantage on opposed tests when defending against melee attacks with this weapon.

***

**Heavy (Rating).** Heavy equipment requires a Might rank equal to the rating to wield effectively. If you do not meet this requirement:

- Heavy armour imposes Disadvantage on Agility tests.
- Heavy melee weapons impose Disadvantage on Fighting tests.
- Heavy ranged weapons impose Disadvantage on Ranged tests.

Instead of moving, a character may brace a Heavy ranged weapon against a suitable terrain feature. While braced, the Heavy trait is ignored until the character next takes a Move action.

***

**Ineffective.** This weapon struggles to penetrate armour. All Armour values are doubled against attacks from Ineffective weapons.

***

**Inflict (Condition).** If an attack with this weapon causes unsoaked stress, the target suffers the listed condition. If no Stress rating is listed, the attack need only hit to apply the condition. Unless otherwise specified, the condition lasts for one round. Conditions have default durations and means of removal, but specific equipment may stipulate otherwise. (See 91 — Conditions Appendix.)

***

**Loud.** This equipment is noticeably noisy. A weapon with the Loud trait alerts all characters within Long range when used to make an attack. Armour with the Loud trait imposes Disadvantage on all Stealth tests.

***

**Penetrating (Rating).** This weapon's attacks ignore Armour equal to its rating.

***

**Pious.** *(magical / spiritual)* The item is consecrated. It deals +1 stress against creatures of the Profane (demons, undead, cult abominations); against other targets it functions as an ordinary item.

***

**Reach.** You may attack any character within your zone with a Reach weapon without becoming Engaged with the target. When you are already Engaged, you have Disadvantage on Fighting tests to defend yourself with a Reach weapon.

***

**Reliable.** When you Fumble while attacking with this weapon, roll 1d12. On a result of 5 or higher, ignore the Fumble result entirely.

***

**Rend (Rating).** When an attack with this weapon inflicts one or more wounds, the outermost armour on the hit location is reduced by the Rend rating until repaired. Armour may be reduced multiple times in this way; if reduced to 0 it is completely destroyed.

***

**Shield (Rating).** When you brace with this shield against incoming attacks, you gain additional Armour equal to the rating. See the Shields entry in 15.1 for the full effects of wielding a shield.

***

**Slow.** *(ranged)* This weapon requires a Reload action (1 Action) between each shot. A character may avoid this cost only by spending Stamina equal to the weapon's listed reload cost.

***

**Subtle.** This weapon or piece of armour is quiet and easily concealed.

- Subtle weapons are silent in use and easy to hide, imposing Disadvantage on Observation tests to detect their use or locate them on your person.
- Subtle armour is not obviously defensive at first glance, imposing Disadvantage on Observation tests to determine whether you are wearing it. Subtle armour may also be worn beneath other armour that lacks the Subtle trait; when worn this way, add both Armour values together.

***

**Thrown (Range).** This melee weapon may be thrown as a ranged attack. The range at which it can be thrown is listed in parentheses.

***

**Two-Handed.** This weapon requires two hands to wield.

***

**Unstable.** When you Fumble while attacking with this weapon, on a result of 5 or higher on the Fumble table the weapon self-destructs, dealing its Stress to the arm wielding it — or to a random arm if the weapon is Two-handed.

## Locations And Coverage

Armor protects specific **locations** that map onto the combat hit-location table (Chapter 11):

| Hit Roll | Location | Covered By |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | Head | Helmet, hood, cowl |
| 3 | Left Arm | Body armor with arms, manica, vambrace |
| 4 | Right Arm | Body armor with arms, manica, vambrace |
| 5–6 | Left Leg | Body armor with legs, greaves (knemides), splint chausses |
| 7–8 | Right Leg | Body armor with legs, greaves, splint chausses |
| 9–12 | Body | Cuirass, mail shirt, padded jack |

An attack with the **Penetrating** trait ignores Armour from the struck location only. **Rend** reduces the struck location's outermost armor. **Sundered** (a Condition) likewise affects the struck location.

When two pieces of armor cover the same location (e.g., mail under a leather jerkin), only the higher Armour value applies — *unless* one of them has the Subtle trait, which permits stacking (see Subtle, above).

## Item Damage And Breakage

Equipment is fragile under hard use. The following situations damage gear:

- **Fumble on a related test:** roll 1d12; on 1–4 the item gains the Worn flaw if it does not have it, or breaks if it does.
- **Sundered armor:** loses 1 Armour at the struck location until repaired.
- **Rent armor:** loses Armour equal to the Rend rating at the struck location until repaired.
- **Unstable weapon detonation:** the weapon is destroyed.
- **Unreliable armor critical hit:** the armor is destroyed.
- **Bleeding through cloth, oil through cracked flask:** GM call; usually one scene of degraded use.

Repair rules and costs are in 15.4 — Wealth, Trade, and Maintenance.

## Quick Reference: Reading An Item Block

Throughout 15.1 and 15.2 each item is presented in a table row with the following columns:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Name | The item's common name and (for setting clarity) Latin or vernacular form. |
| Damage / Armour | Weapon damage as a single integer N (see Damage Resolution below); armor as a flat reduction. |
| Hands | M = melee one-handed; M2 = melee two-handed; R = ranged one-handed; R2 = ranged two-handed. |
| Range | Melee: Engaged or Reach. Ranged: Immediate / Short / Medium / Long. |
| Encumbrance | Tiny / Light / Standard / Heavy. |
| Restriction | 0–5 per the table above. |
| Price | In denarii (see 15.4). Prices are baseline; quality and flaws modify. |
| Traits | Comma-separated. See the Traits section above. |

When a value is not specified for an item it is "—" or assumed at the default: damage 3, range Engaged, Restriction 1, Encumbrance Standard.

## Damage Resolution

Weapon damage is a single integer **N** printed on each item's row. To resolve damage on a successful hit:

1. **Take the weapon's Damage rating N.**
2. **Add the difference in Success Levels (SL)** between the attacker's roll and the defender's (or against the test's Difficulty if there is no opposing roll).
3. **Roll that many d3 and subtract the same number.** A final damage value of **N** is resolved as **NdN-N** (so Damage 4 = 4d3-4, Damage 6 = 6d3-6, and so on).
4. **Apply the relevant attribute bonus** (Might for melee, Insight for ranged, Willpower for mental — per Ch 4) as a flat addition to the total **after** the dice are rolled.
5. **Reduce the result by the target's Armour** at the struck hit location.
6. The remainder is suffered as Physical Stress against Vitality (Ch 11).

This dice expression has a minimum of 0 (it can never heal the target) and a maximum of 2N. The expected value is N, so the printed number is also the average damage delivered before SL, attributes, and armour.

> *Example.* Lucia attacks with a Spatha (Damage 4) and beats her foe by 2 SL. She rolls 6d3-6 = 7, adds her MIG 2 = 9, then subtracts the target's Body armor of 3, dealing 6 Stress to Vitality.
