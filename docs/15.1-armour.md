### Lorica Hamata (Mail)
Mail, including the Lorica Hamata worn by some legionaries, is a mesh made of interlocking rings of iron or other metals that provides good protection against slashing attacks. While mail has a vulnerability to Piercing weapons, it can still mean the difference between a minor wound and a deadly one. A coat of mail is labor-intensive to produce.
### Leather Armor
Leather armor is a light, flexible form of armor made from a plentiful resource. The leather is treated to increase its stiffness and durability, but it does not limit the wearer’s mobility.
### Lorica Segmentata (Laminar)
The armor of the Roman legions, lorica segmentata consists of a series of overlapping metal plates fastened together with internal leather straps. The plates of the metal cuirass move with the wearer, allowing for greater mobility than with fully rigid armors.
### Lorica Squamata (Scale)
Lorica squamata is a metal cuirass made up of metal scales of iron or brass sewn to a fabric backing. This scale armor was prominent during the days of the Republic, and it continues to see use throughout the Roman Empire.
### Lorica Musculata (Plate)
"muscular cuirass" The armor consisted of front and back plates fastened together at the shoulders and sides with inserted hinges or straps. When assembled, the cuirass had a barrel shape that roughly imitated the musculature of the male torso. Both plates had cutouts for the warrior's throat, arms, and waist. The lower part of the cuirass had a so-called bell-like shape due to its characteristic wide flaring. This shape allowed the warrior to sit without removing the armor. The cuirass itself was short and protected the warrior's torso only up to the waist.

## Shields
Wielding a shield takes one hand, and grants a number of benefits. 
- When you make an Opposed Test to defend against a melee attack you do not suffer Disadvantage if you are not wielding a melee weapon. 
- If you are wielding a melee weapon and a shield at the same time, your melee weapon gains the Defensive Trait.
- You gain Advantage on Opposed Athletics (Might) Tests to Defend a Zone.
- Once per round, when you are the target of a ranged attack from outside your Zone, you can brace yourself against incoming fire from the attacker’s Zone. If you do so, you gain bonus Armour equal to the Rating against that attack, and all subsequent attacks originating from that Zone until the beginning of your next turn. 
- If you use a shield to protect yourself from a melee or ranged attack which has the Rend Trait, the shield is destroyed. If the attack's Rend Trait was higher than the Rating, you take damage as normal. Otherwise the attack has no further effect.
