# Armour

This chapter lists helmets, body armor, and shields. Item Qualities and Flaws apply per [15 — Equipment](15-equipment.md). Armour values are flat reductions applied at the struck hit location (Chapter 11). Where a number in parentheses appears next to Encumbrance, the parenthetical is the Stamina value when worn for **Bracing** (a Reaction in Chapter 11) rather than during normal movement.

## How Armor Is Worn

A character may wear up to one **body armor** at a time, one **helmet**, and may use one **shield** in hand. Subtle armor may be layered beneath non-Subtle armor as a single exception (see Chapter 15, Subtle trait).

A character whose **Might** is less than an armor's Heavy rating treats Agility tests at Disadvantage and gains no Stamina recovery from the Catching Breath action until the armor is doffed.

**Donning and doffing.** Helmets and shields require 1 Action. Body armor of Encumbrance Standard requires 1 minute with help, 3 minutes without. Heavy body armor requires 5 minutes with help, 15 minutes without; greaves and manicae each add 1 minute.

## Helmets

A helmet covers the **Head** location only. Helms with face protection impose Disadvantage on Observation (Sight) and ranged attack tests at Long range.

### Helmet Notes

- **Coif.** A flexible mail hood often worn beneath a helmet. May be worn under any non-Subtle helmet; in that case use the higher Armour and add 1 (max 5).
- **Galea.** The classic Imperial legionary helm. Cheek guards and a neck flare; no face plate.
- **Cassis.** Used by cavalry and frontier officers. The face guard is bronze or iron; vision is constricted.
- **Spangenhelm.** Frame-and-plate construction common among Germanic, Gothic, and steppe peoples. Lighter than a Galea, easier to repair in the field.
- **Sallet / Visored Cassis.** A heavy helm with a hinged or fixed visor. Excellent protection, severe vision penalty.
- **Phrygian Cap.** A pointed felt or soft leather cap. Provides token padding and is unmistakably non-military; useful for travel through hostile territory.

## Body Armor

Body armor lists its covered **Locations** explicitly. When a location is covered, that armor's Armour value applies on hits to that location. If a location is not covered, the wearer takes the hit unarmored unless a helmet, shield, or limb piece applies.

### Body Armor Notes

- **Padded Jack (subarmalis).** A thick padded garment worn under any heavier armor. It does not stack with the armor it is worn beneath unless that armor is non-Subtle; then Subtle stacking applies (add both values).
- **Leather Armor.** Treated and stiffened hide. Light, flexible, common across the Empire and beyond.
- **Studded Leather.** Leather reinforced with metal studs or small plates. A militia or city-watch standard.
- **Hide / Animal-hide Cloak.** Worn over the body for warmth as much as protection. The Skald and northern hunter's default.
- **Lorica Hamata (Mail).** Interlocking rings of iron. The mainstay of legionary infantry; effective against slashing, weak to piercing — represented mechanically as **Vulnerable (Piercing)**, giving Penetrating attacks +1 to their Penetrating rating against this armor.
- **Lorica Squamata (Scale).** Iron or brass scales sewn to a fabric backing. Distinctive sound when struck or jogged; thus Loud.
- **Lorica Segmentata (Laminar).** Overlapping plates fastened with internal straps. Excellent body protection; nothing for arms or legs by itself. Loud when moving briskly.
- **Lorica Musculata (Plate Cuirass).** Sculpted bronze or iron breastplate, traditional for officers and high command. Expensive, statusful, and silent.
- **Lamellar.** Small plates laced rather than riveted. Persian, Hunnic, and Gothic forces favor it. Loud, but lighter to repair than segmentata.
- **Manica.** A laminar guard for a single arm. Often worn paired or in combination with mail by gladiators, frontier swordsmen, and centurion-level officers.
- **Knemides (Greaves).** Plate shin guards strapped over the calves. Roman officers and Greek-style hoplites alike wear them.
- **Splint.** Metal strips riveted to a leather backing. Almost exclusively for limbs.
- **Brigandine.** Small overlapping plates riveted between two layers of cloth or leather. Appears as a coat or tunic; Subtle.
- **Quilted Coat (Gambeson).** A common standalone armor where mail is unavailable. Layers of linen or wool quilted with horsehair or tow.

## Shields

Wielding a shield takes one hand and grants a number of benefits.

- When you make an Opposed Test to defend against a melee attack, you do not suffer Disadvantage if you are not wielding a melee weapon.
- If you are wielding a melee weapon and a shield at the same time, your melee weapon gains the Defensive trait.
- You gain Advantage on Opposed Athletics (Might) Tests to Defend a Zone.
- Once per round, when you are the target of a ranged attack from outside your Zone, you can brace yourself against incoming fire from the attacker's Zone. If you do so, you gain bonus Armour equal to the Shield rating against that attack, and against all subsequent attacks originating from that Zone until the beginning of your next turn.
- If you use a shield to protect yourself from a melee or ranged attack which has the **Rend** trait, the shield is destroyed. If the attack's Rend rating was higher than the Shield rating, you take damage as normal. Otherwise the attack has no further effect.

### Shield Notes

- **Buckler.** A small metal disc strapped to the forearm. Concealable beneath a cloak; Subtle.
- **Parma.** A small round shield used by velites, auxiliaries, and many cavalry.
- **Scutum.** The iconic large rectangular legionary shield. When two or more scutum-bearers occupy the same zone and declare a Shield Wall, each gains +1 Shield rating against attacks from outside the zone. Setting up a Shield Wall costs 1 Action per participant.
- **Clipeus.** A large round shield favored by Greek-style hoplite forces and later cavalry.
- **Pelta.** A light, crescent-shaped shield used by skirmishers. It may be hurled as a thrown weapon for 1d4 stress at Short range; once thrown, it is gone.
- **Targe.** A center-grip round shield with a metal boss. Common among Celtic, Pictish, and Caledonian fighters.
- **Tower Shield (mantlet).** A portable wall, often planted and braced. Cannot be used for attacks (including shield bashes). Excellent against ranged fire from one direction.

## Improvising Shields And Cover

Any solid object of roughly the right size and density may serve as an improvised shield: a heavy table, a wagon wheel, a stout door. Treat as Shield (1), Heavy (1), and Bulky for one scene; it breaks if used to block any attack with Rend, regardless of rating.

**Cover** is set by the environment and is a separate bonus to Defense or Armour depending on the GM's reading; see Chapter 11.

## Wearing Multiple Pieces

A character may layer non-overlapping armor freely:

- One body armor.
- One helmet.
- One pair of manicae (or splint arms), if the body armor does not already cover the arms.
- One pair of greaves (or splint legs), if the body armor does not already cover the legs.

When a Sundered or Rent condition damages armor at a location with multiple pieces, the **outermost** piece is damaged first. A character may declare which piece is outermost during a Long Rest.

## Maintenance Reminder

Armor degrades with use. See [15.4 — Wealth, Trade, and Maintenance](15.4-wealth-and-trade.md) for repair costs and times. As a quick reference: restoring 1 lost Armour at a location costs **(item price × 0.05) denarii** and 1 Camp Action with an Armorer's Kit, or 1 day with a town smith.
