# Weapons

This chapter lists the weapons available in the default setting and the ammunition that feeds them. Weapon Traits are defined in [15 — Equipment](15-equipment.md); Item Qualities and Flaws apply per the same chapter.

## Reading The Tables

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Damage | The weapon's damage rating **N**. On a hit, roll **NdN-N** — that many d3 minus the same number — then add the relevant attribute bonus (see below) and any SL difference (added to N before rolling, per Ch 11). Reduce the total by Armour at the struck location. Full procedure in [15 — Equipment](15-equipment.md#damage-resolution). |
| Hands | 1H = one-handed; 2H = two-handed; V = Versatile (either). |
| Range | Engaged (immediate) or Reach (melee); Immediate / Short / Medium / Long (ranged). |
| Enc. | Encumbrance category (Tiny, Light, Standard, Heavy). |
| Rest. | Restriction rating, 0–5. |
| Price | Baseline price in denarii. |
| Traits | Weapon traits; see Chapter 15. |

Melee damage adds **Might (MIG)** as a flat bonus (per Chapter 4). Ranged damage adds **Insight (INS)**. Mental attacks add **Willpower (WIL)**. Attribute bonuses are added to the post-roll total, not to N before the dice are rolled. SL difference, by contrast, is added to N **before** rolling, so a bigger margin of success means a bigger spread of possible damage.

## Light Melee Weapons

Light weapons are quick, easily concealed, and well-suited to thieves, scouts, and second-hand off-hand work. They deal modest stress on their own but excel paired with a primary weapon, a shield, or surprise.

### Light Melee Notes

- **Pugio.** The standard legionary sidearm worn on the right hip. Compact, well-balanced.
- **Dagger.** Any general utility blade, hunter's knife, or eating knife pressed into service.
- **Sica.** A curved Thracian and gladiator's blade; its hooked profile slips around shields and into seams.
- **Akinakes.** Persian short sword carried by Scythian and Median fighters in the eastern marches.
- **Seax.** A long single-edged knife favored by Germanic and Saxon raiders. May be wielded as a small sword.
- **Cudgel.** A heavy stick of seasoned wood. The simplest weapon a peasant can swing in earnest.
- **Cestus.** Leather-bound, sometimes weighted, hand wraps for pankration and brawling.
- **Fighting Net.** A weighted net used by retiarius gladiators. Catches limbs and weapons; needs a free hand to re-coil.

### One-Handed Notes

- **Gladius.** The short stabbing sword of the legions. The Reliable trait reflects centuries of refinement; Penetrating 1 reflects its emphasis on the thrust.
- **Spatha.** A longer cavalry and late-imperial infantry sword. May be wielded one- or two-handed.
- **Falcata.** A forward-curved Iberian sword that concentrates the cut at the tip.
- **Sabre.** Curved cavalry blade favored by steppe and frontier riders.
- **Hand Axe.** A working tool that doubles as a serviceable weapon, balanced for throwing.
- **Francisca.** A short-handled throwing axe associated with Frankish warriors; designed to be thrown before the charge.
- **Dolabra.** The legion's pickaxe-and-blade combination tool. As effective at breaching shields and armor as at digging entrenchments.
- **Horseman's Pick.** A short, hammer-shaped pick designed to punch through plate; useless against soft targets.
- **Mace, War Hammer, Hand Flail.** Crushing weapons. The Flail's articulated head curls around shields; ignore the Defensive bonus shields normally grant.
- **Hasta.** The legionary thrusting spear; older issue than the pilum.

## Two-Handed Melee Weapons

Two-handed weapons trade defense for reach, mass, or both. A character wielding a two-handed weapon may not also use a shield.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Longsword | 5 | 2H | Engaged | Standard | 3 | 200 | Versatile (declared); Defensive |
| Two-Handed Axe | 5 | 2H | Engaged | Heavy | 2 | 160 | Rend 2, Heavy (3) |
| Great Axe (Northern) | 6 | 2H | Engaged | Heavy | 2 | 180 | Rend 2, Heavy (4) |
| Falx | 5 | 2H | Reach | Heavy | 4 | 220 | Reach, Penetrating 2, Rend 1 |
| Maul / Two-Handed Hammer | 5 | 2H | Engaged | Heavy | 2 | 140 | Inflict (Stunned, 2 SL), Heavy (4) |
| Pike | 4 | 2H | Reach | Heavy | 2 | 60 | Reach, Set vs Charge (+1d on opposed Fighting test) |
| Quarterstaff | 3 | 2H | Reach | Standard | 0 | 4 | Reach, Defensive |
| Glaive / Pole-arm | 5 | 2H | Reach | Heavy | 3 | 150 | Reach, Rend 1 |

### Two-Handed Notes

- **Longsword.** A cruciform-hilted sword usable in one or two hands; "Versatile (declared)" means the wielder chooses one- or two-handed grip at the start of each round. Two-handed adds either +1 to the Damage rating (one extra d3, with the subtraction increased by 1 to match) or the Penetrating 1 trait.
- **Two-Handed Axe / Great Axe.** Used by Norse, Goth, and Caledonian warriors; the great axe is heavier and longer.
- **Falx.** A long curved two-handed Dacian blade. Its reach and penetrating cut led the Roman legions to reinforce their helmets specifically to counter it.
- **Maul.** A heavy hammer used to break shield walls and gates.
- **Pike.** A long thrusting weapon best used in formation. "Set vs Charge" grants +1d on the opposed Fighting test against a charging foe.
- **Quarterstaff.** A traveler's staff; cheap, ubiquitous, and respectable.
- **Glaive / Pole-arm.** A blade on a pole — the dolabra's grandfather, broadly. Many regional variants exist.

### Thrown Notes

- **Pilum.** The legionary throwing spear. A long, soft-iron shank bends on impact, fouling the target's shield. After a pilum strikes a shield (whether the wielder is hit or not), the shield's Shield rating is reduced by 1 until cleaned with a 1-minute action.
- **Plumbata.** A small lead-weighted dart hidden behind a legionary's scutum; thrown in volleys.

### Bow Notes

- **Sling.** A loop of leather and cord. Quiet, cheap, and surprisingly accurate in trained hands.
- **Fustibalus (Staff Sling).** A sling mounted on a staff, throwing larger stones farther and harder.
- **Short Bow.** Common across the Empire and frontier. A hunter's weapon as often as a soldier's.
- **Bow (Self / Composite).** The standard military bow of the Imperial archer cohorts and Eastern auxiliaries.
- **Recurve Bow.** Used by horse archers and steppe peoples; greater stored energy than a self bow.
- **Longbow.** A long self-bow drawing heavy weight. Devastating; demanding to draw.
- **Spear Thrower.** A short lever extending the arm's leverage; the woomera and atlatl. Used with javelins, not arrows.

- **Arcuballista.** A light, hand-spanned crossbow. Accurate, slow to reload.

## Selecting And Pricing A Starting Weapon

A character building from Chapter 2's "one weapon of Restriction 3 or lower" allowance has effectively the entire mainstream weapon list available. The GM may forbid specific entries on the basis of region (no falx outside Dacia) or season (no fresh pila outside an active campaign).

For a quick comparison:

- **Citizen militiaman:** Hasta + Parma, Pugio.
- **Legionary:** Gladius + Scutum, Pilum × 2, Pugio.
- **Auxiliary archer:** Short Bow, 24 arrows, Dagger.
- **Frontier scout:** Hand Axe, Short Bow, 12 arrows.
- **Steppe rider:** Sabre, Recurve Bow, 24 arrows.
- **Gothic warrior:** Two-Handed Axe, Seax.
- **Gladiator (retiarius):** Fighting Net, Trident (treat as Pike for stats), Manica.
- **Officer:** Spatha, Pugio, Plate Cuirass.
- **Druid:** Quarterstaff, Druidic Sickle.

These are not rules — they are pricing checks against the table above.
