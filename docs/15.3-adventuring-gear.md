# Adventuring Gear

Adventuring gear covers everything that is not armor, a weapon, or wealth — kits, tools, light sources, containers, consumables, animals, vehicles, and the small material reality that makes the difference between a successful expedition and a frozen, hungry, lost party.

Encumbrance, Restriction, and Availability work as defined in [15 — Equipment](15-equipment.md). Prices are in denarii.

## Kits And Toolsets

A **kit** is a bundle of small tools relevant to a skill. Owning the kit allows skill use that would otherwise be impossible or made at +1 Difficulty step (see Improvised Equipment in Chapter 15). A character may carry a kit on the belt, in a pack, or in a mule's load.

## Spellcaster's Materials

Spell components are explicitly listed under Chapter 3 by tradition and ritual. As a rule of thumb:

- A minor casting consumes 1 den. of common reagent per spell.
- A ritual consumes ingredients of combined Restriction equal to the ritual's Difficulty (see Chapter 3).
- Consecrated water, incense, and small candles run 1–5 den. each.

## Encumbrance Worked Example

A scout traveling light:

- Backpack (Light)
- Bedroll (Light)
- Cook's Kit (Standard)
- Hunter's Kit (Light)
- Rope, 50 ft hemp (Light)
- Waterskin (Light)
- 3 days' rations (Light)
- Short Bow (Standard)
- Quiver, 24 arrows (Standard)
- Hand Axe (Standard)
- Dagger (Light)
- 60 coins (Tiny)

Total Stamina invested: 4 (Standard) + (7 Light = 4 Stamina) = **8 Stamina invested**, leaving the rest of their Stamina for movement and pushing. The backpack discounts 1 Standard item, dropping to **7 Stamina invested** in practice.

Compare a heavy infantryman:

- Lorica Hamata (Heavy = 2)
- Galea (Light)
- Scutum (Heavy = 2)
- Gladius (Standard)
- Pugio (Light)
- 2 Pila (2 Standard)
- Bedroll (Light)
- 3 days rations (Light)
- Waterskin (Light)
- 40 coins (Tiny)

Total: 2 + 2 + 1 + 2 + (5 Light = 3) = **10 Stamina invested**. A legionary's life is measured in carrying capacity.
