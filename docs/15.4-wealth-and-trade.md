# Wealth, Trade, And Maintenance

This chapter covers coinage, purchasing, haggling, scarcity, item degradation, and repair. It is the economy-of-play sub-chapter implied by Chapter 13 (Connections) and Chapter 18 (Downtime), and it formalizes the prices used in 15.1, 15.2, and 15.3.

## Coinage

The default setting uses the late-Imperial denominations. Coins are physical Tiny items; in bulk they count as Light, Standard, or Heavy per Chapter 15.

| Coin | Metal | Value (in denarii) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| As (sg.) / Asses (pl.) | Bronze or copper | 1/16 denarius (0.0625) | Petty change; market vendors use this. |
| Dupondius | Brass | 1/8 denarius (0.125) | Two asses. |
| Sestertius | Brass | 1/4 denarius (0.25) | The common reckoning coin for everyday prices. |
| Quinarius | Silver | 1/2 denarius (0.5) | Half-denarius coin. |
| **Denarius** | Silver | **1 denarius (1.0)** | The base reckoning unit for this chapter. |
| Antoninianus (radiate) | Silver-alloyed | 2 denarii | Late-Imperial double denarius. |
| Aureus | Gold | 25 denarii | Used for large payments, dowries, military bonuses. |
| Solidus | Gold | 40 denarii | Late-Imperial reform coin. |
| Foreign coinage | varies | varies (assess via Academia (Evaluation)) | Steppe, eastern, and barbarian coins may be worth more or less than face. |

**Price baseline.** All prices in this rulebook are stated in **denarii (den.)**. A character paying for a 30-denarius healer's pouch is satisfied by 30 denarii, 7.5 quinarii, 120 sestertii, or any combination thereof.

## Purchasing Items

The act of buying an item is usually narrated, not rolled. A roll is called for only when the price, the availability, or the buyer's standing is in doubt.

### Step 1 — Availability

The GM declares the item's Availability for this scene, considering its Restriction and the location. Defaults:

| Where You Are | Common | Uncommon | Rare | Very Rare | Unique |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Village or hamlet | yes | maybe | no | no | no |
| Town market | yes | yes | maybe | no | no |
| City market | yes | yes | yes | maybe | no |
| Capital, named source | yes | yes | yes | yes | maybe |
| Special connection or quest | — | — | — | — | possible |

### Step 2 — Negotiation (Optional)

If the buyer accepts the listed price, no roll is needed. If the buyer wants a better price, make a **Charisma + Manipulation (Haggle)** test opposed by the seller's relevant skill. Default difficulty for a fair seller: **Challenging**.

| Outcome | Result |
|---|---|
| Critical Success | Pay 50% of listed price. |
| Success | Pay 75% of listed price. |
| Success with Complication | Pay 75% of listed price; the seller now distrusts you. Future haggling with them is at +1 step Difficulty. |
| Marginal Failure | Pay 100% of listed price. |
| Failure | Pay 100% of listed price; the seller refuses further sales today. |
| Critical Failure | Pay 125% of listed price; the seller spreads word, and future haggling in the same town is at +1 step Difficulty for one Downtime Period. |

A seller may also raise the price. If the player's character has Reputation (Chapter 13) with a faction the seller respects, they may use **Reputation in place of Charisma** for the haggle test, per the Name-dropping rule.

### Step 3 — Delivery

Common items leave with the buyer. Uncommon and Rare items may require a wait or a commission deposit (typically 25% of price). Very Rare and Unique items always require a deposit and a deadline; the GM is encouraged to make their acquisition into a Downtime project or an adventure hook.

### Step 4 — Quality

Most items are baseline. An item with a Quality is doubled in price and one step rarer in Availability; an item with a Flaw is halved and one step more available. See Chapter 15 for Qualities and Flaws.

A seller may not disclose Flaws. To identify Flaws before purchase, make an **Academia (Evaluation)** test against a Difficulty set by the seller's Subterfuge (default Challenging).

## Selling Items

Selling is the inverse, and is generally less favorable than buying. Default rates:

- **Used or looted items:** sell for **25%** of listed price.
- **Unused or freshly crafted items:** sell for **50%** of listed price.
- **Items with full provenance, certified by a guild or scribe:** sell for **75%** of listed price.
- **Master Crafted, Ornamental, or unique items:** sell for **75%–125%** of listed price, depending on connections and demand. Often requires a buyer scene.

A character may attempt to improve sale price with a successful Charisma + Manipulation (Haggle) or Charisma + Society test, using the table above with the modifiers reversed.

**Black market.** Restricted items (Restriction 3+) sold openly attract notice. Selling Restriction 4–5 items requires a Subterfuge or Society connection; failing the test exposes the character to the GM's choice of consequence — typically a faction reaction in the next Downtime.

## Maintenance And Repair

Items degrade with hard use. The most frequent forms of damage and their repair costs follow.

### Armour

| Cause | Effect | Repair Cost | Repair Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sundered (Condition) | Lose 1 Armour at the struck location | 5% of armour price; Routine Craft (Armouring) | 1 Camp Action (Armourer's Kit) or 1 day in town |
| Rent (Trait, per point) | Lose Armour equal to Rend rating | 5% of armour price per Armour point lost; Challenging | 1 Camp Action per point, or 1 day in town |
| Critical Hit on Unreliable armour | Armour destroyed | n/a — replace | n/a |
| Worn from neglect | gains Worn flaw | 10% of armour price to remove; Routine Craft (Armouring) | 1 Downtime Activity |
| Catastrophic damage (storm, fire, magic) | GM call; usually halve max Armour | 25% of price; Difficult Craft (Armouring) | 1 Downtime Activity |

### Weapons

| Cause | Effect | Repair Cost | Repair Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Worn from neglect | gains Worn flaw | 5% of weapon price; Routine Craft (Smithing) | 1 Camp Action with Whetstone or 1 day in town |
| Edge dulled in combat (after 3 fights without maintenance) | −1d on first attack each scene until honed | 1 den. + 1 Camp Action with Whetstone | — |
| Unstable detonation | Weapon destroyed | n/a — replace | n/a |
| Cracked haft / shaft | Unusable until repaired | 10% of weapon price; Routine Craft | 1 Camp Action with Carpenter's Tools |
| Broken (Fumble result) | Item destroyed | n/a — replace | n/a |

### Other Gear

- **Kits** are consumed by use; refill at the per-use rate listed in Chapter 15.3.
- **Vessels** (rope, lantern, waterskin) wear down. The GM may call for a replacement at the end of an adventure if hard-used.
- **Animals** require feed, stabling, and occasional veterinary care. A neglected mount becomes Fatigued after a Long journey, halving its travel speed until rested.

### Camp Action Repair Limits

A character with the appropriate kit may make **one** repair per Camp Action, per kit. A character without the kit cannot perform armour or weapon repair beyond a hone-with-whetstone (which addresses only the "Edge dulled" entry).

## Property

A character may purchase property in Downtime. Property:

- Reduces lifestyle costs by half while occupied (you sleep in your own bed).
- Anchors a Connection to a place (a tavern, a workshop, a temple).
- Can be levied against in a future Faction conflict; property is a hostage to fortune.

| Property | Approximate Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tenement room (in a city) | 600 den. + 4 den./month upkeep | Modest lifestyle anchor. |
| Small house (village or town outskirts) | 2,000 den. + 10 den./month | Modest. |
| Townhouse | 4,000 den. + 20 den./month | Comfortable. |
| Workshop (smithy, brewery, scriptorium) | 1,500–6,000 den. + 30 den./month | Lets a character craft at home and accept commissions; becomes a Faction node. |
| Tavern / Inn (small) | 5,000 den. + 60 den./month | Generates 100–300 den./month if managed; becomes a Connection hub. |
| Country villa | 40,000+ den. + 200 den./month | Wealthy or Aristocratic lifestyle anchor; estate income may offset costs. |
| Ship (river barge or skiff) | 800–2,500 den. + 25 den./month | A mobile property; needs a crew. |
| Ship (sea-going) | 8,000–25,000 den. + 200 den./month | Adventure-scale; usually owned by a faction the character is part of. |

## Banking, Letters Of Credit, And Storage

Carrying coin invites theft. The setting supports:

- **Money changers (argentarii).** Most cities have one. They hold deposits, issue letters of credit redeemable at distant cities (typical fee 2% of value), and exchange foreign coin (2–10% off face value depending on the coin).
- **Faction storage.** A faction the character belongs to may hold property and coin on the character's behalf, withdrawable at any time but tied to relationship standing.
- **Temple deposits.** Temples accept deposits for safekeeping in exchange for offerings (typical 1% per month). They will not return funds to a thief or fugitive without faction support.
- **Letters of credit** themselves are Tiny items but must be inspected by a scribe (Routine Education) before they are accepted.

## Spending For Effect

A character may spend coin to gain a temporary bonus, with GM permission. Suggested rates:

| Effect | Cost (den.) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| +1d on a Society or Manipulation test in a scene | 10 | A round of drinks; a generous bribe. |
| +1 SL on a single test in a scene | 50 | A magnificent gift; a serious bribe. |
| Buy off a single Doom about to be spent | 25–200 | Cost scales with the Doom's severity; GM sets the price. |
| Cover a complication ("the witness vanishes") | 200+ | Always plot-significant. Faction reactions follow. |
| Sponsor a public spectacle (gain Reputation +1 with one faction) | 1,000 | 1 Downtime Activity required. |

These spends should be narrated — the money is going *somewhere* — and the GM should treat them as offers, not buttons.

## Inheritance, Loot, And Treasure

Treasure recovered from adventures is at the GM's discretion. As a guideline budget:

| Adventure Tier | Expected Loot (den. per player) |
|---|---|
| Tone-setting / introduction | 20–50 |
| Standard | 80–200 |
| Hard | 250–600 |
| Deadly / climactic | 800–2,500 |
| Campaign milestone | 3,000–10,000 |

Loot need not be coin. A piece of art at 400 den. value may be sold, returned to its owner for Reputation, or kept as an heirloom and pivot of future plot. A magical or Master Crafted item at 1,500 den. value is itself a meaningful reward.

When the party divides loot, encourage them to agree before the fight: equal shares, share-and-a-half for the GM-designated leader, a fixed share for hirelings, and a tithe to a faction or temple. A fair division procedure prevents one of the system's classic table-time sinks.
