# Downtime And The Campaign Loop

The 2d12 system runs on a repeating loop: **Adventure → Recovery → Downtime → World Reaction → Adventure**. This chapter formalizes the Downtime and World Reaction steps that have been implied by Full Rest, Connections, Reputation, and Talents but never written out in full.

A downtime period covers any stretch of in‑fiction time longer than a Full Rest, typically a week or more, during which characters are not directly pursuing an adventure goal. Downtime is when training pays off, wounds close, favors get called in, and the world quietly changes around the party.

## The Campaign Loop

| Step | Length | Primary Mechanics |
|---|---|---|
| Adventure | Hours to days of fiction | Action rolls, Combat, Social Conflict, Pursuit, Magic, Superiority |
| Recovery | Hours to a few days | Short, Long, and Full Rest; First Aid; Attribute Drain healing |
| Downtime | Days to months | Downtime Activities (this chapter); Advancement |
| World Reaction | Concurrent with Downtime | GM-driven Faction Clocks, Doom advances, Connection requests |

Each loop ends when the GM presents (or the players seek out) the next adventure hook, which is usually generated by World Reaction. A campaign that never leaves Adventure burns out characters; a campaign that never returns to Adventure stalls. The default cadence is roughly one full Downtime period for every two to three adventures.

## Downtime Periods

A **Downtime Period** is declared by the GM and has three traits:

- **Length.** Short (1 week), Standard (1 month), or Long (3+ months). Length determines how many Downtime Activities each character may attempt.
- **Lifestyle.** Each character chooses a lifestyle for the period that determines living costs and certain bonuses (see *Lifestyles* below).
- **Location.** Where the character spends the period. A character at sea cannot patronize a city workshop; a character holed up in a hermitage cannot tend their faction reputation.

| Length | Downtime Activities | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Short | 1 | A breathing spell between two pressing adventures. |
| Standard | 3 | The default. Most campaigns assume one Standard period between major arcs. |
| Long | 5 | Sabbaticals, prison stretches, long winters, exile. |

A character may always choose to **rest** with their unused activities instead, automatically removing all Fatigue and healing 1 additional Wound per unused activity at the end of the period.

## Downtime Activities

Each Downtime Activity is resolved with a single Extended Test, taking the entire period to complete. Unless otherwise noted, a character may not attempt the same activity more than once per Downtime Period.

### Training

Choose one skill the character already has at least 1 rank in. Make an Extended Reason + Education or Willpower + Resilience test (GM's choice based on the skill) at Challenging difficulty over the period.

- **2 SL:** Earn 1 Skill Advancement Point (see *Advancement*).
- **4 SL:** As above, plus halve the XP cost of your next rank in that skill.
- **6+ SL:** As above, plus narrate a memorable mentor or breakthrough scene that the GM should fold into the next adventure.

A character cannot train a skill above their attribute’s natural maximum without a teacher whose rank exceeds their own.

### Convalescence

Mandatory if the character ended the previous adventure with a lingering Critical Wound, ongoing disease, or Madness. Make an Extended Constitution + Resilience or Willpower + Resilience test at a difficulty set by the lingering condition (Hard is the baseline).

- **Pass:** The condition is cleared. Lingering Critical Wounds heal fully; Madness becomes dormant.
- **Fail:** The condition persists into the next adventure, and the character begins it with 1 Fatigue.
- **Critical Fail:** The condition deepens. The GM may upgrade a Madness, convert a lingering wound into a permanent Trait scar, or advance a disease's stage.

Convalescence does not consume a normal Activity slot in a Standard or Long period, but it does occupy the slot in a Short period.

### Crafting And Research Projects

Make an Extended test with the appropriate skill (typically Reason + Craft, Reason + Education, or Insight + Lore). The difficulty is set by the project scope:

| Scope | Difficulty | Time | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| Modest | Challenging | 1 Activity | A masterwork weapon, a forged document, a short treatise. |
| Major | Difficult | 2 Activities | A suit of bespoke armour, a translated ancient text, a complex alchemical batch. |
| Grand | Hard | 3+ Activities | A new spell formula, an enchanted item, a small siege engine. |

The GM may require components, access to a workshop, or a contact (see Chapter 13) before the project begins. Each SL spent on success may improve quality, reduce cost, or shorten future use of the item.

### Faction Maintenance

Spend the period working on behalf of a single contact or faction. Make a Charisma + Manipulation or Charisma + Society test at the relationship's current classification difficulty.

- **Contact:** Pass to improve relationship classification by 1 step, or earn 1 favor at the current step.
- **Faction:** Pass to gain Reputation +1 (cap 10), or convert 5 Reputation into Influence +1 (cap 5) if your Reputation is already at 10.
- **Critical Success:** Choose two of the above benefits.
- **Failure:** No change.
- **Critical Failure:** A rival within the contact or faction makes a move against you; the GM gains 2 Doom usable only against you during the next adventure.

A character may not maintain the same contact or faction in two consecutive Downtime Periods unless they are actively in trouble with them.

### Carousing And Lifestyle Investment

Make a Charisma + Society test at Challenging difficulty. The character spends one lifestyle tier above their current one for the period (see *Lifestyles* below) and gains contacts and rumors instead of property.

- **Pass:** Gain one new Neutral contact appropriate to the location, plus one rumor or lead from the GM.
- **Each additional SL:** Improve the contact one step toward Fair or gain an additional rumor.
- **Failure:** No new contact, but still pay the upgraded lifestyle cost.
- **Critical Failure:** Make an enemy or trigger a scandal; the GM gains 1 Doom usable against you in any social scene during the next adventure.

### Personal Project

A catch-all for activities not covered above: writing a memoir, courting a paramour, raising a child, running a tavern, restoring a homestead, hunting one specific quarry. The GM and player agree on a target SL total (typically 6 for Modest, 12 for Major, 20 for Grand) and a skill to use. Each Downtime Period contributes one Extended test toward that total. The reward is narrative and chosen at the start: a new Connection, a Trait, a piece of property, a permanent +1 to a derived stat, or a unique Talent prerequisite.

### Recovery From Sanity Loss

A character with current Sanity below maximum may dedicate an Activity to recovery in a safe and supportive environment. Make an Extended Willpower + Resilience test at a difficulty equal to the current Madness's severity (Difficult for mild, Hard for moderate, Very Hard for severe).

- **Pass:** Recover 1 Sanity per 2 SL.
- **Critical Success:** Also reduce one Madness's severity by one step.
- **Failure:** No recovery; the Madness may surface during a Downtime scene chosen by the GM.

## Lifestyles

Lifestyle covers food, lodging, clothing, and incidental expenses for the period. Pay at the start; a character who cannot afford their declared lifestyle defaults to **Squalid** and gains 1 Fatigue at the start of the next adventure.

| Lifestyle | Cost / Month | Effects |
|---|---|---|
| Squalid | 0 coins | −1d on first Charisma test of next adventure; +1 Fatigue if maintained two periods in a row. |
| Poor | 20 coins | No effect. |
| Modest | 50 coins | +1 Stamina recovery at start of next adventure. |
| Comfortable | 150 coins | +1 SL on one Downtime Activity of your choice. |
| Wealthy | 400 coins | +1 SL on one Downtime Activity and +1d on first social roll of next adventure. |
| Aristocratic | 1,000 coins | As Wealthy, plus you may borrow one piece of standard equipment from your station for the next adventure at no Stamina cost. |

Lifestyle costs may be reduced or waived by the right Connection (a contact who shelters you), faction membership (a guild dormitory), or property (your own homestead).

## World Reaction

While the player characters rest and train, the world acts. Concurrent with every Downtime Period, the GM advances:

- **Faction Clocks.** Each significant faction has a clock with 4, 6, or 8 segments tracking a long-term goal. The GM ticks each clock by 1 during a Short period, 1–2 during a Standard period, or 2–3 during a Long period, modified by player interference or aid.
- **Doom.** Any Doom remaining at the end of an adventure carries into Downtime. The GM may spend it to advance a faction clock by 1 extra segment, introduce a rumor that costs the players a Connection, or guarantee a complication in the next adventure's opening scene.
- **Connection Requests.** Roll 1d6 per Connection at the start of Downtime. On a 1, that contact or faction calls in a favor or requests aid during the period. Refusing without an excuse worsens the relationship as per Chapter 13.

The GM should narrate at least one short World Reaction beat per Downtime Period — a letter delivered, a rumor heard at the tavern, a body found at the crossroads. These beats become the hooks of the next Adventure step.

## Sample Downtime Sequence

A Standard period (1 month) for a character with 3 Activities, Comfortable lifestyle, and a lingering Critical Wound:

1. **Activity 1 — Convalescence.** Extended Constitution + Resilience, Hard. Passes with 3 SL; the Critical Wound clears and they begin the next adventure healthy.
2. **Activity 2 — Training (Manipulation).** Extended Charisma + Education, Challenging. Earns 1 Skill Advancement Point.
3. **Activity 3 — Faction Maintenance (the Merchants' Guild).** Charisma + Society at Fair (Challenging). Passes with 2 SL; Reputation rises from 6 to 7.
4. **Lifestyle Bonus.** +1 SL applied to the Training activity, retroactively earning a Critical Success.
5. **World Reaction.** The Merchants' Guild clock advances 2 segments toward "monopolize river trade"; a contact in a rival faction sends a warning that becomes the hook of the next adventure.

The character returns to play rested, slightly improved, deeper in with one faction, and squarely in the crosshairs of another. The loop is ready to repeat.
