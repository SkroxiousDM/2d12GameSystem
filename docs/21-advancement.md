# Advancement

Talents, Skills, and Attributes all exist as builds, but the cadence by which a character grows is not yet written down. This chapter defines XP, Skill Advancement Points, Talent acquisition, attribute growth, and retraining.

The system uses two parallel currencies:

- **Experience Points (XP)** are spent on Skills, Talents, and (rarely) Attributes.
- **Skill Advancement Points (SAP)** are earned through use and training, and are spent specifically on Skills.

A character may use either currency to raise a skill, but Talents and Attributes require XP.

## Earning XP

XP is awarded by the GM at defined moments. Suggested awards:

| Trigger | XP |
|---|---|
| Completing a session | 1 |
| Resolving the central goal of an adventure | 2–4 |
| Achieving a personal goal or fulfilling a Belief | 1–2 |
| Surviving a Deadly encounter | 1 |
| Roleplaying a Madness or Flaw to a meaningful cost | 1 |
| Discovering a major secret or piece of lore | 1 |
| Spending a Connection's favor in a defining way | 1 |

A typical adventure of 3–5 sessions awards 8–14 XP. A character who plays every session for a year of weekly play will accrue roughly 100 XP, which is enough to take an early-career character to a late-career one without inflating beyond the attribute caps in Chapter 4.

## Earning Skill Advancement Points

SAP is earned through *use*, not award. The triggers:

- **Marked Skill.** At the start of a session, each player nominates up to two skills as **Marked** for that session. Any time a Marked skill is used on a Common, Opposed, or Extended Test that produces a Success With Complication, Failure, Failure With Complication, or Critical Failure, the character earns 1 SAP for that skill at the end of the session (regardless of how many times it triggered). One SAP per Marked skill per session, maximum.
- **Training.** A successful Training Downtime Activity grants 1 SAP per 2 SL (see Chapter 18).
- **Mentorship.** A successful Mentor scene during downtime, with an NPC of higher rank, grants 1 SAP in the mentor's specialty.

SAP is **skill-specific**. SAP earned for Stealth can only be spent on Stealth.

## Spending Currency

### Skills

Raising a skill costs XP equal to the new rank, **or** SAP equal to twice the new rank.

| New Rank | XP Cost | SAP Cost |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1 | 2 |
| 2 | 2 | 4 |
| 3 | 3 | 6 |
| 4 | 4 | 8 |
| 5 | 5 | 10 |

A character may not raise a skill above their natural attribute cap (typically equal to the number of Traits the character possesses).

A character may **mix** currencies — for example, pay 4 SAP and 1 XP to reach Rank 3 in Manipulation — provided the total cost is met. Track partial payment between sessions.

### Specializations

A Specialization within a skill costs 2 XP or 4 SAP and grants the listed bonus from Chapter 5. A character may hold multiple specializations within the same skill.

### Talents

A Talent's XP cost is determined by its tier as listed in Chapter 6:

| Talent Tier | XP Cost |
|---|---|
| Initiate (no prereq) | 4 |
| Practiced (1 prereq) | 6 |
| Veteran (2 prereqs) | 8 |
| Master (Veteran + Skill 3+) | 10 |
| Legendary (Master + narrative) | 14 |

Talents may not be paid for with SAP.

A character may hold any number of Talents subject to prerequisite chains; an unofficial guideline of **roughly one Talent earned per 3 adventures** keeps the system at the density it was designed for.

### Attribute Improvement

Attributes are sticky on purpose. To raise an attribute by 1 (subject to the cap in Chapter 4):

- Pay **8 XP** *and* spend a Downtime Period dedicated to the improvement (training, ordeal, blessing).
- The character must have used the attribute in a scene where it cost them something significant in the previous adventure.
- An attribute may not be raised more than once per **5 adventures** of play.

### Traits

A new Trait may be acquired only by a narrative milestone determined with the GM. Acquiring a Trait usually carries no XP cost but unlocks new caps on derived stats and attribute ceilings. A character may have at most one Trait gained from any single adventure.

## Recommended Pace

These targets describe a healthy growth curve for a campaign measured in adventures (not sessions).

| Adventures Played | Approximate Skill Ranks | Talents | Attribute Bumps |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 (creation) | 12–16 ranks distributed | 0–1 | per Chapter 4 |
| 3 | 18–22 | 2 | 0 |
| 6 | 25–30 | 4 | 1 |
| 10 | 32–40 | 6 | 1–2 |
| 15 | 40–48 | 8 | 2–3 |

If a campaign is overshooting, the GM should reduce non-goal XP awards. If it is undershooting, the GM should add more goal and personal-goal awards rather than raising the per-session award.

## Milestones

Some campaigns prefer not to track per-session XP and instead use **milestones**. A milestone is a narrative anchor — completing an arc, defeating a major adversary, saving a faction. A milestone grants:

- 4–8 XP
- 1 SAP in each Marked skill currently outstanding
- A free Trait advancement opportunity at the GM's discretion

Milestone XP can be substituted entirely for per-session XP. Tables should pick one method and stick with it for a campaign.

## Retraining

A character may **retrain** an investment to reflect changes in story, build mistakes, or evolving concept. Retraining requires:

- A Standard Downtime Period dedicated entirely to retraining (no other Activities).
- A narrative justification: an apprenticeship, a religious conversion, a maiming, an oath.
- Payment of **half** the original XP cost of the retrained option, refunded as XP.

### What Can Be Retrained

| Investment | Retrainable? | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| 1 rank in a skill | Yes (down to 0) | 1 Downtime Period |
| A Specialization | Yes | 1 Downtime Period |
| A Talent | Yes, if it is not a prerequisite for another currently held Talent | 1 Downtime Period |
| An Attribute bump | Once per character, after a permanent ability damage event | 1 Long Downtime Period |
| A Trait | Only by narrative loss (curse, surgery, divine reversal) | GM call |

A retrained Talent's prerequisites must still be satisfied at the moment of retraining. A character may not "ladder down" by retraining the prerequisite of a Talent they still possess.

## Death, Replacement, And Legacy

When a character dies, the player may:

- **Bring in a new character at the same XP total** as the deceased, minus 4 XP to represent the bench warm-up.
- **Bring in a heir or apprentice at 75% XP**, who inherits one Connection and one Talent from the deceased and gains a free Trait tied to the loss.
- **Spend a Fate to be "Left for Dead"** per the Attributes chapter and return after Convalescence in the next Downtime, with one permanent scar (a Flaw chosen with the GM).

The campaign loop should absorb a death within one Downtime Period; if it cannot, lower the encounter difficulty floor.

## XP And SAP Tracking Sheet

A minimal tracking line:

```
XP:    earned [    ] / spent [    ] / on hand [    ]
SAP:   [Skill A: __]  [Skill B: __]  [Skill C: __]
Next:  [purchase goal: __________ ] cost: [ XP __ / SAP __ ]
```

Players should update this at the end of every session, even if nothing was earned. The act of tracking is itself a pacing cue: a sheet that never moves is a sheet that signals a stalled loop.
