# 2d12 Game System — Sample Characters

These six pre-generated characters cover a range of archetypes, cultures, and play styles for the *Aetas Obscura* setting (AD 400–550). Each is built using the standard creation rules: **16 attribute points** distributed across Culture (4), Background (4), Role (6), and free assignment (4 — revised to account for Role now granting 6). No attribute exceeds 7 at creation.

Derived stats are calculated per the rules:
- **Stamina** = MIG + CON + DEX + AGI + Traits
- **Vitality** = 3 + CON + Traits
- **Defence** = CON − 2 + Armour
- **Alacrity** = (AGI + INS) / 2 + Traits
- **Resolve** = 3 + WIL + Traits
- **Courage** = WIL − 2 + Morale + Traits
- **Sanity** = REA + INS + CHA + Traits
- **Critical Wound Capacity** = 3 + CON + Traits

*Traits = 0 at creation unless the character gains one from a Talent.*

## 1. Marcus Valerius Corvus — The Veteran Legionary

> *"The legion made me. Whatever I am now, it made me first."*

A retired Roman centurion from a Plebeian family in Ravenna, Corvus served twenty years on the Danube frontier. Theodoric's new order has no use for him, and his pension is three months late.

| Stage | Points Allocated |
|:---|:---|
| **Culture:** Roman | REA +2, CHA +1, MIG +1 |
| **Social Class:** Plebeian / Freedman | 200 denarii starting wealth |
| **Background:** Legionary | MIG +1, CON +1, WIL +1, REA +1 |
| **Role:** Warrior | AGI +1, MIG +2, WIL +1 |
| **Free Points (4)** | CON +2, AGI +1, DEX +1 |

### Attributes

| Attribute | Score |
|:---|:---|
| **MIG** (Might) | 4 |
| **CON** (Constitution) | 3 |
| **DEX** (Dexterity) | 1 |
| **AGI** (Agility) | 2 |
| **REA** (Reason) | 3 |
| **WIL** (Willpower) | 2 |
| **INS** (Insight) | 0 |
| **CHA** (Charisma) | 1 |

**Total: 16**

### Derived Stats

| Stat | Value | Calculation |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Stamina | 10 | MIG(4)+CON(3)+DEX(1)+AGI(2)+Traits(0) |
| Vitality | 6 | 3+CON(3) |

| Resolve | 5 | 3+WIL(2) |
| Courage | 0 | WIL(2)−2 |
| Sanity | 4 | REA(3)+INS(0)+CHA(1) |
| Crit Wound Cap | 6 | 3+CON(3) |

### Skills

| Skill | Rank | Source |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Fighting | 2 | Warrior Role |
| Tactics | 2 | Warrior Role |
| Athletics | 1 | Warrior Role |
| Resilience | 1 | Warrior Role |

### Specializations
- **Resilience — Fortitude** *(from Legionary Background)*
- **Fighting — Melee Weapons** *(from Warrior Role)*

### Talent
- **Civic Authority** *(Roman Culture)* — Advantage on CHA tests when dealing with Imperial institutions
- **Dangerous** *(Warrior Role — Soldier Talents list)* — Desperate Action: make a Fighting check as though Rank 5; may take one additional Reaction this turn

### Equipment
- Gladius (Damage 3, Penetrating 1, Reliable)
- Pilum (Javelin, Damage 3, Reach, Thrown Short)
- Lorica Segmentata (Armour 4, Body only)
- Galea helm (Armour 3)
- Soldier's kit
- 200 denarii

### Languages
- Latin (native); 1 bonus language from REA 3 (half = 1) — *Gothic* (picked up on the Danube)

### Notes
Corvus is a straightforward frontline fighter. High Might keeps his melee damage strong; Fortitude lets him shrug off punishment. His low Insight means he often acts late in initiative. Best used as the party's anchor and door-kicker. His Civic Authority can smooth dealings with Roman bureaucrats, and his reading ability (REA 3) means he can handle written orders, maps, and dispatches.

His exceptional Sanity (12) makes him nearly immune to mental shock and Madness effects. Play him as the party's walking library and post-battle medic.

He cannot read; strategic planning and social interaction are beyond him. Pair him with Porphyrios and let them complement each other perfectly.

## Design Notes

These characters are intentionally complementary:
- **Marcus** and **Aspar** hold the line and absorb damage. Aspar hits harder; Marcus is more technically skilled.
- **Aelflæd** is the fastest, best at wilderness survival, and ideally suited to scouting ahead and setting ambushes.
- **Porphyrios** and **Brennus** handle healing from different traditions — Greek empiricism vs. Gaulish druidic lore. Both are fragile.
- **Miriam** is the social operator — indispensable in cities and courts, useless in a stand-up fight.
