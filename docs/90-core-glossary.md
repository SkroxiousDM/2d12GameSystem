# Core Glossary Draft

This glossary consolidates the currently defined core terms from the existing rules text and leaves unresolved terms blank where the present source either does not define them or does not provide enough context to infer a reliable definition.

## Defined terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Action Roll | A roll used to resolve uncertain actions, including attacks, skill checks, and some spell uses. It uses 2d12, compares the result to a Challenge Threshold, and may produce success, failure, complications, or critical outcomes. |
| Advantage | A favorable modifier to a roll. In action rolls, it is usually represented by rolling one extra d12 and discarding the highest result, though talents and special rules can also grant it. |
| Attribute | A core character rating such as Strength, Constitution, Dexterity, Agility, Reason, Insight, Charisma, or Willpower. Attributes determine many checks and feed derived values. |
| Challenge Threshold | The target number for an action roll. The GM sets it based on task difficulty and circumstance. |
| Characteristic | A stat used in opposed or other checks. In the current text, this appears to be a functional term rather than a separately formalized rules category. |
| Common Test | A check used when nobody directly opposes the acting character but the outcome still matters. |
| Complication | A result where the action succeeds or fails with a cost, twist, or other negative consequence. Complications can also feed scene resources such as Doom. |
| Condition | A named status effect that changes what a character can do or how they function, such as Staggered, Irrational, Unconscious, Frightened, Restrained, or Desperate. |
| Critical Failure | A disastrous action roll result in which both dice match on a failure. It causes severe consequences, including Doom gain and, for attacks, a Fumble roll. |
| Critical Hit | A strong attack result that causes a Critical Wound even if the target has not exceeded maximum Vitality. |
| Critical Success | A powerful action roll result in which both dice match on a success. It grants a strong benefit, including Fortune gain, and can improve attack results. |
| Critical Wound | A severe injury suffered when excess damage is taken, or when an attack or effect inflicts one directly. Critical Wounds have location-based effects and can lead to death if untreated. |
| CT | Abbreviation for Challenge Threshold. |
| Damage | The amount of harm inflicted by an attack or effect before mitigation by armor or other defenses. Physical damage reduces Vitality, while mental damage reduces Resolve. |
| Desperate | A condition applied to NPCs when the party’s Superiority exceeds their Resolve. It can cause them to flee, surrender, or make a final attack, depending on context. |
| Doom | A GM resource gained through certain failures, complications, and critical failures. |
| Doom token | A unit of Doom held by the GM. |
| Drain | A damage effect or consequence in which any unsoaked Resolve stress causes Fatigue. |
| Extended Check | A check resolved over multiple rolls, used for complex or time-consuming tasks with a threshold and possible deadline. |
| Fate | A resource the party may gain from successful rolls or other reward cycles. |
| Fate Point | A reward or meta-resource that may be granted by the GM in place of some success rewards. |
| Fatigue | A cumulative condition representing exhaustion, deprivation, or despair. Each point temporarily reduces Stamina, Vitality, and Resolve by 1. |
| Fighting | The skill used for combat actions, melee attacks, and some force-based checks. |
| Fortune | A player resource gained through certain critical successes or other rewards. |
| Fortune point | A unit of Fortune held by a player. |
| Full Rest | A 24-hour rest in a safe location. It restores all spent Stamina, all spent Resolve, all spent Power, and heals some Wounds and ability damage. |
| GM | The gamemaster, who sets difficulties, adjudicates uncertain outcomes, and manages NPC and scene resources. |
| Hit Location | The body part struck by an attack, usually determined by the Complication die. Current locations include head, arms, legs, and body. |
| Initiative | The order in which characters act in combat, determined by 1d12 plus Alacrity. |
| Level of Success | A measure of how well a check succeeded or failed beyond the threshold. It can be spent on benefits such as information, speed, or confidence. |
| Long Rest | An 8-hour rest that restores some Stamina, Resolve, and Vitality, and improves recovery through first aid. |
| Opposed Test | A check where two characters roll against each other, and the higher result or higher Level of Success wins. |
| Pursuit | A pursuit is a contest of speed, nerve, and positioning through roads, streets, halls, fields, rooftops, woods, or fortress grounds. Each round, every participant chooses a maneuver in secret, the Game Master reveals or generates an obstacle, and the maneuvers are then resolved. The quarry may run on foot, ride a horse, flee in a wagon, or seek shelter behind walls and gates; the pursuer may give chase, cut off escape routes, block the way, or loose arrows from afar.|
| Push | Rerolling a failed check at a cost of Stress, Vitality, or Resolve depending on the attribute used. |
| Power | A magical rating used for spells and rituals, determining how many damage or ritual dice a character rolls. Its value varies by spellcasting type and related formulas. |
| Reaction | An action taken outside a character’s turn, usually in response to a trigger. Reactions cost action economy and sometimes Stamina depending on the rule. |
| Resolve | A derived mental durability stat used to resist fear, mental damage, and pressure. When it reaches 0, the character loses 1 Sanity and becomes Irrational. |
| Rest | A period of recovery, including Short Rest, Long Rest, and Full Rest. |
| Sanity | A derived mental resilience limit. Exceeding it has severe narrative and mechanical consequences determined by the GM. |
| Skill | A trained capability tied to an attribute and used for checks. Skills can have ranks and specializations. |
| Specialization | A focused sub-area of a skill. When it applies, it grants Advantage to the related check. |
| Speed | A movement value used to determine how far a character can move on their turn. The current text references a base speed of 30 ft. unless otherwise noted. |
| SL | Abbreviation for Success Level. |
| Social Conflict | A structured scene where one character tries to get something from another through words, leverage, fear, negotiation, or persuasion. |
| Social Superiority | A social momentum resource gained and spent during Social Conflict. It can improve social checks and may grant Advantage when spent in sufficient quantity. |
| Spellcaster | A keyword or character trait indicating access to magic-related talents, skills, and rules. |
| Staggered | A condition gained when Vitality reaches 0. It reduces Alacrity, breaks Concentration, and makes the character more vulnerable to further injury. |
| Stamina | A physical exertion resource used for movement, carrying, and other strenuous actions. It is also part of carrying capacity and recovery. |
| Stamina Invested | Stamina committed to carrying gear rather than spent temporarily. It represents encumbrance and returns when the load is reduced. |
| Stress | A cost paid when pushing a failed check and a broader resource or consequence tied to mental and physical strain. |
| Superiority | A shared momentum resource representing advantage in combat or social conflict. It is gained through preparation and success, and spent for bonuses or pressure. |
| Talent | A character feature that provides passive or active benefits, often with prerequisites. Talents can grant actions, reactions, desperate actions, or modifications to attributes and skills. |
| Threshold | A target amount that must be reached in an extended check. |
| Trait | A character feature that can affect attributes, derived stats, or requirements. The term appears broadly used, but not yet fully formalized in one single definition. |
| Vitality | A physical health resource. Damage reduces Vitality first; when it reaches 0, the character gains a Critical Wound and becomes Staggered. |
| Wound | A major injury tracked separately from Vitality. A character has a maximum number of Wounds, and exceeding that limit can be fatal. |
