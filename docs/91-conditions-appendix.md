# Conditions Cross-Reference Appendix

Conditions in 2d12 are described in the chapter where they first matter (Combat, Attributes, Sanity, Falling, Pursuit, Social Conflict). This appendix is the **single index** of every named condition the system uses, where it is fully defined, and what it does in one line. Use this page at the table; use the source chapter for the full rule.

If a condition is not listed here, it does not exist in the rules — treat the term as flavor.

## Read Order Of Severity

When two conditions overlap, apply the more severe first. **Major** conditions override **Minor** conditions of the same name; they do not stack.

A character may have any number of *different* conditions at once.

## Conditions By Trigger

A quick reverse index for the GM mid-scene.

- **Damage exceeds threshold:** Staggered, Critical Wound, Unconscious.
- **Stress exceeds Soak:** Bleeding (if blade), Ablaze (if fire), Poisoned (if envenomed), Stunned (if blunt/lightning).
- **Resolve reaches 0:** Irrational, Desperate, Madness gain.
- **Stamina reaches 0:** Out Of Breath.
- **Failure With Complication or Fumble:** Knockdown, Disarmed, Snare, Sundered, Guard Lost (GM choice).
- **Environmental:** Coughing, Frightened, Lost, Restrained, Blinded, Deafened.

## Conditions And Recovery

| How it ends | Conditions cleared |
|---|---|
| End of round | Blinded (1d10 rds), Stunned, Snared (with action) |
| End of scene | Coughing, Guard Lost, Surprised |
| Short Rest | Out Of Breath, Winded |
| Long Rest | Frightened, Stunned (long), Bleeding (after treatment) |
| Full Rest | Staggered (when Vitality recovers), Restrained (if treated) |
| Convalescence | Critical Wounds, lingering Poisoned, Irrational, Madness severity |
| Specific action | Ablaze (drop + roll), Bleeding (Medicine), Restrained (break free), Disarmed (pick up) |
| Never automatically | Unconscious (must be revived); permanent Critical Wound scars |

## Stacking Rules

- Conditions of the **same name** do not stack; the higher severity replaces the lower.
- Conditions of **different names** stack normally.
- Fatigue is the only condition that *adds*: each point is a separate −1/−1/−1.
- Persistent X is rerolled each turn; it does not increment.

When in doubt, the source chapter rules over this appendix. This page exists to speed lookup, not to override.
