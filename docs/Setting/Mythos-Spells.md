# Mythos Spells

# **Tome of Cthulhu**

The Great Old One Cthulhu's mind is so elemental that even while he dreams, his followers are able to use the power inherent in his thoughts, molding and shaping his arcane visions to affect change. Based on water magic, Cthulhu's spell tome is primarily used by his children, the Deep Ones, spawn of Father Dagon and Mother Hydra. Mortal spellcasters who worship the Great Old One may also draw upon his incantations to summon the power of the unwholesome deep.

***

## **Call of the Deep**

Just as the ocean depths call to those creatures swimming near the surface, beckoning them deeper, this spell awakens a powerful urge that compels those affected to move towards the nearest body of water, heedless of the harm it might cause them.

* **Skill:** Resilience  
* **Difficulty:** 2, opposed by target's Will + Resilience  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain. In addition, each Effect rolled on the cost adds 1 to Doom.  
* **Duration:** Until the end of the current scene  
* **Category:** Curse spell

**Spell Effect:** This spell targets a single living creature within Medium range. If the target is affected, it will move towards the nearest large body of water it can see (GM's discretion as to whether a body of water is large enough). The only actions the target may take are moving towards that body of water and trying to break free of the spell (a Will + Resilience test with a difficulty of 2). If the target reaches the body of water, they will enter the water and dive downwards until they either break free of the spell or drown. If the target cannot see a body of water, they may act normally, as if unaware of the spell's effects, but the spell remains in effect until they break free.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, the difficulty of any skill test to break free of the spell increases by +1 (this is not repeatable).  
* For 2 Fortune, the target moves towards the nearest large body of water, whether or not they can see it.

***

## **Curse of Cthulhu**

The spellcaster calls on Cthulhu's dreams made flesh and projects strange portents and terrible visions of the god who sleeps in the city of R'lyeh, sowing terror and confusion in his enemies' ranks. Individuals and units affected by the Curse become terrified and disorientated, losing focus on the task in hand and suffering significant damage to their willingness to fight.

* **Skill:** Academia  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 4 Drain, Piercing 1  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** All enemies within Close range suffer X mental stress, where X is equal to the spellcaster's power +1, with the Piercing 1 and Stun effects.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell affects all creatures within range, not just enemies.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, add the Persistent 4 effect to the damage; the persistent damage also has the Stun effect.

***

## **Echo of the Ocean**

The spellcaster calls forth an evocation of the ocean's limitless depths to strengthen and reinforce his allies. Anyone imbued with this mighty force feels the raw, surging energy of the waves channeled through them, boosting their strength, performance, and morale until they feel nigh on unstoppable, ready to crash upon their foes like a mighty tsunami.

* **Skill:** Persuasion  
* **Difficulty:** 1  
* **Cost:** 2 Drain  
* **Duration:** A number of rounds equal to the spellcaster's power  
* **Category:** Blessing spell

**Spell Effect:** The spellcaster, and all allies within Close range, inflict +1 stress on all their attacks—melee, ranged, and mental—and gain +2 Morale.

***

## **Gift of Cthulhu**

Using this spell, the spellcaster is able to temporarily take on the properties of a Deep One, even if they do not have a single iota of the Deep Ones' blood. Eyes bulge and become lidless, gill slits emerge from their neck, webbing appears between their fingers, and their feet elongate and take on a paddle-like quality. They are able to swim and breathe in the depths as if they were a partially transformed Deep One-human hybrid. Although the transformation is striking, the effects are not usually permanent, although a rare few suffer a profound longing to return to the comfort of the deeps.

* **Skill:** Survival  
* **Difficulty:** 2; if targeted at an unwilling victim, opposed by the target's Will + Resilience (Difficulty 2)  
* **Cost:** 4 Drain, Piercing 1  
* **Duration:** A number of days equal to the spellcaster's power or earlier if the spellcaster chooses to end the effect  
* **Category:** Transmutation spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell targets either the spellcaster or a single human within Reach. The target transforms, gaining the truth Deep One transmutation, representing their ability to breathe underwater, their ease at moving underwater, and their peculiar physical appearance. The target also gains an Athletics focus in Swimming. In addition, the character gains the Nauseating ability (page @@). When the spell's effects end, unwilling targets must roll to see if they develop a scar, as if they had recovered from a mental injury.

**Fortune:**

* If cast upon an unwilling victim, for 2 Fortune, the target also suffers 4 Piercing 2 mental damage.  
* If cast upon the spellcaster themselves, or a willing target, for 2 Fortune they may choose to suppress their transmutation for periods of up to an hour to allow them to hide without ending the effect.

***

## **Pool of Limpid Bliss**

Utilizing this unholy spell, the spellcaster causes a beautiful-looking pool to form which appears to gleam and glisten, containing beautiful bathing water, nymphs, and handsome satyrs. Any individual who passes within Close range of the pool's seductive area of effect must try to resist its watery allure. If they fail, they will throw down their weapons and attempt to bathe in its glorious waters, floating there before sinking to drown in liquid ecstasy.

* **Skill:** Resilience  
* **Difficulty:** 3  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain  
* **Duration:** Until the end of the current scene  
* **Category:** Manifestation spell

**Spell Effect:** Choose a zone within Medium range—a pool of water, woven with spells of attraction, appears in that zone. Enemies in that zone at the start of their turn must attempt a Will + Resilience test with a difficulty of 2. Failure means they suffer 4 mental stress with the Stun effect, and must move into the pool if not already within it (if stunned, they still move into the pool, compelled by the spell's effects). A character who is Defeated by mental stress caused by the pool will submerge themselves in the pool and begin Dying as they allow themselves to drown.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, increase the difficulty of all tests to resist the spell's effects by 1.  
* For 1 Fortune, increase the damage the spell inflicts by +1 per Fortune spent.  
* For 2 Fortune, add the Piercing 1 effect.

***

## **Swimmer in the Elder Current**

Harnessing this watery enchantment, the spellcaster transforms themselves and any nearby allies into a purely liquid form. While in this state they are impervious to all damage and can flow according to the spellcaster's will, escaping immediate harm and slipping away from danger.

* **Skill:** Survival  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 4 Drain, Stun  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Blessing spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell may affect the spellcaster and any of the spellcaster's allies within Close range, up to a number of targets equal to half the spellcaster's power (rounding up). The affected characters transform into water and flow away, reappearing moments later at any point within sight within Long range. Characters moved in this manner cannot take actions in their following turn, as if affected by the Stun effect.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, all characters transported by the spell receive +2 Cover until the start of the spellcaster's next turn.  
* For 2 Fortune, all characters transported by the spell other than the spellcaster recover stress equal to the spellcaster's Survival skill.

***

## **Wave Storm**

Summoning a maelstrom of spinning, unpredictable liquid fury, the spellcaster projects a column of tempestuous water at his foes, enveloping them in watery doom. The vortex is short-lived and unpredictable, blown this way and that as if by an ocean storm, but any caught in its embrace will often have breathed their last.

* **Skill:** Survival  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain, Piercing 1  
* **Duration:** A number of rounds equal to the spellcaster's power  
* **Category:** Summoning spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell conjures a vortex of water into a zone within Medium range. The vortex receives its own turn in the action order, but it cannot be attacked or suffer damage in any way. At the start of the vortex's turn, it will inflict physical stress equal to the spellcaster's power +1, with the Piercing 1 and Snare effects on all creatures and damageable objects within Close range of it, before moving to an adjacent zone, carrying any creatures it ensnares with it.

Each time the vortex moves, the spellcaster may choose the direction if they are within Medium range of it. If the spellcaster is further away from the vortex than that, then the vortex remains stationary. The GM may spend 1 Doom to move the vortex instead, when the spellcaster is further than Medium range from it.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell does not allow the spellcaster to control the vortex and does not require the GM to spend any Doom to move the vortex.

**Fortune:** The spellcaster may increase the damage the vortex deals by +1 per Fortune spent.

# **Compendium of Mormo**

Mormo, often associated with fertility, growth, and natural abominations, inspires magic of creation and transformation, frequently with chaotic, uncontrolled elements. Cultists often utilize spells that control and shape life in perverse and unnatural ways.

***

## **Acidic Transformation**

The spellcaster alters their own flesh into a corrosive substance, allowing them to inflict horrific injuries on those they come into contact with.

* **Skill:** Medicine  
* **Difficulty:** 3  
* **Cost:** 6 Drain, Piercing 2  
* **Category:** Transformation spell

**Spell Effect:** The spellcaster transforms their own flesh into a corrosive substance, inflicting damage on those they touch. The spellcaster's unarmed attacks inflict +Power physical damage with the Persistent 4 and Piercing 1 effects. Enemies who make melee attacks or grapple the spellcaster with their natural weapons or unarmed attacks suffer 3 physical stress with the Piercing 1 effect.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell means that the spellcaster's body corrodes itself too; the spellcaster will suffer 36 physical stress with the Drain effect at the end of every turn while the spell remains.

**Fortune:** For 1 Fortune, damage inflicted by this spell's effect gains the Vicious effect.

***

## **Black Goat's Blessing**

The spellcaster channels the energy of Mormo, invigorating their allies and making them more formidable.

* **Skill:** Resilience  
* **Difficulty:** 3  
* **Cost:** 4 Drain  
* **Duration:** Until the end of the current scene  
* **Category:** Blessing spell

**Spell Effect:** The spellcaster bestows the blessing of Mormo upon their allies. Choose a number of allies equal to your Power within Close range, increasing their Armor resistance by +2 and giving them the ability to regenerate 2 stress at the end of each turn.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell causes the recipients to mutate grotesquely, increasing their Complication range by +1.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, increase the stress regeneration by an additional 2.  
* For 1+ Fortune, the spell affects one additional ally for each Fortune spent.

***

## **Call the Corpse Eaters**

The spellcaster focuses, piercing the thin veil between dimensions to summon ghoul mobs to aid them.

* **Skill:** Persuasion  
* **Difficulty:** 1 plus the number of ghoul mobs  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain, Piercing 2  
* **Duration:** Until the end of the current scene  
* **Category:** Summoning spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell summons one, two, or three ghouls (see Chapter 8: Adversaries & Allies on page 215) to aid the spellcaster. If multiple ghouls are summoned, they will act as a mob. The ghouls obey the spellcaster's commands, though more demanding orders may require additional persuasion. The ghouls return to their realm when the spell ends.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell summons the ghouls, but they are not under the spellcaster's control.

**Fortune:** For 1+ Fortune, summon one additional ghoul per Fortune spent.

***

## **Foul Birthing**

The spellcaster targets a single allied NPC, infusing them with a latent spell that will trigger upon their defeat.

* **Skill:** Survival  
* **Difficulty:** 3  
* **Cost:** 4 Drain  
* **Duration:** Until the end of the current scene  
* **Category:** Transformation spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell infuses a single NPC with a latent transformation spell that will trigger upon their defeat, spawning an insect swarm or a rat swarm. The summoned creatures disperse when the spell ends.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell may trigger prematurely, causing the NPC to transform before they are defeated; the GM may spend 2 Doom to cause the spell's effect to trigger when they desire.

**Fortune:** The spawned creatures are under the spellcaster's control. This cost must be paid when the spell is cast.

***

## **Necrophagus**

The spellcaster calls upon dark magic to inflict harm on their enemies, gaining sustenance from their pain.

* **Skill:** Medicine  
* **Difficulty:** 4  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell targets a single enemy within Medium range, inflicting X physical stress, where X is the spellcaster's Power +2. The spellcaster or one of its allies within Close range can recover up to half the stress inflicted by this attack.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell does not allow the spellcaster or an ally to recover stress.

**Fortune:** For 1 Fortune, the spell gains the Vicious effect.

***

## **Sacrificial Rebirth**

The spellcaster sacrifices a creature, utilizing its life force to create a new, monstrous being loyal to the caster.

* **Skill:** Survival  
* **Difficulty:** 4  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain, Piercing 3  
* **Duration:** Until the end of the current scene  
* **Category:** Summoning spell

**Spell Effect:** The spellcaster sacrifices a creature within their Reach, its life force being consumed to summon a Foetid Young (see Chapter 8: Adversaries & Allies on page 240). The sacrifice must be either willing or completely immobile, no larger than Scale 0, and be a Trooper NPC, and they are slain instantly. The Foetid Young obeys the commands of the spellcaster until the end of the scene, after which it vanishes.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell summons the Foetid Young, but it is not under the spellcaster's control.

**Fortune:** For 2 Fortune, the Foetid Young is summoned with additional +1 Brawn and Resilience.

***

## **Whisper of the Woods**

The spellcaster imprints their will upon plant life, causing it to grow and change to obstruct enemies.

* **Skill:** Survival  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 3 Drain  
* **Duration:** Until the end of the current scene  
* **Category:** Control spell

**Spell Effect:** The spellcaster controls plant life within Medium range, causing it to grow rapidly and ensnare enemies. Select a single zone within Long range. Enemies within the affected zone must make an Athletics test with a Difficulty of 2 or become restrained, unable to move until they make a successful Athletics test on their turn.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell causes the plant life to grow out of control, affecting any creatures within the area, not only enemies.

**Fortune:** For 2 Fortune, affect one additional zone within range. This option may be used a maximum number of times equal to the spellcaster's Power.

# **Grimoire of Nyarlathotep**

The Messenger of the Outer Gods confers ominous boons upon his children and worshippers, sowing illusion, deception, delusion, and death. Those worshippers who wield Nyarlathotep's unholy magics do so at great cost to their own personal health and rationality.

Drawn from the power of the Dreamlands, the battlefield spells contained in the Grimoire of Nyarlathotep are among the foulest sorceries practiced by any faction in the Hidden Wars. The Crawling Chaos delights to see them inflicted upon friend and foe alike.

***

## **Delirium of the Dark Pharaoh**

Tapping into the sprawling chaos of the Dreamlands, the spellcaster calls forth the power of the Crawling Chaos, conjuring horrific visions and glimpses of strange dimensions and eerie planes beyond this earthly existence.

Characters find their willpower stretched to breaking point, and any who are unable to resist are gripped in an unearthly fervor. Delirious, they turn against each other or nearby allies until their temporary frenzy abates.

* **Skill:** Persuasion  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain; in addition, each Effect rolled on the cost adds 1 to Doom  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Control spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell targets a single creature within Medium range. The target suffers power rating +1 mental stress with the Persistent 5 damage effect.

If this spell would otherwise inflict one or more injuries on its target, either from the mental damage dealt or from the Persistent damage effect, then instead of sustaining injuries, the target is driven to a violent frenzy, and will attack the nearest creature, friend or foe, without thought of pain or danger. This frenzy ends at the end of the scene or until the target is Defeated.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, both the initial damage roll and subsequent rolls required by the Persistent damage effect gain the Piercing 1 damage effect.  
* For every 2 Fortune spent, the spell affects one additional target.

***

## **Lesser Black Rain**

Adapted from ancient Hyperborean magic, this spell summons a swirling maelstrom of lightning-flecked, thunderous black clouds. Once the maelstrom has formed, the spell cannot be halted, and soon after, the vortex rains down razor-sharp shards of jagged obsidian on any victims not under hard cover.

* **Skill:** Fighting  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 4 Drain, Piercing 1  
* **Duration:** A number of rounds equal to the spellcaster's power rating  
* **Category:** Manifestation spell

**Spell Effect:** The spellcaster selects a zone they can perceive within Long range, and a menacing storm gathers above that zone. The storm cannot be attacked or damaged in any way, and it receives its own turn in the action order. The storm does nothing on its first turn after being created, but on each of its subsequent turns, it inflicts power rating physical damage with the Piercing 1 damage effect on all creatures and damageable objects within its zone that are not within hard cover (a ceiling or object that fully impedes an attack).

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, add the Vicious damage effect.  
* For 2 Fortune, move the storm to any adjacent zone as a major action, requiring a Will + Resilience test with a difficulty of 2.

***

## **Mask of the Faceless Sphinx**

Calling on the power of the Crawling Chaos, the spellcaster invokes blessings for their allies, who gain the ability to cloak themselves in the aspect of one of the God of a Thousand Forms' most inscrutable incarnations, the Faceless Sphinx. While under this protection, their outlines seem to shift and shimmer, making them much harder to hit, while any damage they take is much reduced.

* **Skill:** Academia  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 4 Drain, Persistent 3  
* **Duration:** A number of rounds equal to the spellcaster's power rating  
* **Category:** Blessing spell

**Spell Effect:** The spellcaster and any allies within Close range when the spell is cast become harder to hit, increasing the difficulty of attacks against them by +1. Further, they count as having heavy cover.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, the difficulty increase for attacks against the affected characters increases to +2.  
* For 2 Fortune, affected characters receive +4 Cover resistance instead of the +2 Cover resistance normally granted by heavy cover.  
* For 3 Fortune, any ranged or melee attack that misses an affected character inflicts 4 mental damage with the Stun damage effect upon the attacker.

***

## **Rays of the Black Sun (Lesser Dread Equinox)**

Evoking the power of chaos and confusion, the spellcaster draws down and directs a scintilla of the rays of the black sun directly at the enemy.

* **Skill:** Fighting  
* **Difficulty:** 3  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain, Piercing 1  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell targets a single enemy within Medium range. The target suffers power rating +2 mental damage with the Piercing 1 and Snare damage effects.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, the spell gains the Persistent 4 damage effect.  
* The spell may affect one additional target for every 2 Fortune spent.

***

## **The Nameless Mists**

The spellcaster summons a small portion of the Dark Pharaoh's unholy sibling, the Nameless Mist, to Earth. There, it manifests as a restless, shifting, chaotic mist, lingering and burning with a horrible internal intensity, damaging anyone caught in its unholy tendrils.

* **Skill:** Academia  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain; in addition, each Effect rolled on the cost adds 1 to Doom  
* **Duration:** A number of rounds equal to the spellcaster's power  
* **Category:** Manifestation spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell conjures a foul mist that fills the spellcaster's current zone. Any enemy within the mist at the end of their turn suffers mental damage equal to the spellcaster's power rating +1, with the Piercing 2 and Stun damage effects. In addition, the zone gains a location truth—Shifting, Vile Mists—that hinders vision into, out of, and through the zone, though only for the spellcaster's enemies.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell affects all creatures, not just enemies.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, the spell also gains the Drain damage effect.  
* The spell may extend the mist out to one or more adjacent zones for 2 Fortune per additional zone filled.

***

## **The Shape of Hate**

Devotees anointed with this blessing of Nyarlathotep are seized with battle fury and sprint straight at the enemy, desperate to get into melee and tear them to shreds.

* **Skill:** Academia  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 4 Drain, Piercing 1  
* **Duration:** A number of rounds equal to the spellcaster's power rating  
* **Category:** Blessing spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell affects a number of allies within Close range equal to the spellcaster's power rating; this number may include or exclude the spellcaster at their discretion. When the spell is first cast, the affected characters immediately make a minor action to move toward the nearest enemy. In addition, affected allies inflict +2 damage on all melee attacks.

**Fortune:** For 2 Fortune, when any affected ally makes a successful melee attack, they may make a second melee attack at +1 difficulty.

***

## **Touch of the Black Sun**

This spell projects a torrent of jet-black flames from the spellcaster's hands directed at nearby enemies, causing their skin to writhe and burn.

* **Skill:** Fighting  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 4 Drain, Piercing 1  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell targets an enemy within Close range, inflicting power rating +2 physical damage with the Area and Persistent 4 damage effects. The spellcaster cannot affect themselves with this spell.

**Fortune:** For 2 Fortune, the spell gains the Intense damage effect.

# **Spellbook of Yog-Sothoth**

Yog-Sothoth, the Outer God, is both keeper of all knowledge and coterminous with all time and space and so its magic is based on unlocking gateways, freeing forbidden knowledge, and revealing the hidden pathways which open believers' eyes to the true nature of reality. Unlike Nyarlathotep, Yog-Sothoth is not malign in the traditional sense, but welcomes those who would gaze upon the true state of the multiverse, even if it might drive them insane. Even though one aspect of it is trapped with the black sun, the god sometimes manifests itself as a succession of gleaming vortexes and spheres. Occasionally it has been known to interfere in the affairs of mortals, impregnating them to create monstrous hybrids.

***

## **Binding of Yog-Sothoth**

Calling on the chaotic aspects of the Outer God to confuse and mislead, the sorcerer binds all enemies nearby with this spell. While it does no direct physical damage, those who succumb to its deleterious effects are overcome, becoming rooted to the spot, unable to move, attack, or defend themselves.

* **Skill:** Persuasion  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Control spell

**Spell Effect:** All enemies within Close range suffer power +2 mental stress with the Snare effect. Enemies who would suffer one or more injuries from this spell are paralyzed instead of suffering injuries. Paralyzed enemies may not take any action other than to try to break free, requiring a successful Will + Resilience test with a Difficulty of 3.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell affects all creatures within Close range, not just enemies.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, the spell gains the Piercing 1 effect.  
* For 2 Fortune, the spell gains the Persistent 3 effect; the Persistent damage also has the Snare effect.

***

## **Foetid Touch of Aforgomon**

Sometimes the Outer God can be persuaded to 'favor' mortals with its direct touch, impregnating them with its unholy seed. Some may be able to resist these foul effects but, if possessed, they slowly begin to transform into the fiendish Bloodborn.

* **Skill:** Fighting  
* **Difficulty:** 3, opposed by the target's Agility + Fighting (Difficulty 3)  
* **Cost:** 7 Drain. In addition, each Effect rolled on the cost adds 1 to Doom  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell targets a single enemy within Reach. If the spell is successfully cast, the target suffers power +1 physical stress with the Persistent 3 effect. Any character Defeated by this spell immediately starts dying, and if they die, a Bloodborn (see Chapter 8: Adversaries & Allies on page 2n) will emerge from the character's remains in the following round.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, the spell gains the Piercing 2 effect, applied to both the initial stress and any stress caused by the Persistent effect.  
* For 2 Fortune, the spell gains the Intense effect, which applies to both the initial stress and any caused by the Persistent effect.

***

## **Gaze of the Multiverse**

Yog-Sothoth is the key and the gate, the keeper of hidden knowledge. Using this spell, the spellcaster opens the victim's eyes to the true nature of the cold, harsh, unyielding universe which has the nuclear chaos of the mad, blind god Azathoth at its center. Those affected by Gaze of the Multiverse are overcome by the overwhelming nature of this revelation and lose coherence and focus for its duration.

* **Skill:** Academia  
* **Difficulty:** 3  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain, Piercing 2  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** All enemies within Close range of the spellcaster suffer power +2 mental stress, with the Stun effect.

**Flawed:** A flawed version of this spell affects all creatures within Close range, not just enemies.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, the spell gains the Drain effect.  
* For 2 Fortune, the spell gains the Vicious effect.

***

## **Loathsome Bond of 'Umr At-Tawil**

Yog-Sothoth is the warden of forbidden knowledge and this fearsome spell is aimed at breaking a foe's morale. Using this loathsome incantation, the sorcerer not only does substantial harm, but siphons off a portion of their foe's life force to replenish their own.

* **Skill:** Persuasion  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 4 Drain. In addition, each Effect rolled on the cost adds 1 to Doom  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell targets each enemy within Close range and inflicts power +1 mental stress with the Drain and Piercing 1 effects. In addition, if the spell inflicts one or more injuries, the spellcaster may remove one of their own injuries.

**Flawed:** If a flawed version of this spell is cast, it targets every creature within Close range, not just enemies.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, the spell gains the Piercing 2 effect.  
* For 2 Fortune, the spell gains the Intense effect.  
* For 2 Fortune, the spellcaster removes one injury for each injury inflicted.

***

## **Way of the Gatekeeper**

Yog-Sothoth is the opener of the ways, and guardian of the hidden paths, and casters are able to draw on this forbidden knowledge to move themselves and their allies swiftly and silently across the battlefield, sometimes appearing deep behind enemy lines, or using these hidden dimensional byways to evade imminent danger.

* **Skill:** Academia  
* **Difficulty:** 2  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain, Piercing 2  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Manifestation spell

**Spell Effect:** The spellcaster transports a number of willing allies in Close range equal to half their power, rounding up; this may include or exclude themselves. The affected characters vanish and reappear in any zone within Long range.

**Fortune:**

* For 2 Fortune, the maximum number of affected allies is equal to the spellcaster's power, rather than only half.  
* For 2 Fortune, the affected characters may reappear in a location within a mile which the spellcaster is familiar with (they must have spent at least an hour in that location previously).

***

## **Spheres of Yog-Sothoth**

The sorcerer summons a small portion of Yog-Sothoth's being to this plane, in the form of a series of ephemeral spheres glowing with a pallid unholy light, then directs them at their enemies who wilt under the Outer God's unclean touch.

* **Skill:** Fighting  
* **Difficulty:** Varies, see below  
* **Cost:** 5 Drain. In addition, each Effect rolled on the cost adds 1 to Doom  
* **Duration:** Instant  
* **Category:** Attack spell

**Spell Effect:** The spell conjures one, two, or three spheres, which the spellcaster hurls at enemies within Medium range. The difficulty of the spell is equal to the number of spheres summoned. Each sphere targets a different enemy, and each enemy hit suffers power +2 physical stress with the Drain and Stun effects.

**Fortune:** For 2 Fortune, the spell gains the Piercing 2 effect.

***

# **Rituals**

The following rituals are examples from the Cthulhu Mythos and are drawn from the traditions which have their own forms of ritualistic magic. These should also serve as inspiration and guidance for any GMs or players wanting to create their own rituals. Where a value has a + sign this indicates that it varies at the GM's discretion, and the value given is the minimum requirement.

## **Alter Weather**

This control weather ritual allows the spellcaster to shape and control meteorological conditions. Most magical traditions have a version of this ritual and, while the components and specifics vary, the sorcerer who is its focus only has to name the effect desired to activate it at the culmination of the ritual.

* **Requirements:** Ritualistic tools (varies)  
* **Test:** Survival, Difficulty 2  
* **Stress:** 5, plus double the ritual's Steps  
* **Steps:** 1+  
* **Cost:** 3 Drain  
* **Duration:** A number of hours equal to the spellcaster's Power

**Outcome:** The spellcaster may evoke any weather condition they can name, from "bright and sunny" to "blinding snow and hail," and that weather condition will persist in a local area of the spellcaster's choosing equal to one mile per step for the spell's duration.

**Miscast:** A weather effect that is the exact opposite of the spellcaster's choosing, as determined by the GM, will instead affect the chosen area and the spellcaster will suffer 5 mental stress with the Drain effect.

## **Astral Walker**

This ritual allows the spellcaster to detach their consciousness from their physical body so that their ethereal form roves across the multiverse, exploring locations and dimensions that are hidden from the eyes of mortal men.

* **Requirements:** Ritual tools, libations  
* **Test:** Resilience, Difficulty 1  
* **Maximum Stress:** 6, plus twice the ritual's Steps  
* **Steps:** 1+  
* **Cost:** 3 Drain  
* **Duration:** A maximum number of days equal to the spellcaster's power

**Outcome:** The spellcaster's spirit leaves their physical body, allowing it to travel far and wide, in this dimension and many others. The character's spirit is treated exactly like their body, but while disembodied in this manner, they gain the Incorporeal special rule with a rating equal to their power, which is only ignored by magical attacks, and are entirely invisible to mundane senses.

As a disembodied spirit, the character can reach normally inaccessible places. The ability of the character to travel varies based on the Steps of the ritual.

* At Step 1, the character's spirit enters a place of mental refuge, isolated from the world, taking a form based on the character's nature and beliefs. If the character is a traditional spellcaster, others of their tradition can also enter this refuge, allowing them to commune and converse in safety; this is often used for mentors and students to commune over vast distances.  
* At Step 2, the character can traverse the material world as a spirit, able to walk or run through the world at their normal movement speed.  
* At Step 3, the character can move more swiftly through the material world, traveling miles in seconds.  
* At Step 4, the character can enter and navigate other dimensions, though doing so without proper knowledge is extremely perilous.

While the character is invisible to mundane senses, they may still be visible to supernatural entities and Mythos creatures. If the character takes one or more injuries while under the effects of this ritual, the ritual's effects end immediately, and the character finds their body has suffered the same injuries.

Those who study the spells of Yog-Sothoth are especially adept at performing this rite, reducing the difficulty of tests to perform it by 1.

**Miscast:** The character's spirit departs, but it does not head to where they intend. Rather, they see glimpses of numerous other dimensions and places, and receive confusing, horrific visions. They take 6 mental stress, with the Drain and Vicious effects, and then the spell's effects abruptly end.

## **Banishment**

This ritual is most often used to cast out demons and malevolent spirits possessing a person, but it has also been adopted and adapted to cast out any power possessing a place or object.

* **Test:** Persuasion, Difficulty 2+  
* **Requirements:** Ritual Tools, libations, presence of possessed individual  
* **Maximum Stress:** 8  
* **Steps:** 3  
* **Cost:** 4 Intense  
* **Duration:** Instant

**Outcome:** Make a Will + Persuasion test against the possessing entity at a difficulty set by the GM, normally equal to the number of Injuries the entity can withstand, with the person possessed acting as the spellcaster's assistant. At each breakthrough this ritual inflicts 8 mental stress, with the Piercing 1 effect, to the possessing entity. If the possessing entity is Defeated it leaves its victim and may never return.

It should be noted that this spell will banish the mental influence of alien entities, or malevolent sorcerers who have exerted power over an individual's mind.

**Miscast:** An unsuccessful cast inflicts 4 mental stress upon both spellcaster and possessed victim and may also incur further complications, such as lingering physical and mental ailments.

## **Commune with Deity**

The ability to commune with some of the great powers is a powerful one, but not one that should be attempted lightly. Communing with strange, alien Mythos entities such as Azathoth or Yog-Sothoth in search of arcane knowledge is a quick and dangerous route to power and the price is likely to be extremely high. Many find their mental stability ripped away, and the spellcaster's immortal soul is often imperiled, for few such favors are given for free.

* **Requirements:** Ritual tools, a blood sacrifice at minimum will be necessary, and more may be required for the powerful Mythos deities  
* **Test:** Persuasion, Difficulty 3+  
* **Maximum Stress:** 15  
* **Steps:** 3  
* **Cost:** 4 Drain, Piercing 1  
* **Duration:** Varies

**Outcome:** Commune rituals are less complicated forms of the Evoke/Dismiss Deity ritual and, if successfully cast, the spellcaster is able to contact a Deity, Outer God, Great Old One, or Elder God and ask directly for guidance, boons, or favors. Successfully communing with a god may take a number of forms and is unlikely to be a simple question-and-answer style conversation. Responses are more likely to be made in the form of visions, prophecies, portents, and omens, which must be interpreted carefully to discern their true meaning and significance.

With some Mythos deities, such as Nyarlathotep or Cthulhu, the cost is less likely to be onerous, as these entities often seek to contact and influence mankind. Spellcasters from native Earth traditions are able to commune with their higher powers much more easily and at far less cost. A Celtic wise woman or Runic mystic, for example, is able to attempt to contact their patron with far less onerous penalties, apart from the ignominy of being ignored if they are unsuccessful. Still, all gods and deities, earthborn or alien, have a deserved reputation for capriciousness and are unlikely to simply give away knowledge or power unearned, perhaps seeking deeds or favors in return, though they may look kindly on a spellcaster's motivation or past conduct which favors their aims and ambitions.

**Miscast:** The spellcaster is unable to contact their chosen deity and loses a Fortune point due to the ignominy of their failure.

## **Create Ward**

This enchantment creates a magical symbol with powerful anti-Mythos properties, repelling followers and minions of the Outer Gods, as well as barring the way to those gods and entities themselves.

Each tradition has its own unique symbol: a Runic mystic might draw and activate the properties of Algiz, the rune of protection, while a Celtic wise woman might enchant a triskelion or figure from the Ogham script. This ritual may also be used to create and enchant the universal Elder Sign, often identified as a pillar of flame within a five-sided star, a traditional and powerful protective symbol against the gods of the Mythos and their servants.

* **Requirements:** Ritual tools, suitable material to draw or engrave the Ward  
* **Test:** Persuasion Difficulty 2  
* **Maximum Stress:** 10  
* **Steps:** 1+  
* **Cost:** 3 Drain  
* **Duration:** Variable

**Outcome:** The spellcaster draws or engraves the symbol onto a suitable medium and, once the spell is successfully cast, the symbol is activated. Mythos entities attempting to move into Close range of the symbol are repulsed by its power, suffering 8 physical stress with the Piercing 2 and Stun effects. If a creature takes one or more injuries from the damage, they are physically forced out of the warded zone and knocked prone. A creature Defeated by this damage is banished to its home dimension.

The duration of the warding depends on the ritual's number of Steps:

* With 1 Step, the ward is quickly created, using salt, purified water, silver dust, holy oils, or similar materials poured on the ground, and the ward lasts for a number of hours equal to the spellcaster's power.  
* With 2 Steps, the ward is painted onto the floor with specially prepared pigments and lasts a number of days equal to the spellcaster's power.  
* With 3 Steps, a lasting etching, engraving, or other mark is left, and the ward remains for a number of weeks equal to the spellcaster's power.  
* With 4 Steps, the ward is a lasting mark in a surface, which is then filled and anointed with prepared and sanctified pigments, lasting for a number of months equal to the spellcaster's power.

**Miscast:** Either through a mistake in its depiction, or a failure to imbue it with magical energy, the ward will not provide its usual protective effects; the spellcaster will not know this, and the player should act accordingly. However, the flawed ward is immediately obvious to any Mythos creature in the vicinity, who will know that the ward does not work.

## **Descry Enchantment**

This ritual allows the spellcaster to identify magic which has been cast in a particular area, or on a person or object. It is also able to help identify the malevolent nature of curses, evil eyes, or enchantments, as well as the benign effects of sympathetic magic.

* **Requirements:** Ritual Tools  
* **Test:** Observation, Difficulty 1+  
* **Maximum Stress:** 5  
* **Steps:** 1  
* **Cost:** 2 Drain  
* **Duration:** Instant

**Outcome:** Once successfully cast, the spellcaster gains knowledge of the magic within the immediate area, or upon a person or object within sight. When successfully cast, the spellcaster gains three Fortune, which may only be spent to Obtain Information. Questions asked must relate to magic or supernatural effects in a location, or upon an object or person, that the spellcaster is looking at when the spell is cast.

**Miscast:** The spellcaster suffers no additional stress, but the GM will supply them with false, faulty, or imperfect information about the spell, object, or person they are enquiring about.
