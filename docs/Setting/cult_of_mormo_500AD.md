# THE CULT OF MORMO

*Aetas Obscura — Year of the Consulship of Patricius and Hypatius · Anno Domini 500*

> *"Your terrifying armor makes me dizzy. I beg you, take away that Mormo!"*
>
>-Aristophanes, The Acharnians (425 B.C.)
>
> *"Oh, what a trembling the Mormo brought us then, when we were little ones! -- On its head were huge ears, and it walked on all fours, and changed from one face to another!"*
>
>-Erinna, The Distaff (c. 320 B.C.)
>
> *"O friend and companion of night, thou who rejoicest in the baying of dogs and spilt blood, who wanderest in the midst of shades among the tombs, who longest for blood and bringest terror to mortals, Gorgo, Mormo, thousand-faced moon, look favorably on our sacrifices!"*
>
>-invocation to Hecate/Mormo, recorded by Hippolytus of Rome (c. 220 A.D.)
>
> *"They told us the Mother of God would burn the old she-goat out of the dark. They did not tell us that the woman in blue was older than the goat, and hungrier."*
>
>-marginal gloss in a Faiyum psalter, recovered by the Magdalene Circle (c. 488 A.D.)

Mormo is a secret name praised by the inner circles of countless seemingly unrelated cults, a name given to a fecund god who is ancient beyond reckoning. She is given other names and epithets, such as the Black Goat of the Woods with a Thousand Young, All-Mother, the Mighty Mother, and the Lord of the Woods. The Concord of the Seven Seals files her, with their usual cold precision, as a near-aspect of **Shub-Niggurath** — the fertility-horror of the Outer Dark — and treats the name "Mormo" as one of the masks that aspect wears in the Mediterranean and the northern forests. Though the numbers of her hardened adherents are fortunately few, the Cult of Mormo has a long reach and pervasive influence, scattered everywhere humans have gathered, concentrated most heavily in a few dark corners where they are free to thrive. The cult has spread by latching onto certain vulnerable minds and taking root under the guise of more benevolent fertility cults. Unlike most fertility gods who are praised and beloved, Mormo is feared and shunned outside the circle of her devotees. The growth she fosters is cancerous, predatory, and parasitic. Her cult is an adversary of civilization and an insidious cabal that serves to pave the way for the Outer Ones.
At her core, Mormo represents how easily the boundless fertility of life can be turned upon itself, a trait given expression in the spread of infection and disease, as well as among great predatory beasts. Mormo's dominion is occupied by all living things that invade the niches once occupied by others, those that subdue and overwhelm rival creatures. The wolf that stalks the weak and infirm, as well as the one that succumbs to cancerous growths in its jaws, the deer blinded by flies hatching in its eyes, these are signs of Mormo's passage. She also exists in the fungal forms spreading under the soil of forests, delivering spores to break down the flesh of the dead or rotting masses of vegetation. She is a god of life at its most violent, but also of decay and rot.

In the **Darkening Age**, with the Western Empire a generation in its grave and the old gods
starving as their worship-pools drain into Christ, Mormo is one of the few powers of her kind
that has not weakened. She does not need temples or named priesthoods; she feeds on plague,
on famine, on the rotting of a civilization, and the fifth century has fed her well. Where the
dying Nephilim gods grow thin, Mormo grows fat on their corpses.

The Cult of Mormo has no large hierarchy or central leadership, and in each region where it has spread it has assumed different shapes. Like outcroppings of toadstools or powdery stumps separated by miles, they may share a deeper root structure of strands and threads connecting them as a single greater being. Each of its smaller cults engages in its own rites to praise the god, seeking converts and slowly twisting their minds through exposure to the deeper lore of Mormo. Her priests serve as willing conduits for vast and malevolent alien intelligences seeking to secure a foothold on Earth. The branches of the cult are loosely aligned and require no organized conspiracy or collusion, only a willingness to conduct rites to Mormo and thereby summon and nurture her diverse children, a brood of otherworldly horrors.

The cult's priests are predominantly but not exclusively women, in part because many of its adherents are former members of female priesthoods. The cult often arises amid more traditional fertility faiths, and seeks to co-opt, infiltrate, and eventually absorb and replace them. For this reason, and given the nature of their beliefs, the Cult of Mormo is an existential threat to these faiths. Wherever the cult is revealed, bitter conflict ensues. Organizations in the old Roman heartlands, in Ægyptus, in Britannia, and elsewhere have been founded with the primary goal of exposing and expunging Mormo's cultists. The most tenacious of these hunters now wear the blue of the Mother of God — but that is a later page in this telling. The various branches of the cult recognize a myriad of holy days, but the vernal equinox is especially significant to all, its eve is considered the "Day of Blood." The greatest of sacrificial rites are conducted at this time.

## PRIMEVAL HARBINGER

Even knowledgeable members of the Cult of Mormo are ignorant of the true origins and unfathomable antiquity of their god. They say she has existed since the world began, and credit her with the germination of life, but even this does not encompass the deeper truth. Mormo is not a terrestrial god but is a name given to a fragment of one of the Outer Gods, a being worshiped on thousands of worlds across vast eons, persisting wherever life has arisen and thinking beings evolved.

Precursors of this cult lurked at the fringes of the Hyperborean Empire and served as a harbinger to allow other cults of the Outer Gods to spread and thrive. Mormo is a facilitator and ally of Nyarlathotep — the **Crawling Chaos**, whose mortal instrument in this age is the Brotherhood of the Black Pharaoh — and has standing within the unfathomable court of Azathoth. She has mated with Yog-Sothoth to give rise to an entire lineage of terrifying gods. She is worshiped, under other names, by many creatures other than humanity, including Mi-Go and Elder Things. She exists to facilitate Mythos invasion into any place where intelligent life arises, given these places serve as havens for their greatest rivals: the Elder Gods, whom the Concord and the Twilight Courts strain to keep at the gates.

The mind of Mormo is impossible to fathom, and she does not engage in plotting and scheming as mortals understand it. She does not display the same degree of active will as her frequent ally, Nyarlathotep, and she rarely bestows messages or prophecies, though dreams of ill-omen are attributed to her. Her power manifests in the spawning of monstrous horrors, in the spread of fungal lifeforms and disease, and her favor is shown by being spared when her children begin to feast. And while Mormo is most often depicted as female, the Black Goat exists as an interconnected male aspect of the divinity, one revered by certain offshoots of the cult.

## IN LEGEND AND MYTH

General lore about Mormo is fragmented and difficult to unearth, even by otherwise knowledgeable occultists. The name is most commonly associated with Greek legends about monstrous winged child-slaying creatures or demons, sometimes conflated with the lamia. This creature is associated with Hecate, the god of magic and ill omen. Some of Hecate's cultists consider Mormo to be a dangerous messenger or spiritual guide who can provide an introduction to their patron if appeased with sacrificial blood, preferably that of children or the young.

Legends of Mormo trace back to prehistoric times, passed down by oral tradition. The cult may have its Earthly origins in neolithic times at the onset of agriculture, when grain stores were infected with ergot, a fungal parasite, inducing hallucinations connecting receptive minds to this primeval divinity. Even the cult's earliest members felt the compulsion to give Mormo blood offerings, the most sacred drawn from their own offspring. By such rites they received visions instructing them in the methods to summon Mormo's diverse progeny.

Stories suggest a cabal of Sumerian magi turned to such rites two millennia before the founding of Rome, inspired to seek Mormo in a time of war and collapse. Their summoning rites resulted in the Eridu Breach and the appearance of a terrifying being they called the Ancient Mother, also referred to as Shuba-Nigur-Urash, words meaning "Shining Terrible Secret One of Earth" in a now extinct variant of Sumerian. This is thought to have been a corruption of an older name, Mash-Ngi, which means both "Black Goat" and "Dreams of Omen." The Concord's Inner Seal holds — and does not publish — that the Eridu Breach the Sumerian seven tore open is the *same wound* the Court of Long Night has bled to hold shut for four thousand years, and that every rite worked to Mormo puts a feather's weight more pressure on that ancient seal. The fate of those magi is unclear, though aspects of the cult endured in the lands east of Rome.
The tie between Mormo and the black goat persisted in iconography associated with the god, found on murals and pottery. Cave inscriptions where the cult sought shelter depict thrashing beasts with a myriad of thorned branches and writhing tentacles, possibly an indication that some of these groups had learned how to summon the god's offspring. The greatest branches of the Cult of Mormo, such as the one that would arise in the dark forests of Germania, recovered rites allowing their most blessed adherents to become the Reborn, also known as Goats of the Mother. Transfigured to become more goat than human, these creatures were thought to enter communion with Mormo, their frenzied attacks guided by the god. Many worshipers of Mormo would join battle wearing terrifying goat masks and hides to present the appearance of being Reborn even when they had not in fact received this blessing.

## ANCIENT FERTILITY CULTS
The existence of Mormo may be viewed as a dark and sinister mirror of the countless traditional fertility faiths existing across these regions, the majority of which have earned a place of deserved respect within their respective pantheons. For most of the cult's history these were the matriarchal faiths of an older world — Cybele, Magna Mater, Freya, Ceres, Isis, Tawaret, Diana — and the Cult of Mormo throve by infiltrating them and turning the resentment of women against the patriarchal order of Rome. **That world is now dying.** In 500 AD the pagan fertility cults that Mormo once parasitized are themselves starving, their altars cold, their worshippers baptized. The Concord's quiet judgment is that the old gods behind those altars — the Nephilim — are guttering out one by one as Christ drains their pools.

This collapse has changed the cult's hunting. Where once Mormo's priests hid inside the temple of the Magna Mater, they now find the temple half-abandoned and its faithful gone over to the Church. The cult has adapted as it always does: it has begun to follow the worship. Some sects have crept inside Christianity itself — but here they have discovered, to their terror, that they are not the only predator in that particular field. (See *The War of the Two Mothers* below.)
The traditional fertility cults that survive are still quite strong in a few backwaters and possess their own mystical traditions and powers. They seek to find and root out any infiltrators of the Cult of Mormo in their midst. While cultists of Mormo lurk within the faiths of gods such as Freya, Cybele, Magna Mater, Juno, Ceres, Isis, Tawaret, Hubaba, Diana, and others, this does not mean the Cult of Mormo and these other cults are one and the same. It is best to view the Cult of Mormo as a dangerous recurring infection afflicting these more traditional faiths — an infection that, in this age, has learned to move into a new and far larger host.

## THE WAR OF THE TWO MOTHERS

Of all the enemies the Cult of Mormo has made across its long history,
the most ancient and most implacable is **Isis**.

In the deep lore guarded by the Concord's Inner Seal, Isis is no mere goddess but one of the **Nephilim** — the half-divine, worship-hungry survivors of the union of the Watchers and mortal women, the true beings behind every pagan altar. Among that ancient brood, Isis is reckoned a *mother-power*: a Nephil whose worship-pool was always built on grieving women, on childbirth, on the protection of the vulnerable, and on the promise of resurrection. From her earliest dynasties along the Nile she has regarded Mormo as her exact and obscene inversion — a mother-goddess whose fecundity is cancer, whose children devour, whose milk is rot. The war between them is older than Rome, older than the Pyramids in their present form. The active cults of Isis dedicated to the obliteration of the Cult of Mormo, mentioned wherever the two faiths touch, are merely the mortal face of a feud between two immortal mothers that has run, hot and cold, for four thousand years.

For most of that war Isis fought as Isis — openly, under her own name and blue mantle, from her own temples. But by the close of the fourth century her temples were closing, her Nile shrines were being torn down by Christian mobs, and her worship-pool was draining away exactly as every other Nephil's was. A goddess starved of worship grows weak, sleeps, and dies in truth. Isis was losing — not to Mormo, but to the dead Galilean's redirected reputation and the Nephil who fed on it.

**So Isis did the most dangerous and brilliant thing of her long life: she changed masks.**

Around her there had risen, in the new Christian Empire, a devotion the bishops themselves had not fully sanctioned and could not quite suppress — the cult of the **Mother of God**, *Theotokos*, the grieving virgin who cradles her murdered son and intercedes for the desperate. To the eye of a four-thousand-year-old mother-Nephil, the resemblance was not a resemblance at all. The mourning mother. The lost-and- restored son. The mantle of blue. The protectress of women in childbirth and sailors at sea. It was *her own iconography*, walking into a faith that was swallowing the world. And so Isis stepped quietly into the avatar of **Mother Mary**, and began to drink.

### What the Marian Campaign Actually Is

The Concord understands the public history of Christianity through the bitterest doctrine it keeps: that the canonical Church is a **worship-heist**, the redirected reputation of the murdered Gnostic teacher Yeshua of Nazareth poured into the pool of the Nephil **Jehovah**, the only god in the Empire whose cult is *growing* rather than dying. Every baptism feeds him. He is, by the Inner Seal's reckoning, the single most dangerous Nephil in the Mediterranean.

What almost no one yet understands — not the bishops, not the Brotherhood, and crucially **not Jehovah** — is that a second mouth has fastened onto the same pool. Isis, wearing the Marian avatar, is **a quiet parasite on Jehovah's worship-flow.** Every prayer to the Mother of God, every lamp lit before the blue-mantled Virgin, every sailor's vow to the Star of the Sea, is a prayer that the worshipper believes flows to Christ and his Church — and a measurable fraction of it flows instead to a hidden Egyptian mother-goddess feeding in the dark. She does not contest Jehovah's claim. She does not announce herself. She has not declared the war she is plainly fighting. She simply *eats*, carefully, beneath his notice, rebuilding a worship-pool that the closing of her temples had nearly drained — and she spends every drop of it on the one project that has obsessed her for four millennia: the extermination of Mormo.

The Inner Seal regards this as the most precarious arrangement in the hidden world. Three truths balance on a knife:

- **If Jehovah notices**, the most dangerous Nephil in the Empire will turn on a rival feeding inside his own cult, and the resulting Nephil-on-Nephil war will be fought through every Christian congregation between Alexandria and Tolosa.
- **As long as he does not**, Isis grows stronger every year that the Church grows, and her Marian hunters become a more and more effective scourge of Mormo's sects.
- **Mormo, alone among the powers, may benefit from being hunted** — because the harder   the Marian cult presses, the more attention it draws, and the more likely Jehovah is to look   down and see the thief in his own house.

### The Marian Hunters

In practical terms, the cult's priests are now opposed in region after region by something new and frightening: bands of devout Christians — many of them women, widows and consecrated virgins, ascetics and grieving mothers — who hunt Mormo's sects with a power that *works*. It works not because their faith is true but because Isis, the Nephil wearing the Marian mask, is *spending the worship-harvest back on them*: she skims a sliver of magical potential from every prayer they offer her, and lends portions of it back as the holy power they wield against Mormo. They believe with their whole hearts that they serve the Mother of God and her Son. They do not know that the Mother who answers them is older than Bethlehem and Egyptian to the bone, nor that the power in their hands was harvested from their own devotions and loaned out again. They do not need to know. Their devotion feeds her, and so she arms them.

These hunters are most concentrated where Isis's old strength was greatest — Ægyptus above all — but the Marian devotion is portable in a way her Nile temples never were, and her hunters now turn up wherever the Church has gone. A Mormo sect that has spent three centuries learning to hide from pagan priestesses and Roman magistrates finds itself, in 500 AD, exposed from an unexpected direction: by a faith it had assumed was just another host to be infested, which has turned out to harbor its most ancient predator.

The Cult of Mormo's higher priests have begun, in fragments and through screaming dreams, to *understand* this — to grasp that the woman in blue who burns their spawning-grounds is not the new god's mother at all, but the old Egyptian enemy in a stolen mask. A very few of them have grasped the further implication: that if they could only make Jehovah *notice* his hidden parasite, they might set the two greatest mothers of the age at each other's throats and slip away in the smoke. This is the most dangerous game any Mormo sect can play, and the Brotherhood of the Black Pharaoh — who would love nothing better than a Nephil civil war inside the Church — watches for any cult bold enough to attempt it.

## HISTORY OF THE CULT, 165–500 AD

The body of lore above describes the cult as it has always been. What follows is what *happened
to it* across the long catastrophe of the late Empire — the three and a half centuries between the
old chronicles (which the cult's own archives date to the Antonine plague years, around 165 AD)
and the Darkening Age in which your game is set.

### The Antonine Sickness and the First Feast (c. 165–180 AD)

The chronicles of the cult that survive into 500 AD begin in earnest with a plague. When the
legions returning from Parthia brought a great sickness into the Empire, the Cult of Mormo
read it — correctly — as their goddess feeding on an unprecedented scale, and many sects
emerged from hiding to hasten and worship the dying. This was the cult's high-water mark of
*visibility* in the Roman world. It was also a mistake. The plague years taught the Empire's
authorities and the surviving pagan priesthoods to recognize Mormo's hand, and the
persecutions that followed drove the cult back underground for generations.

### The Crisis Century (c. 235–284 AD)

The fifty years of civil war, usurpation, and invasion that nearly destroyed the Empire were a
banquet. The cult thrived in the chaos, particularly along the Rhine and Danube frontiers and
in the great cities where order had collapsed. Mormo's children walked more freely in this period
than at any time since. But the cult never learned to build; it only fed. When Diocletian and his
successors restored order, the Cult of Mormo had grown bloated and careless, and the restored
state hunted it hard.

### The Christian Turn (c. 312–391 AD)

The conversion of the Empire to Christianity changed everything, and the cult was slow to
understand it. For a generation the priests of Mormo treated the new faith as merely another
patriarchal structure to be subverted, exactly as they had subverted Rome's worship of the old
gods. They were wrong twice over. First, Christianity did not behave like the old cults — it did
not tolerate rivals, and its bishops hunted heresy and witchcraft with an institutional ferocity the
pagan priesthoods had never mustered. Second, and unknown to the cult, the closing of the
pagan temples was draining the worship-pools of the very Nephilim the cult had hidden inside.
The faiths of Cybele, of Magna Mater, of Isis, of Tawaret — the cult's traditional hosts —
were *dying*, and with them died the cover Mormo's priests had relied on for centuries.

The destruction of the Serapeum at Alexandria in 391, and the closing of the temples across the
Empire, marks the end of the old way of hiding. After this, a Mormo sect could no longer assume
that a pagan fertility temple would be standing to hide inside.

### The Two Mothers Go to War in the Dark (c. 380–431 AD)

It is in these decades, the cult's archivists believe, that Isis changed masks. Her Nile temples
were falling; the cult of the Theotokos was rising; and somewhere in that transition the old
Egyptian mother slipped into the blue mantle of the new one. The cult felt the consequences
before it understood them. Across Ægyptus and the eastern provinces, sects that had survived
for centuries were rooted out and burned by Christian devotees whose holy power was real —
Isis arming her hunters from the harvest of their own prayers — and whose hatred of Mormo was
strangely, *anciently* specific, as if the new faith had inherited a
grudge older than itself. The proclamation at the Council of Ephesus in 431 that Mary was rightly
called *Theotokos*, Mother of God, did not create this hunger; it merely gave it the imperial
sanction it needed to grow without limit. The cult marks Ephesus as the year the war it had always
fought against Isis followed it into the new world.

### The Fall of the West and the Fate of Herjan (c. 406–476 AD)

In the north, the great Germanian branch of the cult had bound itself, generations before, to a corrupted warlord called **Herjan** and the warriors of his Horde. When the western frontier collapsed and the peoples beyond the Rhine poured into the dying Empire, the cult expected Herjan's hour had come. It had not. Herjan's Horde shattered against the same migrations it had hoped to ride — broken in part by rival warbands, in part by the plagues Mormo herself had sown, and in part (the cult whispers) by a Concord operation that has never been confirmed. Herjan himself is gone from the world; whether slain, fled into the deep forest, or sunk into a spore-sleep beneath his old hall, no sect agrees. What survived him was not an empire but a *memory* and a method — and the warband-shaped sects of the north now revere Herjan the way earlier cultists revered older dead gods, conflating him with Mormo's male aspect, the Black Goat.

The deposition of the last western emperor in 476 passed almost unnoticed by the cult. They had stopped believing in Rome long before Rome stopped believing in itself.

### The Darkening Age (c. 476–500 AD)

In the world your players inhabit, the Cult of Mormo is in a paradoxical position. The collapse of
the old order has fed it richly — there has never been so much plague, famine, displacement, and
dying for Mormo to grow fat upon. Yet the cult is more exposed than it has ever been, hunted from
a direction it never anticipated: the Marian devotion of a triumphant Church that harbors, without
knowing it, the cult's most ancient enemy. The pagan hosts the cult once hid inside are gone or
going. The new host is far larger but far more dangerous. And in the deepest desert and the rural
Bithynian hills, the Brotherhood of the Black Pharaoh stirs toward its own appointed season,
regarding Mormo as a useful harbinger and the Marian war as a lever it might one day pull.

## REGIONAL BRANCHES

### THE CULT IN ITALY AND THE OLD ROMAN HEARTLANDS
The branch the old chronicles call "the Cult in Rome" survives, but the Rome it survives in has changed beyond recognition. The Western Empire is gone. Italy is ruled from Ravenna by **Theodoric the Ostrogoth**, an Arian Christian king who tolerates the Pope and the Senate alike, and the city of Rome itself is a shrunken, holy, half-ruined place dominated by the Church.

For centuries this branch had deeply infiltrated the Cult of Magna Mater, the "Great Mother," founded in 205 B.C. to serve the Anatolian god Cybele. The acceptance of Cybele, an outsider god, was prompted by recommendations of the Sibylline oracle, who anticipated this faith would be a useful ally in Rome's second war against Carthage. The black meteoric stone brought from Phrygian Pessinos became the face of Cybele's statue, and the cult of the Magna Mater spread across the Empire, engaged in the Taurobolium, where the blood of a sacrificed bull was released to pour through a grate and onto a priestess standing beneath, symbolically feeding the earth in the hope of securing a bountiful harvest. But among the  embership grew a small cabal who preserved the lore of Mormo and practiced more profane  ites in secret, conducting prayers in hidden passages below the temple, where they made periodic sacrifices to Mormo's Foetid Young.

**That temple is now closed, its altars cold, its cult outlawed and gone.** This is the central fact of the Italian branch in 500 AD: its ancient cover is gone. The Galli are dispersed, the Taurobolium is forbidden, and a cabal that once hid inside a great public cult of the Great Mother must now hide inside nothing at all — in private households, in the catacombs that honeycomb the Roman suburbs, in the half-pagan countryside the bishops have not yet reached. Some of the cult's surviving lore-keepers have done the obvious thing and crept inside the *new* Mother's cult, taking the blue veil of Marian devotion as their fathers took the robes of Cybele. A handful have discovered, too late, that this host is infested by Isis, and have been quietly destroyed by Marian hunters who never knew what they had killed.

This branch remains more involved in esoteric and mystical matters than active clashes against rivals. They seek to remain hidden and unseen, and to find ways to encourage and foster other seditious cults dedicated to Mormo's allies among the Outer Ones. They have at times been responsible for unleashing localized afflictions and contagions, seeking to undermine the peoples' faith — once in the Roman gods, now in the bishops and their promises of protection. Adherents in high positions among the old senatorial families have sought to leverage their power to influence Italy's great houses, though they now compete not with the Vestal Virgins but with the Church, with the Ostrogothic court at Ravenna, and — in the city itself — with the House of Lamia, whose patrician vampires regard a Mormo cell in *their* city as vermin to be exterminated.

Pockets of this branch are spread throughout the old western provinces, generally operating in the shadow of whatever local devotion survives. They were always strongest in Gallia, having attached themselves to a number of Gaulic traditions; in 500 AD, with Gaul ruled by the newly-Catholic Franks under Clovis and the remnant Burgundians and Visigoths, these Gaulic cells are pressed between a militant new orthodoxy and the Concord chapter at Tolosa.

Roman epithets for Mormo include *caprea atra nemorum* (black dismal/unlucky she-goat of the  woods) and *caprea furva nemorum* (obscure/nighted/shadowy/hidden she-goat of the woods). Other genitives associated with her include *Vespicum*, *Virgultorum*, *Frutectorum* (of the thickets), to create epithets such as Mormo *Vespicum*, etc. These names are sometimes employed to describe specific manifestations of Mormo or her Foetid Young.

### THE CULT IN GERMANIA
The Cult of Mormo has deep roots in the forests and hills of Germania, and the collapse of the western frontier has freed it from the last restraint Rome ever placed upon it. The legions that once made Rome's soldiers fear to tread in these regions are gone entirely; there is no Empire left to assail. Here the cult is at greater liberty than ever to openly conduct rites and unleash Mormo's monstrous children, hidden now not from Rome but from the migrating peoples — Franks, Saxons, Thuringians, Alemanni — carving the old frontier into kingdoms.

The cult has long competed for the religious devotion of more traditional Germanic faithful, and they are actively opposed by priests of Frigg and the triple goddess, and increasingly by itinerant Christian missionaries pressing in from the Frankish south. The Cult of Mormo in Germania is an active and festering cancerous growth. For generations it bound itself to the corrupted chieftain **Herjan** and the warriors of his Horde — but Herjan is gone now, broken in the great migrations (see *History of the Cult* above), and the Germanian branch has fractured into rival warband-sects, each led by the most ruthless priest who can prove themselves. The strongest of these revere Herjan's memory as a dead god, conflating him with the Black Goat, and squabble over which is his true heir.

It is difficult to trace the origins of Mormo in this region, so long have they had grip here, though the worship of the god is connected to several Germanic faiths. They have aggressively co-opted and infiltrated the worship of the goddess Perchta or Berchta, a mountain god of the Alps. Her name is thought to mean either "the bright one" or "the hidden one," and worship of Perchta has often involved the wearing of fearsome goat-like masks. Thought to be a guardian of beasts and a mother of wild things, the worship of Perchta served as a natural cover for those praising Mormo.

The Cult of Mormo in Germania is only loosely organized, with leadership falling to the most ruthless priests who have proven their capability in opening the way for the Foetid Young. In some areas they need not operate in secret, having overtaken entire villages whose people worship Mormo, though sometimes referring to her by the names of another local god. They band together when the need arises, heeding the strongest of their number and eager to prove their devotion. When the Cult of Mormo in Germania commits to conflict, they are joined by countless horrors. Packs of Teufelshunde howl and stalk alongside them, and they have weaponized fungal spores in glass globes that can transform men into servitors.

The most zealous of Germanic faithful view their human bodies as inferior and inadequate. Powerful priests know rites that bring the blessing of transfiguration on those they deem worthy. This practice is not widely done, as it brings with it certain risks and difficulties, not least being the fact that the transfigured are shunned by other tribes, and might be attacked on sight as abominations. Priests who are changed sprout tentacles from their faces, grow additional limbs, or see their hands changed into clawed appendages. The exact nature of these changes is unpredictable and varied. Other devoted adherents might become Reborn as Goats of the Mother, taking on the appearance of goat-like satyrs, at which point speaking human tongues becomes difficult.

Some higher priests of the cult have chosen to remain unchanged so they can continue to serve as emissaries abroad, visiting other villages and tribes and seeking to recruit initiates. They often betray small but concealable signs of the favor of the god, such as sprouts of wire-like hair, scaly patches of skin, or minor seeping wounds that cause no pain but refuse to heal and close. Other priests are able to communicate with Mi-Go and may be able to coordinate their arrival at certain times and places.

### THE CULT IN ÆGYPTUS
The faith of Mormo has had a tumultuous time in the lands of Ægyptus, having secured a considerable following and a tenacious hold, while also at times being hunted nearly to extinction. **This is the front line of the War of the Two Mothers.** Ægyptus is an Eastern Roman province now, but Alexandria seethes with religious violence, and the deep south and the Faiyum are contested ground where the Concord's Old Seat, the open temples of the Brotherhood of the Black Pharaoh, and the cult of Mormo all maneuver in the dark beneath the Christian surface.

This region may be one of the first areas where the Cult of Mormo became pervasive in the earliest days of agriculture, persisting among the farming communities of the earliest major dynasties. Remnants of places once thought to be sacred to Mormo can be found along the Nile. Secret adherents of Mormo arose and were fostered in the faiths of several prominent gods — the Cult of Tawaret, "She Who is Great," the hippopotamus god of fertility and childbirth; the faithful of Nephthys, "Lady of the Enclosure"; and others among Hathor and Tefnut. **But those host-faiths are now ash.** The Egyptian temples are closed or thrown down, and the cult that once hid among them is more exposed in Ægyptus than anywhere on earth.

For Ægyptus is Isis's own country, and it is here that her Marian campaign burns hottest. The active cults of Isis once dedicated to the obliteration of the Cult of Mormo have not vanished with the old temples — they have *converted*, or appear to have, taking up the devotion of the Mother of God and carrying their ancient grudge into the new faith intact. The Marian hunters of Ægyptus are the most effective scourge of Mormo's sects in the world, and the cult's survivors here have been driven entirely underground, into the subterranean holdings and the deep desert, practicing in far greater secrecy than is the case anywhere else. For these reasons, manifestations of the Foetid Young or other children of Mormo are rarely seen here, though such creatures lurk in the halls of certain subterranean holdings.

The Ægyptus branch has long had to reckon with a powerful corrupted Atlantean named **Menes** who has exerted power in the region for thousands of years. Though Menes serves Nyarlathotep — ostensibly an ally of Mormo — he is unpredictable and dangerous, prone to lashing out at those he views as interfering in his dominion. The cult's priests act cautiously here, seeking to support Menes indirectly and, where they can, to direct the attention of the Brotherhood of the Black Pharaoh toward Isis's Marian hunters. A very few of the boldest Ægyptian priests cherish the cult's most dangerous dream: to make Jehovah *notice* the Egyptian thief feeding inside his Church, and thereby loose a war of two Nephil mothers that would burn the hunters from the cult's back.

### THE CULT IN BRITANNIA
Though the cult is not especially strong or prevalent in Britannia, it has a foothold here, surviving among the wreckage of the old Cybele-worship that clustered at Verulamium and Corbridge near Hadrian's Wall in the days of Roman occupation. **That occupation is long over.** The legions withdrew generations ago; Britannia in 500 AD is a patchwork of sub-Roman warlords and rising Saxon kingdoms, living through the Arthurian moment, and the cult must now make its way among Britons clinging to a fading Romanitas and Saxons bringing their own gods. Its faithful are opposed by a number of traditional religions, including some of Britannia's druids, and now by the spreading British Church.
However, they have also found inroads and entered into pacts with the followers of **Drest**, a corrupted descendant of Atlantis who controls the majority of Britannia's druidic circles. These two groups have worked together to gain information on and undermine — in earlier centuries the occupying Romans, and now the warlords and the bishops alike. Early in the old occupation came a significant clash between Drest's minions supported by the Cult of Mormo against the invading Roman Army, one that resulted in significant losses on both sides. That ancient engagement set the Britannian cult back for generations, but with Rome gone and the island fragmented, the cult is on the rise again amid the general collapse of order.

The cult's priests have been slowly nurturing a number of beasts sacred to Mormo against the eventuality of unleashing them upon their adversaries. The cult of Mormo in Britannia is particularly adept at mystical rites that foster the spread of disease. With no Empire left to drive out, they have turned these rites toward the warlords and the Saxon incomers, and toward any who would bring the new Marian devotion into the island — for here, too, the woman in blue has begun to appear in the dreams of the cult's priests, and they have learned to fear her.

### THE CULT IN THE EAST
Long before the Cult of Magna Mater was founded in Rome, bringing with it the seeds of the cult westward, the faith had already been active in the east. It had become entrenched in the Anatolian Hills and deeply tied to the faith of the Mater Kubileya, "the Mother of Mountains," the god Cybele. Here the worship of Mormo became associated with the inner circle of that god's adherents, who associated the goddess with the spilling of blood, the sounding of drums below the earth to represent her heartbeat, and the roaring of lions. Her faithful could be found in other places, having infiltrated those who once venerated Hubaba, the cults of Diana of Ephesus, and among the self-castrated Atargatis priests of Syria.

In 500 AD the East is the heartland of a triumphant and bitterly divided Christianity — Chalcedonian against Miaphysite, Constantinople under the old and careful Anastasius, the cities of Anatolia and Syria torn by theological riot. **Ephesus, once the seat of Diana, is now the city where Mary was proclaimed Mother of God** — a fact the cult finds darkly fitting, for the same hills that fed Mormo through Cybele now feed Isis through the Theotokos. The eastern branch is squeezed hardest of all: between a Church that hunts heresy with imperial machinery, the largest chapter of the Concord at Constantinople, the Mobedān across the Persian frontier, and the Marian devotion that has made Ephesus its own. What survives of the eastern cult survives in the deep countryside and the mountain villages, far from the riots of the great sees.

## ROLES OF THE FAITHFUL
The membership of the Cult of Mormo is very fluid and organic, with very little structure or hierarchy. Nonetheless certain adherents occupy distinct roles and undertake tasks related to those roles. Those who are aggressive and ambitious can seize power and influence over their peers, particularly if they show knowledge of the cult's higher rituals and can display tangible blessings of the god.

### PRIESTS
Any adherents within the cult who are capable of wielding mystical power are considered priests, a position of greatest inclusion and respect. Most of the cult's priests have in their past served within the faiths of other more traditional and accepted religions, and they frequently retain these roles and garb to serve as a cover for their true activities. In 500 AD, with the old temples closed, this cover has shifted: where a priest of Mormo once posed as a galla of Cybele or a priestess of Isis, today she is as likely to pose as a Christian widow, a consecrated virgin, a deaconess, or a lay devotee of the Mother of God. This new disguise is more available than ever — and more perilous than ever, for it places the priest inside the very devotion that Isis has made her hunting-ground.

Even if banished from their previous religions, such trappings remain, and most of these priests can feign having retained their standing to ordinary folk, or if traveling away from where they were known.

When engaged in active rites to Mormo they will put aside these remnants and don the garments of their true faith. These vary in color and style by region, but generally include cowled robes worn in multiple layers, enabling the total concealment of the flesh. Full priests initiated into Mormo's secrets may wear circlets or crowns of thorned vines, dyed carmine. Priests may also dye parts of their face black and the rest white, with black bands across the eyes and lips being common. Full Mormo raiment may also include small bones attached by leather thongs to the robes, carved with either decorative inscriptions or sacred sigils. As part of their initiation most priests of Mormo bear a brand with the mark of Mormo somewhere on their flesh, usually in a place that can be easily concealed.

Certain branches of the cult are more organized in regard to their priesthoods than others. The branch that once led the cabal within the Magna Mater in Rome was governed by a trio of female priests called the **High Daughters**, traditionally joined by a self- castrated male advisor called the **High Galli**, a role inherited from the Phrygian priests of Cybele; with the closing of the temple this leadership has scattered into the catacombs and the great households. In Germania, there was a small council of leading priests called the **Höhlenrat** which was looked to for direction on larger matters, such as the old alliance with Herjan; with Herjan gone, the Höhlenrat has fractured along with the warbands, and rival councils now claim the name. Even at the height of their organization, such positions were always fluid, subject to being challenged and overthrown.

Priests that have successfully completed the most sacred rite, summoning the Foetid Young of Mormo, have been marked in the process. The more a priest is in communion with Mormo, the more likely they are to bear physical transformations. The variety of such marks varies widely, but can include the growth of fur, horns, the transformation of hands into claws, eyes becoming goat-like, persistent unhealed open wounds, scaled patches of skin, the growth of additional limbs, feet becoming hooves, the sprouting of tentacles upon the face or back, skin covered in damp moss or with a bark-like texture, or the appearance of pustulating sores. Some have chosen to undertake difficult and painful procedures to minimize physical changes to retain their humanity, a decision most common among those serving as emissaries or recruiters. Most, however, consider such marks to be a blessing and a sign of favor, so bear them proudly.

### ADHERENTS (THE BROOD)
The ordinary faithful of Mormo are those who have been recruited or enticed to worship and serve the god, but who do not know her deeper rites or have any mystical capabilities. They attend rites and perform whatever task is required of them by the priests. Some may be chosen to serve as armed protectors of the cult, arming themselves to the best of their capabilities.
The Cult of Mormo is an underground faith. Even in areas where it is strongest its members might be subject to violence from outsiders, so most live double lives and conceal their allegiance. This also means even the lowest adherents are generally fanatical in their devotion and willing to do anything asked of them by their priests. Adherents might sometimes be asked to sacrifice their own lives or those of their family members for the faith, though most sacrifices are inflicted on outsiders.

In addition to armed warriors of the cult, another position of esteem is to be chosen to serve the priests in tending the cult's beasts. This role requires chosen adherents to bring meat and blood to feed the creatures, a perilous task that sometimes results in maiming or death. The beast-tenders are hardened zealots, some of them quite skilled in the handling of wild animals and the use of tools intended for that purpose.

### METRAGYRTAI
A special class of Mormo cultists are the Metragyrtai, or "beggars for the Mother," a role most commonly found within the old Roman provinces and their successor kingdoms. These are wandering beggars who travel from place to place performing certain entertainments (dancing, playing flutes or other instruments, songs, hymns, conducting plays) and shaking down attendees for coins. Under this guise they are given considerable liberty to travel without greater scrutiny and can serve as couriers and messengers between isolated pockets of the cult, as well as gathering information. In an age of broken roads and shattered authority, the Metragyrtai have become more valuable than ever to a cult whose sects are now scattered and out of contact. They are often employed to test the waters for recruitment in new towns and villages, as well as investigating the tolerance of local priests — pagan and Christian alike. Though they present an innocuous appearance, many Metragyrtai are skilled fighters and quite capable of defending themselves or priests of the cult when required. Some serve as dedicated assassins.

### THE REBORN
Though Mormo's priests are thought to receive the greatest transfiguring gifts of the god, others are sometimes "blessed" and changed to better serve the cult. The Reborn are sometimes referred to as the Goats of the Mother, and they take on a caprine appearance, growing fur and horns, with legs transformed to resemble satyrs. It is possible legends and myths of those creatures have their origins in Reborn who were active in ancient Greece, where the cult was strong.

Becoming Reborn requires supplicants to be willing to entirely surrender themselves and abandon their humanity. They believe their new state represents a purer union with Mormo and helps them embody the rampant fertility of the wild places sacred to her. In this unfettered state they are left in a state of controlled madness, subject to intense frenzies of violence. They delight in tearing apart their adversaries and bathing in their blood. The Reborn are wild and unpredictable, but they recognize Mormo's priests and can be cowed and directed by them.

## COMPOSITION OF A CULT OF MORMO SECT
The various cults of Mormo are disparate and diverse, no two exactly the same. However, the following can be used as a starting point to represent a single sect of the cult in an area, including descriptions of its modes of operation and NPC templates to be used as adversaries. Sects such as this can exist within any community within the areas where the Cult of Mormo is active, and they can be found throughout the old Roman lands and Germania in particular.

In any sect of Mormo, one of the highest priorities is preserving the priests and most devoted faithful, who are generally few in number, by employing dupes and fodder. The leaders of the cult are loath to put themselves directly at risk, and when entering into potentially dangerous conflicts they will find ways to ensure others take the fall. When fomenting dissent in a given town or village, they will try to rile up the regular people such that when violence inevitably ensues, the cult's own following are watching from the fringes but not attacked directly. These leaders use their influence, powers, and wealth to secure armed protectors, and are quite willing to sacrifice these peons when the need arises to evade their foes. The more entrenched and successful sects have access to powerful and formidable creatures they can release against greater threats.

### HIERARCHY
A typical sect of Mormo will include the following echelons, though it is worth remembering their faith lacks a strict hierarchy and any position of leadership must be seized and defended:
##### HIGH MATRON(S) OR PATRON(S) (1-3)
SECT LEADERS
##### PRIESTS (2-4 MATRONS/PATRONS)
THOSE WHO ASSIST THE LEADERS IN SACRED RITES
##### SPEAKER OF THE MOTHER (1-2)
PRIEST OR ZEALOT SERVING AS RECRUITER AND EMISSARY
##### SHEPHERD OF THE GET (1-2)
ASSIGNED TO OVERSEE BEASTS
##### PROTECTORS (2-6 PER PRIEST)
DEDICATED BODYGUARDS OF THE PRIESTS
##### BEASTS (2-12)
TAMED BUT DANGEROUS CREATURES EMPLOYED AS GUARDIANS, POTENTIALLY INCLUDING HOUNDS, WOLVES, BEARS, BIRDS OF PREY, OR MORE EXOTIC ANIMALS DEPENDING ON REGION.
##### THE BROOD (20-40)
INITIATED ADHERENTS OF THE CULT ENTRUSTED WITH MORMO'S NAME
##### THE RABBLE (10X THE BROOD)
CIVILIANS MANIPULATED AND AGITATED TO A FERVOR WHO CAN BE DIRECTED
TO COMMIT VIOLENCE AGAINST TARGETS CHOSEN BY THE SPEAKERS

### OPTIONAL ASSETS
Most sects of the cult rely on ordinary people to fulfill most of their needs, though sect leaders may possess the occult lore and secrets to call on Mormo's supernatural aid when pressed. Rites to summon creatures sacred to Mormo are among the most treasured of lore, though such entities are usually brought forth only when a specific need arises. The greater sects may summon Foetid Young to witness their high rituals and can call on them when facing greater adversaries. Beings such as Teufelshunde can also be summoned and unleashed, and priests may also have enlisted servitors and overlords of Mormo. If engaged in active open conflicts, a sect might also be joined by a number of Die Draugar. In areas where the Cult of Mormo is strongest and can openly engage in worship, such as among the wilder regions of Germania, a number of Reborn will fight alongside other protectors to safeguard Mormo's priests.

## SAMPLE SECTS
The following are examples of two divergent sects of the Cult of Mormo, each functioning in a distinct environment and under different constraints. These can be relocated and inserted into campaigns in other locations by GMs as the need arises.
### PACT OF DIVA TRIFORMUS
This is a sizable secret cult that worships Mormo from within the old Roman trade hub of **Ostia**, now a shrinking port in Theodoric's Ostrogothic Italy. This cult is primarily composed of provincial Romans, though it does include members from other backgrounds and it is eager to expand its reach. Its services to Mormo are disguised under the worship of the Diva Triformus, the triple deity, which includes Luna (sometimes used to refer to the moon aspects of Diana or Juno), Proserpina (Persephone to the Greeks), and Hecate. With open pagan worship now outlawed under a Christian order, the sect maintains a *second* false face as a confraternity of lay Christians devoted to the Mother of God — a disguise that has begun, alarmingly, to draw the notice of genuine Marian devotees. Most of its members lead a double life as ordinary townsfolk, though some live on the fringes as beggars, outcasts, or criminals. The sect includes some who have been enslaved by the wealthier families, who must sneak off to attend services when their duties allow. The sect's influence is spread across a wide area as a loose net, but this also means individual members can be particularly vulnerable if exposed.

New initiates and junior members are not steeped in the secrets of Mormo until they prove their devotion, at which point they come to understand that these gods and others besides are but faces of a greater god so vast she defies easy classification. This sect seeks to bring into its fold any who have been shunned, dishonored, or cast out of more traditional faiths — and, increasingly, those disillusioned with the new Church — offering them a new home and purpose. This includes sheltering criminals being hunted by the authorities, who may be enlisted and armed as protectors.

The lunar cycles are especially significant to this sect and the inner circle conducts special rites at various phases of the moon, reserving the full moon to attempt to commune with Mormo. It is at such times that they may seek to summon Mormo's offspring to afflict and curse their enemies, including those identified by supplicants who come to them seeking revenge on those who have harmed them. The priests rely on offerings made by such supplicants to fund their more covert activities, including bribing local officials to look the other way when needed. This group is also capable of raising a sizable mob of dissatisfied townsfolk within the community on a few days' notice, either as a cover for other activities or to harass highly placed citizens they view as a threat.

This sect keeps a relatively low profile and refrains from more openly malevolent rites due to their proximity to the Church and the Ostrogothic authorities. Its leaders are in contact with other sects, however, including the remnant cabal in Rome, and could be convinced to become more active and join in coordinated efforts on short notice.

#### HEADQUARTERS
A sizable vineyard just outside the town serves as the central gathering place for members of the cult, as part of the Crito estate, home to one of the sect's high matrons. Smaller portions of the cult occasionally meet at various ruined temples and the markets within the town, particularly when seeking new recruits. They have access to several secure locations for conducting their more elaborate rites, including a secluded grove adjacent to the vineyard.

#### SECT COMPOSITION
Most of the priests of this sect are women, though there are men in a few important positions.

**Leadership:** High Matron Statilia Crito, High Matron Bruccia Decimidia, Speaker Mettius Armenius Iulian, Shepherd Laberia Alecta.

**Priests:** The high matrons are assisted by up to a dozen junior priests, currently made up of two men and ten women. Half are natives to the town while the rest came from elsewhere. Most were recruited and trained by either one of the high matrons or the speaker, who sometimes travels abroad on sect business.

**Protectors:** There are currently twenty zealous and armed protectors in the sect, and every priest is accompanied by one or two as bodyguards wherever they go. Among the sect's protectors is a large Germanic warrior named **Berard**, a veteran of the warbands that once rode with Herjan's Horde, who drifted south into Italy after the Horde's collapse.

**Adherents (the Brood):** This sect has at least two hundred dedicated members in and around the town, including those willing to perform whatever services are asked of them and to put their lives on the line if need be. They make regular offerings to the priests and eagerly attend rites. They come from all walks of life, though the majority are among the lower classes, with only a couple of dozen from the wealthy elite.

**Metragyrtai:** This sect includes several dozen "beggars for the Mother" who serve as eyes and ears around the town and beyond. Small groups of Metragyrtai may be sent to specific towns or villages to test the waters for recruitment, and they can also be gathered to help protect the priests if the sect is threatened. They are led by a man named **Lars Verenius** (the "Laughing Beggar") who is skilled with a blade, and an able assassin.

**Beasts:** This sect keeps several vicious but otherwise ordinary war hounds at the vineyard and has also tamed several boars. During their highest rites the matrons may summon Mormo's Foetid Young to witness their devotion and accept offerings, and they know how to attract Teufelshunde if the need arises. The sect's leaders have rarely asked for such protectors, or entreated Mormo to deliver overlords or servitors to them, from a desire to maintain secrecy.

**Complication (GM):** Unknown to its leaders, one of the sect's lay-Christian recruits is a devout Marian worshipper whose prayers Isis has begun, faintly, to *answer* — skimming their potential and lending a trickle back — and whose dreams have begun to fill with a woman in blue who points at the Crito vineyard. The sect has acquired a hunter in its own ranks and does not yet know it.

### OATHBOUND OF THE NIGHT MOTHER
This is a more openly ruthless and active sect of the Cult of Mormo, found in northern Germania where this faith is strongest, in territories once claimed by Herjan and now contested by his would-be heirs. In many respects they function less like a secretive cult and more akin to a tribal warband on campaign.

Ancestors of this sect's members revered the fertility god Nerthus, who has since been conflated by members of the sect with Mormo. Entire families and even villages in this region have given themselves over to the worship of Mormo, whom they view with equal measures of terror and love. Unlike in many sects, Mormo is a name known to most and is spoken of openly.

The peoples of this sect have been making offerings to Mormo for centuries. They drew strength and standing for generations from their alliance with Herjan — but with Herjan broken and gone, the Oathbound have lost their patron and their certainty. They now ride with the warband of a rising chieftain who claims to be Herjan's heir, and the sect's priests have thrown their lot in with him, loaning and borrowing warriors to season the young and protect their holdings in the unsettled north.

#### SECT COMPOSITION
This sect includes almost entirely Germanian villagers and warriors, though there are some in this region with Celtic or other origins. Members of this sect can be of any age and gender, given the family connection. Only those with an aptitude for combat are selected to become protectors, and this sect conducts elaborate trials to select those who would be priests, given that this is a highly prestigious role. Women outnumber men as ranking priests, but only slightly. This sect also includes a number of the Reborn, considered a blessing and sign of Mormo's favor, and maintains a number of beasts, seeking to be ready for battle at all times.

**Leadership:** High Matron Cunigund, High Patron Gerbold, High Matron Saxa, Speaker Æbbe, Speaker Sigiward, Shepherd Odilia, Shepherd Willidrud

**Priests:** There are currently thirty priests in this sect, some of them elders of their respective villages. Initiation as a priest takes years of close inculcation and learning the mysteries of Mormo and is reserved for those thought marked by the god. Investment rites include a baptism where the supplicant is doused in a caustic bath of bile, amniotic fluids, and offal torn from sacrificial offerings of cattle or captive enemies. Most priests of this sect bear physical changes or signs, some of them drastic.

**Protectors:** This sect has over eighty protectors, most of them veteran warriors of their tribes who have been chosen by the priests. A grizzled and heavily scarred older champion named **Cyneric the Fevered** is the most feared. He is said to bear a persistent open wound on his chest that festers with plague-pus, which he uses to coat the blade of his axe.

**Reborn:** The Reborn are somewhat common among this sect, viewed as a specially blessed state, and there are up to two dozen of them present serving its elder priests as additional protectors.

**Adherents (the Brood):** The exact number of this sect is unknown, but might be as many as eight hundred or more dedicated believers. In its region most villages must at least feign reverence or face dire consequences, and so its overall congregation is several times larger. The priests in this region wield tremendous influence over the local tribes and can easily whip them into a frenzy.

**Beasts:** The shepherds of this sect have access to all the great beasts Mormo spawns, including rabid wild wolves, bears, and hounds. When gathered together they can call upon several of Mormo's Foetid Young, a pack of Teufelshunde, as well as multiple overlords and possibly dozens of servitors. At any given time there are several dozen of Die Draugar standing guard over their sacred places and these can also be mustered by the senior priests to join their battles.

**Allies:** Upward of fifty warriors of the chieftain who claims Herjan's mantle can be found alongside this sect when it prepares for battle, most of them untested, but including a handful of veterans and possibly an Einherjar and Walkyr if engaged in a vital struggle.

>*"Let the Night Mother embrace you. Your flesh is but clay for her to shape into something more  pleasing to her eyes."*
>
>-High Matron Cunigund of the Oathbound

## ADVENTURE HOOKS (500 AD)

- **The Thief in the Blue Mantle.** A Mormo cell in Alexandria has worked out the secret of the Marian hunters — that the Mother of God who scourges them is Isis in a stolen mask. They are trying to send proof to the one power who would care: not the Concord, but *Jehovah himself*, hoping to trigger a war of two Nephil mothers inside the Church. The Brotherhood of the Black Pharaoh wants this to happen. The Concord's Inner Seal wants it stopped at any cost. The player characters are caught between them with the letter in hand.
- **The Sincere Recruit.** A Mormo sect disguised as a Marian confraternity has unknowingly recruited a devout worshipper whose prayers Isis has begun to *answer* — harvesting their devotion and arming them in return. The sect wants the players to quietly remove a "troublesome new member." The players slowly realize they have been hired to murder a saint-in-the-making — and that the woman in blue is watching.
- **Herjan's Heir.** Two Germanian warband-sects, each claiming to carry the true succession of the broken chieftain Herjan, are sliding toward open war, and both are summoning Mormo's Foetid Young to settle it. Whatever crawls out of the northern forest when they do will not care which warband wins.
- **The Closed Temple.** Beneath a half-ruined temple of the Magna Mater outside Rome, the old Italian cabal still keeps a spawning-ground — and a Marian hunting-band has just arrived in the district, drawn by dreams. The House of Lamia, who consider the city theirs, would also like the cell gone. Three predators, one prey, and the players in the middle.
