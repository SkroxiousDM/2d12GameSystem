## **Chapter 4: Talents**

Talents are the defining features and abilities that set a hero apart from the common run of humanity. Many Talents have Requirements—certain Skill Ranks, Attribute scores, or other Talents—that must be met before they can be learned. Losing the ability to meet a Requirement means losing the Talent.

Talents provide both **active** and **passive** benefits. Passive benefits persistently modify Attributes, Skills, or other game elements. Many Talents also grant access to **Actions**, **Reactions**, and **Desperate Actions**.

A character earns Talents during character creation through their Role, and potentially through their Culture and Background. After character creation, a character may spend 20 XP to purchase a new Talent, provided all Requirements are met.

Requirements typically include:

* An Attribute above or below a certain value, such as Agility 4 or fewer than 2 ranks in Reason  
* A number of ranks in a Skill, such as Rank 2 Fighting  
* A specialization, such as Fighting (Melee Weapons)  
* The absence of ranks in a specific Skill or specialization  
* Some Talents may only be taken at character creation  
* Some Talents prevent or require taking other Talents

## **Fighting Talents**

## **Hound**

* **Benefit:** Choose one character. You gain Advantage on all Athletics Checks made to reach them, so long as they are no farther than Far range. This lasts until you take a Rest or designate a new target.  
* **Desperate Action:** Gain \+1SL on one attack against your designated target.

---

## **Tough**

*Prerequisite: Constitution 4*

* **Benefit:** \+1 Vitality.  
* **Benefit:** \+2 Armor while unarmored.  
* **Desperate Action:** When a single attack would deal more than 1 Critical Wound, reduce it to 1\.

---

## **Berserker**

*Prerequisite:  Battle Rage 2,  Constitution 6*

* **Benefit:** When you would receive a Wound that kills you, make a Challenging Constitution \+ Resilience Check. On a success, you do not die and instead heal 1 Wound. Each subsequent use of this benefit increases the Difficulty by 1 level. A Rest resets the Difficulty.

---

## **Battle Rage**

You can work yourself into a furious killing state.

* **Free Action:** make a Challenging Willpower Check to enter a rage. While raging, your Strength increases by \+1 and you are immune to the Frightened condition. On your turn, you must move toward the nearest visible enemy and engage them in melee. The rage ends when all visible enemies are defeated or when you suffer the Stunned or Unconscious condition.

This Talent may be taken multiple times. Each time after the first, choose one of the following:

* **Controlled Fury:** You may make a Difficult Resilience Check as an Action to end your rage voluntarily.  
* **Fearless Rage:** Whenever you would become Frightened, you may immediately enter a rage instead.  
* **Relentless Momentum:** Once per turn, when you kill an enemy while raging, you may immediately Move or take one additional Action.

---

## **Dual Wielder**

* **Benefit:** Your base Evasion becomes 10 \+ your Fighting Skill Rank while wielding two swords, daggers, maces, axes, or fighting knives.  
* **Benefit:** When you would inflict a Critical Wound while dual wielding, inflict one additional Critical Wound.

---

## **Opportunist**

*Prerequisites: Dual Wielder, Dexterity 4, Rank 5 Fighting*

* **Reaction:** When you successfully Evade while dual wielding swords, daggers, or fighting knives, you may immediately attack your attacker with a Fighting Skill Check.

---

## **Bruiser**

*Prerequisites: Dual Wielder, Strength 4, Rank 5 Fighting*

* **Reaction:** When your attack while dual wielding swords, axes, or maces is mitigated by armor, you may make an additional Fighting Skill Check against the same target.

---

## **Dervish**

*Prerequisites: Opportunist or Bruiser, Strength 5 or Dexterity 5*

* **Desperate Action:** While dual wielding, make a single Fighting Skill Check against a number of adjacent characters up to your Dexterity or Strength score.

---

## **Ironclad**

* **Benefit:** Armor requires 1 fewer Stamina to wear. You may forgo this benefit to have all armor grant \+1 Armor instead.  
* **Benefit:** Armor's Dexterity limit is increased by 1\. You may forgo this benefit to have all armor grant \+1 Armor instead.

---

## **Retaliation**

*Prerequisites: Ironclad, Rank 5 Fighting*

* **Reaction:** When an attack from an adjacent source is mitigated by your armor, you may immediately make a Fighting Skill Check against the attacker.

## **Dangerous**

*Prerequisite: Rank 1 Fighting*

* **Desperate Action:** Make a Fighting or Archery Skill Check as though you had Rank 5\. If your Fighting or Archery is already Rank 5, this attack affects two characters; make a single Skill Check.
