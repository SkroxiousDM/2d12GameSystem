## Fumble Table

When you Fumble, roll 1d12 and consult the table below.

| Roll | Result | Effect |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| 1 | **Off Balance** — You flail inelegantly and lose precious moments reasserting a defensible stance. | You drop to the bottom of the Initiative order at the start of the next round. |
| 2 | **Clumsy Lurch** — You flounder badly, knocked precariously off balance. | You have Disadvantage on all tests until the end of your next turn. |
| 3 | **Dropped Weapon** — A poorly controlled swing or shot causes you to lose your grip. | You drop the weapon used to attack. Any character may pick it up as a free action. |
| 4 | **Stumble** — An unwieldy swing or unexpected recoil sends you lurching. | You fall Prone. |
| 5 | **Twisted Ankle** — Overextending in melee or absorbing sudden recoil twists your ankle badly. | Your Speed is reduced to Slow until the end of the encounter. This does not count as a Critical Wound. |
| 6 | **Damaged Weapon** — You mishandle your weapon, striking it against a hard surface or fouling its mechanism. | Your weapon gains the Unstable trait until repaired with a successful Engineering test (Difficulty 2). |
| 7 | **Sapped Resolve** — The embarrassment and shock of your blunder rattles your confidence at a critical moment. | You suffer 2 points of mental stress, ignoring Armour. |
| 8 | **Momentarily Blinded or Deafened** — Your ungainly attack turns against your own senses — a blinding flash, a deafening crack, or a clumsy blow to the face. | You suffer the Blinded or Deafened condition, as the GM determines most appropriate for your weapon. |
| 9 | **Damaged Armour** — Your graceless attack causes minor damage to your own protective gear. | Your armour in a random hit location is reduced by 1 until repaired. If you wear no armour in that location, you suffer 1 wound instead. |
| 10 | **Self-Inflicted Blow** — In the fumbling confusion of battle, you manage to strike yourself with a glancing blow. | You suffer the Stunned or Bleeding condition, as the GM determines most appropriate for your weapon. |
| 11 | **Exposed Flank** — Your wild attack leaves you completely open. Your opponent instantly recognises the opportunity and exploits it without mercy. | Your opponent may immediately make a free attack against you outside of their normal turn. This attack does not trigger reactions. |
| 12 | **Unintended Target** — Your attack strikes someone other than your intended target — a blade cleaves through an enemy and into an ally, or a shot ricochets wildly. | Your attack hits a random ally within weapon range with 0 success levels. Roll a random hit location. If no ally is within range, you hit yourself instead. |
